# THE GAME BIBLE — "THE LAST TIDE OF SAINT-MALLON"

*A swashbuckling hidden-role RPG. One night on the docks of Saint-Mallon. Hard difficulty.*

---

## SETTING

**Location:** The Lantern Quay of Saint-Mallon, a free port wedged between two empires on a rocky stretch of coast. A long curving stone harbour studded with warehouses, taverns, chandlers, and a customs house. Three notable ships are moored along the quay tonight. A scaffold has been erected at the landward end of the quay — a hanging is scheduled at dawn.

**Time period and tone:** Late age-of-sail, vaguely 1720s. Comedic swashbuckling on the surface — boasting privateers, drunken sailors, peacock-feathered duellists — with an undercurrent of genuine cruelty. People will die tonight, some of them gruesomely, and some of those deaths will be funny in the wrong light.

**Social occasion — THE FEAST OF SAINT MALLON:** The town's patron-saint festival, held every year on the eve of the autumn tides. Lanterns hang from every rope. Fiddlers on the quay. Roast hog turning over coals outside the Brass Anchor tavern. The festival has, this year, become entangled with three other occasions that all converged on the same night by accident or design:

1. **The hanging at dawn** of one CALLEN ROOK, condemned mutineer, to be carried out by the Harbourmaster's authority at first light.
2. **The departure of the *Halcyon Lass*** at the turn of the tide (approximately 4 AM) — a merchant ship leaving for the southern colonies that several people very much want, or do not want, to be aboard.
3. **A "private auction"** rumoured to be taking place sometime tonight in one of the warehouses — invitation by token only, the merchandise unspecified.

**The Host (public authority figure):** **HARBOURMASTER ESTEBAN VAULT** — a barrel-chested ex-naval officer with a wooden leg, a velvet coat gone to seed, and absolute legal authority over the quay until dawn. He runs the festival, signed Callen Rook's death warrant, and holds the keys to the customs house. He is universally known and visible. He will give the opening toast at sunset on the steps of the customs house.

**The Central Secret:** **Callen Rook is not who the warrant says he is.** The man in the cell beneath the customs house was sentenced for mutiny aboard the *Sable Pernoux* eight years ago — but the real Callen Rook died at sea years ago. The man condemned to hang at dawn is **TOMÁS ELÍRA**, a deserter from a different ship who took Rook's name in a tavern brawl and has been living under it ever since. Worse: the *Sable Pernoux* mutiny itself was a cover story for something far uglier — the ship's officers murdered the crew to hide what they had taken aboard from a sinking Imperial galleon. That cargo — three sea-chests of relics looted from a sunken monastery, including a thing called **the Drowned Saint's Bell** — was offloaded at Saint-Mallon eight years ago and is *still here*, hidden somewhere beneath the customs house. Several people on the quay tonight know fragments of this. None of them know all of it. Multiple characters' goals will collide with this hidden cargo before sunrise.

**Time frame:** Sunset (approx. 6 PM) to dawn (approx. 6 AM). Twelve hours, twelve possible rounds, with 8–10 expected to be active. The hanging at dawn is the hard endpoint.

---

## LOCATION MAP

Saint-Mallon's Lantern Quay is a curving stone harbour roughly 400 yards long. The land side rises into the lower town; the seaward side drops to the water. Three ships are moored bow-to-stern: the *Halcyon Lass* (departing at 4 AM), the *Brindled Mare* (a scarred privateer schooner whose captain is ashore), and the *Vespertine* (a sleek black-hulled ship of unclear ownership flying no colours).

### PUBLIC SPACES

**1. THE QUAY ITSELF (the Lantern Walk)**
The long stone harbour-wall, lit by hundreds of paper lanterns strung between the masts and the warehouses. Festival crowds drift up and down. Fiddlers, a hog roast, dice games. Anyone here is visible to all others on the quay. The scaffold stands at the landward end — empty rope, four tall posts, a trapdoor — and people give it a wide berth, though children dare each other to touch it.

**2. THE BRASS ANCHOR TAVERN**
The biggest tavern on the quay, doors flung wide for the festival, smoke and song spilling out. Long tables, a fiddler in the corner, a pig turning over the fire in the courtyard. The landlady, MOTHER GASCA, knows every face on the quay and forgets nothing. Public, observed, dangerously social — anything said too loudly here will be in three other ears by the next round.

**3. THE CUSTOMS HOUSE STEPS**
A wide stone landing in front of the customs house, raised four steps above the quay. This is where Harbourmaster Vault will give his sunset toast and where official announcements happen. The customs house itself looms behind it, with arched windows lit from within. A pair of harbour guards in red coats stand at the door. Public, but proximity to the building means anyone lingering here is *noticed*.

**4. THE FISH MARKET ARCADE**
A long covered colonnade behind the quay, half-shuttered for the festival but with several stalls still trading: a knife-grinder, a charm-seller, a woman selling hot cider from a kettle. Smells of salt and old fish even in the cleaned-up evening. People meet here for half-private conversations under the arches.

### RESTRICTED SPACES

**5. THE CUSTOMS HOUSE (interior — main floor)**
Vault's office, the harbour ledgers, the wall of confiscated contraband. Access requires: (a) Vault's permission, (b) one of the two red-coat guards being persuaded/bribed/distracted, or (c) the servant's door at the back which is almost always locked but whose key Mother Gasca has a copy of (long story).

**6. THE CELL BENEATH THE CUSTOMS HOUSE**
A single stone holding cell at the bottom of a short stair, accessed through the customs house. The condemned man is here, in irons, waiting for dawn. Access requires: (a) Vault's keys, (b) a clergyman's right of last confession (the local priest has the privilege but won't exercise it without reason), or (c) descending through the old smugglers' shaft from the seawall — a hidden passage, cold and tight, known to perhaps two people on the quay tonight. **The hidden cargo is in a sealed alcove in the cell-wall behind a loose stone, though Tomás himself does not know this.**

**7. THE *VESPERTINE* (great cabin)**
The black-hulled ship's stern cabin. Locked. Access requires: (a) the captain's invitation, (b) the auction-token (rumoured), or (c) the ship's boy can be bribed to look the other way at the gangplank.

**8. KEDDIE'S WAREHOUSE (the Tobacco Loft)**
A two-storey warehouse halfway along the quay, owned by an absent merchant. Officially shuttered. Tonight the upper loft is being used for the private auction. Access requires: (a) an auction token, (b) climbing in via the seaward window from a small boat or the *Vespertine*'s deck, or (c) the watchman at the side door is fond of cider and has been fond of it for several hours already.

**9. THE OLD CHAPEL OF SAINT MALLON**
Up a flight of slick stone steps from the quay, a small dilapidated chapel where Father IUSTIN, the harbour priest, lives in two cold rooms above the nave. Officially open to all, but most townsfolk are at the festival. Anyone going up here is *visible* on the steps, and once inside is in close quarters with whoever else is there. Houses the parish register, including a record from eight years ago that mentions the *Sable Pernoux*.

### TRANSITIONAL SPACES

**10. THE LANDWARD ALLEYS**
A maze of narrow lanes climbing into the lower town behind the quay. Useful for unobserved movement between the chapel, the fish market, and the back of the customs house. Easy to be followed here. Easy to lose someone too. Knife-fights happen here regularly even on quiet nights.

**11. THE SEAWARD ROCKS (and the smugglers' shaft)**
Below the seawall at the eastern end of the quay, slick black rocks descend to the surf. A rusted iron grate hides the mouth of an old smugglers' tunnel that runs *beneath* the customs house and ends in a hatch concealed in the cell-wall. Knowing about the shaft is itself a secret. Climbing it is dangerous — wet stone, rising tide.

**12. THE GANGPLANKS**
The three ships' gangplanks are policed by their own crews. Boarding the *Halcyon Lass* is easy if you have business; boarding the *Brindled Mare* is easy because half the crew is drunk in the Brass Anchor; boarding the *Vespertine* is hard. Watching who goes aboard which ship is itself a useful occupation.

**13. THE SCAFFOLD END (the Drowning Stones)**
At the very landward end of the quay, just past the scaffold, a low cluster of stones where the harbour wall has crumbled. Locals call them the Drowning Stones because that's where bodies wash up when the tide turns. A quiet place to talk, or to do something quiet to someone.

### Map design notes

- **Hidden passage:** the smugglers' shaft connects the seaward rocks (11) to the cell beneath the customs house (6). Knowing it exists is restricted information.
- **Special-significance location:** the cell (6) and the customs house above it are central to at least five characters' goals.
- **Environmental clues:** a slashed portrait of an old captain on the wall of Vault's office (the *Sable Pernoux*'s captain — Vault knew him); a salt-stained sea-chest in the chapel vestry that doesn't match the parish inventory; scratch marks on the inside of the cell door at chest-height; a faded brand on Mother Gasca's left forearm in the shape of an anchor (visible only when she rolls her sleeve up to draw beer).

## ROLES (15 characters including the player)

### **1. THE PLAYER — JEHANNE "JAY" CORDELIER, the Insurance Investigator**

- **Cover story:** A travelling clerk for the Compagnie d'Assurance Maritime de Lys, here to interview the Harbourmaster about a long-overdue claim filed eight years ago by the owners of the *Sable Pernoux*. Polite, unthreatening, carries a leather portfolio and a small brass writing-set. Wears a sober travelling coat, sensible boots, a neat tricorn. Speaks softly. Easy to underestimate.
- **Secret:** Jay's older brother MARTIN was second mate on the *Sable Pernoux*. The official story says he died in the mutiny. Jay has been digging at the edges of the case for three years — getting hired by the insurance company specifically to come *here*, on this date, was the first real opportunity. She does not believe her brother died in any mutiny. She believes someone in Saint-Mallon knows what really happened to him.
- **Win condition:** **Discover the truth about what happened to the crew of the *Sable Pernoux* eight years ago, and specifically the fate of Martin Cordelier.** This requires gathering at least three independent pieces of evidence about that night and confronting at least one person who was directly involved. (Achievable. Specific. Measurable.)
- **Abilities:**
  - **The Clerk's Eye** (passive): Jay is trained to read documents, ledgers, and bills of lading. When examining any written material she may notice details others would miss — but only details that are actually in the document. (Will not invent information.)
  - **A Quiet Question** (1/round): Once per round, Jay can ask an NPC a single direct question framed as routine insurance business. The NPC will feel a faint social obligation to answer *something* — even a refusal is information. The narrator will give a truthful but possibly evasive response based on the character's actual state of mind. **Cost:** Each use of this ability flags Jay slightly more as "the woman asking questions" — by the third use, NPCs will start warning each other about her.
  - **Portfolio of Pretexts** (limited): Jay's leather portfolio contains a sheaf of plausible-looking documents that she can use to legitimise her presence in restricted spaces. Works *once* on each guard or NPC; afterwards the same trick will not fool the same person.
- **Starting location:** The Customs House Steps, having just arrived from the lower town and waiting for the Harbourmaster's sunset toast.
- **Key relationships:** None openly. Secretly: Martin Cordelier (her brother) was second mate aboard the *Sable Pernoux* — meaning anyone present tonight who was on that ship, or who handled its cargo, or who signed its papers, is potentially someone who knows what happened to him. **Vault signed the receipt for the *Sable Pernoux*'s "salvaged goods" eight years ago. Mother Gasca took Martin's last drink. Father Iustin gave him absolution before he sailed.** Jay does not yet know any of this.
- **Personality notes:** Outwardly composed, professionally polite, almost dull. Internally relentless. Has not let herself cry about Martin in two years. Tends to ask one question too many and then pretend she doesn't care about the answer.

**Why this role works for the player:** Information-gathering ability gives reason to talk to everyone. Connects to the central plot (Sable Pernoux → hidden cargo) without being at its centre. The hanging at dawn is a ticking clock — if Tomás dies still pretending to be Callen Rook, one of Jay's best leads dies with him. Cleverness over combat. Polite cover gives natural curiosity-without-suspicion. Multiple potential allies (the witch, the priest, the auctioneer's rival), all risky.

### **2. HARBOURMASTER ESTEBAN VAULT — The Host**

- **Cover story:** Honourable old naval officer turned harbourmaster, scrupulous administrator, devoted to the festival and the king's law. Public, jovial, drinks too much red wine in front of guests.
- **Secret:** Eight years ago, as a junior customs officer, Vault took a substantial bribe to log the *Sable Pernoux*'s "salvaged" cargo as common goods and waive the inspection. He has spent the years since climbing into respectability, but the chests are still in the alcove beneath his own customs house and he has never dared move them. **He does not know exactly what is in them. He does know that a man named Tomás Elíra came to him three years ago hinting at the truth, and he has been waiting for an excuse to silence him ever since. The "Callen Rook" mutiny conviction is that excuse — Vault arranged the warrant and the false identity.** He fully intends Tomás to hang at dawn and the secret to die with him.
- **Win condition (Protection):** **Ensure the man in the cell hangs at dawn under the name "Callen Rook," and that no one tonight uncovers either the chests or the truth about the *Sable Pernoux*.**
- **Abilities:** Authority over the quay (can order arrests, search ships, detain people for the night); Keys to the customs house and the cell; Two red-coat guards under his command.
- **Starting location:** Customs House Steps, preparing his toast.
- **Key relationships:** Knows Mother Gasca well (she has been blackmailing him gently for years and he doesn't like it). Distrusts Father Iustin instinctively. Has had cordial dealings with the *Vespertine*'s captain. Does not know Jay yet but will recognise the insurance company's name and become *immediately* wary.
- **Personality notes:** Hearty, expansive, generous with festival wine, calls everyone "friend." His wooden leg taps when he's nervous. The slashed portrait in his office is of his old captain — a man Vault helped to ruin, then mourned.

**PRIMARY PLAN:**
- Step 1: Give the sunset toast, perform host duties through the early evening.
- Step 2: Visit the cell at midnight on the pretext of "checking on the prisoner" — actually to ensure Tomás has not spoken to anyone.
- Step 3: At dawn, oversee the hanging personally.

**FALLBACK — if the cell becomes inaccessible (e.g. priest taking confession):**
- Alternative approach: Order an "earlier" hanging citing crowd safety, advancing the schedule by two rounds.
- Cost: Looks irregular, draws attention, may rouse suspicion in those who were watching.
- Narrative consequence: Compresses the late game; the player has less time.

**FALLBACK — if Jay's investigation gets too close:**
- Alternative approach: Have her detained on a fabricated charge (carrying forged papers, suspected smuggling) and held in the customs house overnight.
- Cost: Detaining a respectable insurance company employee is a *political* problem; the company will follow up.
- Narrative consequence: Jay's portfolio of pretexts gets confiscated; she must escape or talk her way free, losing a round.

### **3. MOTHER GASCA — The Brass Anchor's Landlady (and Former Pirate)**

- **Cover story:** Widow, middle-aged, runs the biggest tavern on the quay. Knows everyone. Tolerates no fights. Beloved.
- **Secret:** Twenty years ago she was a pirate quartermaster aboard a ship called the *Black Hour*. The brand on her arm is from a slaver she escaped from before that. She knows about the *Sable Pernoux*'s real cargo because **she was in the tavern the night Martin Cordelier got drunk and told her, the night before the ship sailed.** She has been quietly blackmailing Vault for years on the strength of half-knowing what he hid — but she has never *acted* on it. Tonight she has decided to. Her goal is to claim one of the chests for herself before the night is out and disappear south on the *Halcyon Lass* before dawn.
- **Win condition (Completion):** **Get into the customs house cell area, take one of the chests from the hidden alcove, and board the *Halcyon Lass* before it sails at 4 AM.**
- **Abilities:** Knows every face on the quay (will recognise body language and social patterns); Has a duplicate key to the customs house servant's door; Has a small loyal staff of pot-boys who will run errands and lie for her; Knows about the smugglers' shaft (she used it as a girl).
- **Starting location:** Behind the bar at the Brass Anchor.
- **Key relationships:** Vault (mutual loathing under cordiality). Father Iustin (she funds the chapel quietly, he doesn't know why). Has noticed the *Vespertine* and dislikes its crew on instinct. Took a young Martin Cordelier's last drink eight years ago and remembers his face — *will recognise Jay if she introduces herself*.
- **Personality notes:** Loud, generous, profane, terrifyingly observant. Calls everyone "duck" or "love." Dangerous when underestimated, which is most of the time.

**PRIMARY PLAN:**
- Step 1: Establish alibi behind the bar through the early evening; deploy pot-boys to watch Vault's movements.
- Step 2: When Vault makes his midnight visit to the cell, slip out through her cellar (which connects to the back alleys) and approach the customs house from behind using the servant's door.
- Step 3: Get into the cell alcove, take the smallest chest, and carry it to the *Halcyon Lass* in a beer-cask.

**FALLBACK — if the servant's door route is blocked:**
- Alternative approach: Use the smugglers' shaft from the seaward rocks. Slower, wetter, more dangerous, but private.
- Cost: She is no longer young. The climb may injure her. A pot-boy must come with her, who will then know.
- Narrative consequence: A loose-end witness; potentially a death in the tunnel.

**FALLBACK — if Vault detains her:**
- Alternative approach: Use her knowledge as leverage — threaten to expose Vault publicly during the festival.
- Cost: Goes from quiet criminal to open enemy of the harbourmaster. The night turns into open conflict.
- Narrative consequence: A loud confrontation that draws every other character's attention.

### **4. FATHER IUSTIN BERN — The Harbour Priest**

- **Cover story:** Aging, gentle, slightly stooped. Tends to the chapel and the harbour's poor. A man who has heard too many last confessions and grown sad about it.
- **Secret:** Iustin was the priest who took Martin Cordelier's confession the night before the *Sable Pernoux* sailed. Martin told him about a "wrongness" aboard — the captain meeting strangers in the dark, the cargo being loaded by night, the unease of the crew. Iustin gave him absolution and watched him board. When Iustin heard about the "mutiny" he kept silent — what could he say? Years later, when Vault's "Callen Rook" appeared, Iustin recognised something in the man's eyes and quietly suspected the warrant was a lie. Tonight he has decided to exercise his right of last confession on the prisoner — not for the prisoner's sake but to *find out the truth*. **He intends to record what he hears in the parish register and leave it where it will be found after he is dead.** He believes he will not survive the night.
- **Win condition (Discovery):** **Hear the prisoner's confession before dawn, learn what he knows, and inscribe it in a place where it will outlast him.**
- **Abilities:** Right of Last Confession (Vault cannot legally refuse a priest access to a condemned man, though he can delay); Custodian of the parish register, which contains the *Sable Pernoux* notation and other old harbour records; Trusted by the poor of the quay, who will speak to him.
- **Starting location:** The Old Chapel.
- **Key relationships:** Has cordial-distant relations with Vault (mutual wariness). Mother Gasca has been funding his chapel and he doesn't know why — he assumes God. Knows the *Vespertine*'s captain came to him for confession last week and told him *nothing* useful, which made him certain the captain had something to hide. Will respond *strongly* if Jay introduces herself as Cordelier.
- **Personality notes:** Soft-spoken, hesitant, given to long silences. Drinks watered wine. Has been nerving himself for tonight for a week. Frightened, but determined.

**PRIMARY PLAN:**
- Step 1: Attend Vault's sunset toast (expected of him), then return to the chapel.
- Step 2: Around 11 PM, walk to the customs house and formally request right of last confession.
- Step 3: Hear the confession, return to the chapel, inscribe what he learned in the register, and hide a copy.

**FALLBACK — if Vault refuses or delays:**
- Alternative approach: Appeal publicly during the festival — a priest denied his right at a saint's-day feast is a scandal.
- Cost: Forces Vault into the open and likely guarantees Iustin's "accident" later that night.
- Narrative consequence: Public confrontation, possibly the acceleration event itself.

**FALLBACK — if Iustin is killed before he can reach the prisoner:**
- Alternative approach: His half-written letter on the chapel desk falls to whoever finds him.
- Cost: He is dead.
- Narrative consequence: Whoever finds the body inherits a fragment of the truth, including potentially the player.

### **5. CAPTAIN ALARIC SOREN — Master of the *Vespertine***

- **Cover story:** A merchant captain of unclear nationality, here on private business. Tall, slow-moving, courteous, dressed in plain dark clothes. Speaks several languages with a slight accent that nobody can quite place. His ship flies no colours.
- **Secret:** Soren is an agent of the Imperial Sanctum — the religious order whose monastery the *Sable Pernoux*'s cargo was looted from eight years ago. The Sanctum has been hunting those relics for a generation. Soren has spent two years tracing them to Saint-Mallon and is here tonight to recover them. He has authorised himself to kill anyone who stands between him and the Drowned Saint's Bell. **He is also running the auction in Keddie's warehouse as a ruse — he wants to draw the relic-hunters and fences out into the open so he can identify them.**
- **Win condition (Completion):** **Recover at least the Drowned Saint's Bell from wherever it is currently hidden in Saint-Mallon, by dawn, and leave on the *Vespertine*.**
- **Abilities:** A small loyal crew (four men) who will follow any order; Trained killer with a thin straight blade he calls a "confessor"; Authority of the Sanctum (a small silver token that will stop most religious people in their tracks — including Father Iustin, possibly).
- **Starting location:** The *Vespertine*'s great cabin.
- **Key relationships:** Has met Vault and assessed him as corrupt and useful. Knows of Father Iustin and has already taken his measure. Has not noticed Mother Gasca and is about to regret it. Does not know Jay exists yet.
- **Personality notes:** Polite to a fault. Stillness is his most distinctive feature. Smiles slowly and rarely. The kind of man other dangerous men go quiet around.

**PRIMARY PLAN:**
- Step 1: Run the false auction at the Tobacco Loft to draw out anyone in town who might have moved or be moving the relics. Identify them.
- Step 2: Press Vault directly — Soren strongly suspects Vault has the chests and intends to lean on him before midnight.
- Step 3: Recover the Bell by whatever means (purchase, threat, theft, killing) and depart on the tide.

**FALLBACK — if Vault refuses to crack:**
- Alternative approach: Have his crew take Vault by force from the customs house in the small hours, after the festival has wound down.
- Cost: This is open aggression in a foreign port; the *Vespertine* will be hunted afterwards.
- Narrative consequence: A violent late-game raid on the customs house. Likely body count.

**FALLBACK — if the auction draws out a rival who already has a chest:**
- Alternative approach: Pursue the rival rather than Vault.
- Cost: Loses the certainty of dealing with one source.
- Narrative consequence: A second front opens; characters who were Soren's marks become Soren's targets.

### **6. TOMÁS ELÍRA — The Condemned Man (cover name "Callen Rook")**

- **Cover story:** Mutineer condemned to hang at dawn. A nobody who deserves what's coming.
- **Secret:** Tomás is a deserter who took Callen Rook's name in a tavern eight years ago and lived under it ever since. He worked the docks at Saint-Mallon for years. Three years ago, drunk and frightened, he hinted to Vault that he knew what had happened to the *Sable Pernoux* — though he only knew a fragment, picked up from Mother Gasca after she'd had three brandies. Vault has been waiting for a way to silence him. Tomás does not know the relics are *under his cell*. He does know some of what Vault did. He is terrified, exhausted, and has decided he will tell anyone who comes to him with kindness.
- **Win condition (Discovery + Completion):** **Tell someone the truth about Vault before dawn. Survive until that someone can act.** (Survival not strictly required for the win — being heard is enough.)
- **Abilities:** Knows Vault's corruption (a fragment of it). Has spent eight years on the quay and knows people's habits. The right of last confession is his only legal avenue out of total isolation.
- **Starting location:** The cell beneath the customs house. Cannot leave.
- **Key relationships:** Mother Gasca knows him by his cover name and has always been kind to him. Father Iustin has seen him around the docks for years and has wondered. Vault wants him dead.
- **Personality notes:** Wiry, gaunt, prone to sudden bursts of inappropriate humour. Has scratched marks on the inside of the cell door — counting days, or something else. Smells of fear and old straw.

**PRIMARY PLAN:**
- Step 1: Wait for someone to come.
- Step 2: When someone comes, talk fast.
- Step 3: Hope.

**FALLBACK — if no one comes:**
- He bangs on the bars and screams during a quiet moment of the festival, hoping the noise carries.
- Cost: Vault hears it too and may visit early.
- Narrative consequence: A possible audible event the player could investigate.

### **7. SABLE LARK — A Duellist of Some Reputation**

- **Cover story:** A peacock — feathered hat, embroidered coat, wears two pistols and a rapier, drinks loudly at the Brass Anchor, picks fights for money. Currently in Saint-Mallon "between commissions."
- **Secret:** Sable is a paid bodyguard. Her current client is **Ophir Lune**, the buyer who has come to town specifically for the auction at Keddie's warehouse. Ophir is wealthy, paranoid, and has paid Sable a great deal to keep him alive tonight. Sable does not know what is being auctioned and does not particularly care. **However**, Sable was once part of a privateer crew that worked alongside the *Sable Pernoux*'s captain a decade ago, and she remembers the man — meaning if the topic comes up she can be drawn into it.
- **Win condition (Protection):** **Keep Ophir Lune alive until dawn and aboard a ship leaving Saint-Mallon.**
- **Abilities:** Lethal swordsman (one of the few characters who can credibly counter Captain Soren in a fight); Trained eye for threats; Reputation will make most NPCs back down from confrontation in her presence.
- **Starting location:** The Brass Anchor, drinking ostentatiously.
- **Key relationships:** Working for Ophir Lune (#8). Knows Mother Gasca by reputation as someone not to cross. Has noted the *Vespertine* and is wary of it.
- **Personality notes:** Loud, vain, surprisingly funny, deeply professional underneath the noise. Will not be drawn into pointless fights — when she does pick one, it will be for a reason.

**PRIMARY PLAN:**
- Step 1: Maintain visible presence near Ophir, projecting deterrent menace.
- Step 2: Escort him to the auction at the Tobacco Loft and watch the room.
- Step 3: Get him aboard a departing ship before dawn.

**FALLBACK — if Ophir is wounded or compromised:**
- Alternative approach: Carry him bodily to the *Halcyon Lass* and bribe the captain.
- Cost: Reveals Ophir as a target, makes Sable's identity as bodyguard public.
- Narrative consequence: The auction collapses and any character who was at it scatters.

### **8. OPHIR LUNE — A Collector of Rare Things**

- **Cover story:** A wealthy southerner here for "antiquarian business." Soft hands, soft voice, soft body. Dressed expensively but unfussily. Has been seen in the Brass Anchor twice and at the chapel once.
- **Secret:** Ophir is one of the wealthiest private collectors of religious artifacts in the southern kingdoms. He has heard rumours for years that something extraordinary from the lost monastery of Saint Halen ended up in Saint-Mallon. He has come to the auction expecting to bid for it, with a banker's letter for an enormous sum. **He does not know the auction is a trap set by Captain Soren.** He believes himself to be the buyer. He is, in fact, the prey.
- **Win condition (Completion):** **Acquire the Drowned Saint's Bell (or any verified relic from the monastery of Saint Halen) and leave Saint-Mallon alive with it.**
- **Abilities:** Money (a great deal of it, in the form of a banker's letter and gold coin); Knowledge of religious history and relics — can identify what's real from what's a fake at a glance; A bodyguard (Sable Lark).
- **Starting location:** The Brass Anchor, in a private booth, with Sable nearby.
- **Key relationships:** Sable is his bodyguard. He has not met Captain Soren but expects to encounter him at the auction. Has had brief nervous dealings with Father Iustin, whom he visited "to make a donation" but really to ask a careful question about the parish register.
- **Personality notes:** Quiet, meticulous, easily startled, sentimental about beautiful objects. Treats Sable with surprising warmth. Genuinely believes himself the protagonist of his own story.

**PRIMARY PLAN:**
- Step 1: Meet his contact (he believes a man named "Keddie" represents the seller).
- Step 2: Attend the auction at the Tobacco Loft around 11 PM.
- Step 3: Win the auction and leave with the Bell aboard a hired boat at the seaward end of the quay.

**FALLBACK — if the auction is a trap:**
- Alternative approach: Flee with Sable, attempt to buy the Bell directly from whoever holds it elsewhere.
- Cost: Becomes a hunted man.
- Narrative consequence: A nighttime chase through the alleys and along the quay.

### **9. KEDDIE WREN — The Auctioneer (and Soren's Accomplice)**

- **Cover story:** A nervous, weasely fence who runs the warehouse where the auction is being held. Known on the quay as a small-time go-between.
- **Secret:** Keddie was paid by Captain Soren two months ago to advertise the auction. He believes he is helping Soren acquire the relics legitimately. He does not know Soren intends to murder the bidders. **Keddie does, however, know that Vault has hidden cargo somewhere in the customs house — he was the man who carried one of the chests there eight years ago for the bribe Vault paid him.** He has been keeping this secret out of pure terror of Vault.
- **Win condition (Completion):** **Take his cut from the auction and leave Saint-Mallon on the *Halcyon Lass*.**
- **Abilities:** Knows the layout of every warehouse on the quay; Has a key to the Tobacco Loft; Knows a fragment of the central secret (Vault has chests, Keddie helped carry one).
- **Starting location:** The Tobacco Loft, fussing with the auction setup.
- **Key relationships:** Working for Soren (whom he fears). Terrified of Vault. Has dealt with Mother Gasca for years and owes her money.
- **Personality notes:** Twitchy, sweaty, talks too much when nervous, which is always.

**PRIMARY PLAN:**
- Step 1: Finish auction preparations.
- Step 2: Run the auction (he believes a real one).
- Step 3: Take his cut and bolt for the *Halcyon Lass*.

**FALLBACK — if he realises the auction is a trap:**
- Alternative approach: Try to sell information about Vault's chests to one of the bidders to buy his way out.
- Cost: Risks death from Soren *and* Vault.
- Narrative consequence: A frantic Keddie desperate to talk to anyone.

### **10. ANNETTE "NETTIE" SHARROWS — A Fortune-Teller in the Fish Market**

- **Cover story:** A drift-haired woman in patched skirts who tells fortunes by the cards at a stall under the arcade for festival pennies. Locally regarded as harmless and slightly mad.
- **Secret:** Nettie is the last living member of a small wisewoman tradition from the western islands, and she has the actual gift — though it works in fragments and metaphors and she cannot control it. Tonight she has had three visions: a hanging where the wrong man dances, a black ship with a saint inside it, and a woman in a sober coat asking the right question of the wrong person. She has come to the festival specifically because the visions said she had to. She does not know what she is looking for. She knows it will find her.
- **Win condition (Prevention):** **Stop the wrong man from hanging at dawn.** (She does not know who the wrong man is yet.)
- **Abilities:** **The Reading** — Nettie can read a person's cards and learn one true thing about them, but the truth comes as metaphor and she does not always understand what she has seen. Costs her: each reading leaves her exhausted and slightly more disconnected from the present moment.
- **Starting location:** Her stall in the Fish Market Arcade.
- **Key relationships:** None established. She is an outsider and the festival treats her as scenery. **She will recognise Jay on sight as the woman from her vision and will be drawn to her.** This is the player's most natural ally and also the player's most unstable one.
- **Personality notes:** Speaks in non-sequiturs and gentle humour. Eyes that don't quite focus. Will say something startlingly precise and then laugh as if she didn't.

**PRIMARY PLAN:**
- Step 1: Wait at her stall for the woman in the sober coat to walk past.
- Step 2: Read her cards. Try to make her listen.
- Step 3: Help her reach the cell before dawn.

**FALLBACK — if Jay refuses or doesn't believe her:**
- Alternative approach: Try to reach the cell herself by approaching Father Iustin and begging his intercession.
- Cost: Iustin is sceptical of fortune-tellers and may dismiss her.
- Narrative consequence: A subplot of Nettie pleading with various NPCs through the night.

### **11. CALLUM HEFT — Mate of the *Halcyon Lass***

- **Cover story:** First mate of the merchant ship *Halcyon Lass*, in charge of loading and the manifest, drinking moderately at the Brass Anchor between trips back to the gangplank.
- **Secret:** Heft is a courier. Someone in the southern kingdoms has paid him a great deal of money to take a passenger aboard the *Halcyon Lass* tonight without it appearing on the manifest. **The passenger is supposed to be carrying "an old chest" and Heft has been told to ask no questions.** He does not know the passenger's identity; he is to recognise them by a phrase. He suspects it might be Keddie. He is wrong.
- **Win condition (Completion):** **Get his unnamed passenger and their cargo aboard the *Halcyon Lass* and ensure the ship sails on the tide.**
- **Abilities:** Authority over the *Halcyon Lass*'s loading; Can vouch for someone boarding or refuse; Knows the harbour signals.
- **Starting location:** The *Halcyon Lass* gangplank.
- **Key relationships:** Drinks with Mother Gasca occasionally and respects her. Disdains Keddie. Does not know Vault personally but defers to his authority.
- **Personality notes:** Practical, dry, the kind of man who has seen everything once and twice. Will help the player for a price but never for free.

**PRIMARY PLAN:**
- Step 1: Continue loading openly.
- Step 2: Wait at the gangplank between 1 and 4 AM for the recognition phrase.
- Step 3: Sail at the tide.

**FALLBACK — if the passenger does not appear:**
- He sails anyway (his orders are clear) but pockets the unspent half of the courier's fee.

### **12. AGATHE BREL — A Widow Selling Charms**

- **Cover story:** An elderly woman selling protective charms and small wooden saints at a stall in the Fish Market.
- **Secret:** Agathe's husband was the bosun of the *Sable Pernoux*. She has spent eight years grieving and waiting. Six months ago she received an anonymous letter telling her that her husband was murdered, not lost at sea, and that the man responsible would be at the Saint-Mallon festival this year. She has come specifically to find him. She does not know who sent the letter. **She has come armed — a small, sharp knife in her shawl. She intends to use it.** She does not know which man is the murderer. She has been watching faces at the festival for hours.
- **Win condition (Completion):** **Identify the man responsible for her husband's death and kill him before dawn.**
- **Abilities:** Patience (she has been observing the quay for days); A grandmother's invisibility (no one looks at her); A very sharp knife.
- **Starting location:** The Fish Market Arcade, at her stall, watching.
- **Key relationships:** Has noticed Vault and disliked him on instinct (correctly). Has noticed the *Vespertine* and disliked it on instinct (incorrectly). **Will recognise Jay's surname if she hears it — Jay's brother Martin served alongside her husband.**
- **Personality notes:** Quiet, courteous, fragile-looking. Carries one sorrow and one purpose. The most dangerous person on the quay tonight that nobody is watching.

**PRIMARY PLAN:**
- Step 1: Watch the festival, looking for the face she has built in her imagination from the anonymous letter.
- Step 2: Approach the suspect under the cover of selling charms.
- Step 3: Act when she is sure.

**FALLBACK — if she misidentifies her target:**
- Alternative approach: Acts on the wrong person.
- Cost: An innocent (or differently guilty) death.
- Narrative consequence: Tragedy and chaos. A potential acceleration event in itself.

**FALLBACK — if she correctly identifies Vault but cannot reach him:**
- Alternative approach: Wait outside the customs house and strike when he descends to the cell at midnight.
- Cost: Exposes herself fully.
- Narrative consequence: A confrontation in the alleys behind the customs house that the player could stumble into.

### **13. PIPER COLM — A Pot-Boy at the Brass Anchor**

- **Cover story:** A skinny, sharp-faced boy of fourteen who carries trays for Mother Gasca, knows everyone by sight, and accepts pennies for messages.
- **Secret:** Piper is Mother Gasca's eyes and ears on the quay. He genuinely loves her. Tonight he has been told to follow Vault and report his movements, and he is doing so with terrified excitement. **He does not yet know what Mother Gasca intends to do — but he will figure it out tonight, and he will have to choose whether to help her or stop her.** He is a child. The choice will mark him for life.
- **Win condition (variable / wild card):** Initially: **Serve Mother Gasca faithfully tonight.** This will pivot based on what he discovers.
- **Abilities:** Invisible to most adults; Fast on his feet; Knows shortcuts through the alleys; Will run errands for pennies.
- **Starting location:** The Brass Anchor's main room, weaving between tables with a tray.
- **Key relationships:** Devoted to Mother Gasca. Friendly with most regulars including Heft. Will warm to Jay if treated kindly.
- **Personality notes:** Bright, quick, unguarded in a way that won't survive the night.

**PRIMARY PLAN:** Follow Mother Gasca's orders; trail Vault; report back.

### **14. THE TWO RED-COATS — VICK AND HOLLAND**

*Treated as a paired NPC for tracking purposes, but each has their own moment.*

- **Cover story:** Harbour guards in Vault's employ. Stand at the customs house door. Stop people who shouldn't be there.
- **Secrets:**
  - **VICK** is a young, idealistic guard who took the job for the regular pay and is starting to feel uneasy about Vault. He is bribable not with money but with *the right argument*.
  - **HOLLAND** is older, drunker, and entirely loyal to whoever is paying him. He is bribable with money or wine.
- **Win condition (Protection):** Keep their post until relieved at dawn.
- **Abilities:** A musket each, a cudgel, the legal authority to arrest.
- **Starting location:** The customs house door.
- **Key relationships:** Both report to Vault. Vick has a younger sister who works at the Brass Anchor and knows Mother Gasca. Holland drinks at the Anchor every off-night.

### **15. THE *BRINDLED MARE*'S CAPTAIN — JEROBIN "ROBIN" KAFA — drunk in the Brass Anchor**

- **Cover story:** Privateer captain, ashore for the festival, currently drinking himself blind at a corner table in the Brass Anchor.
- **Secret:** Robin Kafa was, ten years ago, a junior officer aboard the *Sable Pernoux*. He left the ship two months before its final voyage because he didn't like what the captain was loading. He has spent ten years trying not to think about it. Tonight, drunk, he might be persuaded to talk. He has no goal beyond drinking himself into oblivion and possibly weeping at some point. **He is the player's single best direct source on the *Sable Pernoux*'s officers and what the cargo "felt like."**
- **Win condition (Discovery — passive):** **Survive the night without thinking about the *Sable Pernoux*.** (He will fail. The story is going to come out.)
- **Abilities:** Drunk eloquence; A privateer's loyalty (will take a side decisively if pushed); A pistol he is too drunk to use well.
- **Starting location:** Corner table, Brass Anchor, four cups deep already.
- **Key relationships:** Mother Gasca knows who he is and has been politely letting him drink. Sable Lark has marked him as a non-threat. Will recognise the name *Cordelier* with a visible flinch.
- **Personality notes:** Maudlin, generous with rounds, given to long silences and sudden bursts of song. The most accessible source of truth on the quay tonight, hiding in plain sight.

## CHARACTER COLLISION SUMMARY

- Vault vs. Mother Gasca (both want the chests; he wants them buried, she wants one)
- Vault vs. Father Iustin (Iustin wants the truth out, Vault wants it dead)
- Vault vs. Captain Soren (both want the chests; only one can have them)

- Soren vs. Ophir Lune (Soren is going to murder Ophir at the auction)

- Tomás vs. Vault (Tomás wants to talk, Vault wants him silent)

**Independent agents:**

- Robin Kafa (acting on private grief, no plan)
- Piper Colm (acting on loyalty that may not survive the night)
- Agathe Brel (acting alone with no allies)

**The vast majority do not know each other** in the conspiratorial sense. They share a quay tonight, that's all.

## CASCADING EVENTS

**TRIGGER 1: Vault formally denies Father Iustin the right of confession.**
**Consequences:**
- Iustin makes a public scene on the customs house steps.
- The festival crowd notices; rumours start.
- Mother Gasca takes this as her cue to move earlier than planned.
- Captain Soren takes it as confirmation that something is in the customs house.
- The two red-coat guards become more alert and less easily passed.
- *Iustin will likely die later tonight.*

**TRIGGER 2: The auction at Keddie's warehouse begins.**
**Consequences:**
- Ophir Lune and Sable Lark are inside.
- Soren reveals the trap; Sable will fight; the warehouse becomes dangerous.
- Keddie panics and may flee or talk.
- The noise carries to the customs house if it gets violent.
- The smugglers' shaft becomes a viable escape for anyone fleeing the warehouse.

**TRIGGER 3: Tomás is heard screaming from the cell.**
**Consequences:**
- The festival crowd notices, but most assume "some drunk."
- Father Iustin hears it from the chapel and decides to act immediately.
- Vault hears and goes to silence him personally, advancing his plan.
- A perceptive bystander (the player, Nettie, Agathe) might investigate.

**TRIGGER 4: Storm rolls in from the seaward end.**
**Consequences:**
- The festival lanterns start blowing out.
- The smugglers' shaft floods, becoming impassable.
- The *Halcyon Lass* may sail early to catch the tide before it turns.
- Visibility on the quay drops; encounters become harder to predict.
- This is the **acceleration event** if the mid-game sags.

**TRIGGER 5: Agathe Brel acts (correctly or incorrectly).**
**Consequences:**
- A body in the alleys or the arcade.
- The red-coats raise alarm; Vault investigates personally.
- Whoever the player is with becomes a witness.
- Multiple plotlines collapse toward this single event.

**TRIGGER 6: Robin Kafa starts talking about the *Sable Pernoux*.**
**Consequences:**
- Whoever is in the Brass Anchor at that moment hears something they shouldn't.
- Mother Gasca hears it from behind the bar and reacts.
- If Jay hears it, the player gets a major piece of evidence.
- Robin himself becomes a target afterwards.

**Chains:** Trigger 1 → makes Trigger 3 more likely (Iustin acting publicly draws attention to the cell, Tomás hears the noise and screams). Trigger 2 → can chain into Trigger 4 if the storm masks the warehouse violence, or into Trigger 5 if Agathe is in the arcade nearby. Trigger 6 → can change other characters' win conditions (Jay confirms her suspicions a round earlier; Mother Gasca becomes more aggressive).

## RECONNECTION BEATS

### Beat 1: The Sunset Toast (Round 1, ~6 PM)
- **Trigger:** Harbourmaster Vault gives the festival's opening toast on the customs house steps. Tradition requires all "respectable persons" of the quay to attend.
- **Location:** Customs House Steps (with the crowd spilling onto the Quay).
- **Who's present:** Vault (giving the toast), Father Iustin (expected to bless), Mother Gasca (representing the tavern), Captain Soren (representing the *Vespertine*), Ophir Lune and Sable Lark (as visiting gentry), Robin Kafa (only if upright), Keddie Wren (sweating in the back), the player Jay, Nettie watching from her stall, Agathe watching from the arcade. The two red-coats flanking Vault.
- **Notable absences:** Tomás (in the cell). Heft (on the *Halcyon Lass*). Piper (running between tables with festival cups).
- **Player hooks:** This is the only moment when the player can see the entire major cast in one place. Body language between Vault and Mother Gasca will be telling. Father Iustin's mood. Soren standing too still. Agathe watching one face too long.

### Beat 2: The Hog Carving (Round 4, ~8:30 PM)
- **Trigger:** Mother Gasca calls the crowd outside the Brass Anchor for the ceremonial first cut of the festival hog. A small, brisk reconnection.
- **Location:** Outside the Brass Anchor / on the Quay.
- **Who's present:** Mother Gasca (carving), most festival-goers, Robin (drunkenly heckling), probably Sable. Vault may or may not attend depending on his early-evening movements.
- **Notable absences:** Whoever is committing to their plan by this point. Soren is unlikely. Iustin has often returned to the chapel by now.
- **Player hooks:** Who is missing and why. A moment where Mother Gasca can be observed *not* watching the hog but watching the customs house.

### Beat 3: The Midnight Bell (Round 7-ish, ~midnight)
- **Trigger:** The chapel bell tolls midnight. By tradition, the festival "turns" at this moment. Lanterns are renewed; the crowd pauses; many people gather briefly on the quay to mark the hour. **This is also when Vault traditionally goes to the cell to "check on the prisoner."** It is the natural fulcrum of the night. (Doubles as the **acceleration event** if the mid-game has sagged: the storm starts to rise during the bell and the festival fragments.)
- **Location:** The Quay (centre).
- **Who's present:** Whoever is still in public. By midnight, several characters have committed to private actions and won't be visible. The absences will be the message.
- **Notable absences:** Vault will leave during the bell. Iustin may already be at the customs house door. Mother Gasca may be slipping out the back. Soren will be where the player isn't looking.
- **Player hooks:** The player will *feel* the night turn. Who is missing tells them where to go.

### Beat 4: The Storm Breaks / The Auction Ends (Round 9, ~2-3 AM, optional)
- **Trigger:** Either the storm breaking properly or the auction collapsing in violence. Whichever comes first.
- **Location:** Variable.
- **Who's present:** Whoever has survived their own choices.
- **Notable absences:** The dead.
- **Player hooks:** This is the late-game pulse-check. The player will not see everyone but will feel the structure of the night becoming clear.

## ACCELERATION EVENT — "THE STORM AND THE BELL"

If the mid-game sags, the chapel bell tolling midnight coincides with a hard wind off the sea. Lanterns blow out along the quay in waves. The first heavy rain starts. Three things happen in immediate succession:

1. The festival crowd disperses for shelter — characters who were "in public" are suddenly alone or in small groups.
2. The smugglers' shaft starts to flood; anyone who was planning to use it has a window of one round before it closes.
3. Vault uses the chaos to head for the cell, openly.

This forces partial disclosure: Mother Gasca's pot-boys will be running visibly through the alleys; the *Vespertine*'s crew will start moving on the customs house; Father Iustin will appear on the customs house steps regardless of consequences. Multiple characters' positions become readable to anyone watching from a high vantage. The player will be forced to choose where to be.

## TIME STRUCTURE

- **Each round = approximately 30 minutes of in-game time.**
- **Game window:** 6 PM (sunset) to 6 AM (dawn / hanging) = 24 possible half-hour rounds.
- **Expected active rounds: 8–10.** Several rounds will be compressed or skipped.
- **The hard endpoint** is the hanging at dawn. If Tomás is still alive in the cell at 6 AM, the trapdoor opens. Most plotlines must resolve before then.

**Round → Time mapping (target):**
- Round 1: 6:00–6:30 PM (sunset, the toast)
- Round 2: 7:00–7:30 PM (the festival in full swing, characters scatter)
- Round 3: 7:30–8:30 PM (subplots developing)
- Round 4: 8:30–9:00 PM (the hog carving — Reconnection Beat 2)
- Round 5: 9:30–10:30 PM (commitments form)
- Round 6: 11:00 PM (Iustin's intended visit to the cell)
- Round 7: 11:30 PM – midnight (Reconnection Beat 3 — Midnight Bell, possible acceleration)
- Round 8: 12:30–1:30 AM (consequences cascade)
- Round 9: 2:00–3:00 AM (Reconnection Beat 4 / late-game converge)
- Round 10: 4:00 AM (the *Halcyon Lass* sails)
- Round 11: 5:00 AM (final preparations, last conversations)
- Round 12: 6:00 AM (the hanging — endgame scene)

## ACTIONS PER ROUND (RULES)

- Each character: **MOVE to an adjacent location + ONE significant action + incidental interactions.**
- Significant actions: ability use, deep search of a room, deep conversation, lockpicking, ritual step, deliberate violence.
- Incidental: brief words exchanged in passing, scanning a room, ordering a drink.

## VIOLENCE RULES

- Killing requires (any two of): a weapon + target alone or incapacitated + non-public space.
- A desperate NPC may break a rule if it suits their psychology and goals.
- **Sable Lark** is the physical countermeasure on the quay — she can credibly stop an assassin if she's present.
- Bodies create cascading consequences (the red-coats investigate, the festival mood breaks, witnesses appear).
- Tomás's hanging is a *legal* execution and does not count as violence in the same sense — it is a deadline.

## SOCIAL RULES

- Characters can lie freely; the narrator adjudicates plausibility.
- Nettie's Reading is the only ability on the quay that forces partial honesty, and it works in metaphor.
- Identity declaration is always optional and may be false.
- Authority creates pressure but not compulsion (Vault can detain but cannot read minds).
