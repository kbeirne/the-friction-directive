## ROUND 0 — STARTING KNOWLEDGE (before play begins)

### Characters Known
- **None by sight on the quay.** Jay is a stranger here. She knows the following names from her dossier and her own family history, but does not yet know what any of them look like:
  - **Harbourmaster Esteban Vault** — the man she has come to interview. She knows from her papers that he signed a receipt eight years ago for "salvaged goods" from the *Sable Pernoux* and stamped the wreck-claim "settled in full." She has never met him.
  - **The condemned man "Callen Rook"** — she knows from the public posting in the lower town that a man of this name will hang at dawn for mutiny aboard the *Sable Pernoux*. She does not know whether this is suspicious or a coincidence — the timing is what brought her *now*.
  - **Martin Cordelier** — her brother. Second mate of the *Sable Pernoux*. Officially dead in the mutiny eight years ago.

### Locations Known
- **Saint-Mallon, the Lantern Quay** — she has just walked down from the lower town. She knows the rough layout from her approach: a long curving stone harbour, lanterns strung between masts, three ships moored along the quay, festival in full preparation, a scaffold at the landward end. She has not yet been inside any building.
- **The Customs House** — she knows it exists and that Vault works there. She has not been inside.

### Plot Knowledge
- The *Sable Pernoux* sailed from Saint-Mallon eight years ago, suffered a mutiny at sea, and was lost. The official insurance file lists no survivors but is unusually thin — suspiciously thin, in Jay's professional opinion.
- Her brother Martin was second mate aboard. The official report says he was killed by mutineers.
- Vault was the customs officer who signed off on the wreck and on whatever cargo was "salvaged" before the ship sailed. His signature is on three documents in Jay's portfolio.
- **A man named Callen Rook is to hang for the mutiny at dawn tomorrow.** This is what made the company suddenly willing to authorise the trip — Jay framed it to her superiors as "an opportunity to interview a confessed mutineer before execution."
- **Jay's private theory:** Something happened aboard the *Sable Pernoux* that was *not* a mutiny in the ordinary sense. She has no evidence beyond her professional unease and the gaps in the file. She is here to find out.

### Items Held
- A leather portfolio containing: copies of the *Sable Pernoux* insurance file (thin); a letter of introduction from the Compagnie d'Assurance Maritime de Lys; three documents bearing Vault's signature; a sheaf of blank pretext-papers (forms, requisitions, letters) usable as cover.
- A small brass writing-set (quill, inkpot, sand-shaker).
- Travelling money in a purse at her belt.
- A locket with a small portrait of her brother Martin (worn under her shirt, not visible).
- A sober travelling coat, sensible boots, a neat tricorn hat.

### Active Commitments
- She has, on paper, an appointment with Harbourmaster Vault to discuss the *Sable Pernoux* claim. She intends to attend his sunset toast first as a matter of courtesy — and to observe him before they speak.

### Critical Gaps (what Jay does NOT know)
- That the man condemned as "Callen Rook" is actually Tomás Elíra, a deserter.
- That Vault is corrupt and arranged the false warrant himself.
- That cargo from the *Sable Pernoux* is still in Saint-Mallon, beneath the customs house cell.
- That her brother Martin's last drink was at the Brass Anchor and his last confession was at the chapel.
- That at least four people on the quay tonight know fragments of the truth about the *Sable Pernoux*.
- That she is about to be drawn into half a dozen other people's plots that all share the same tangled root.

## ROUND 1 — KNOWLEDGE UPDATE

### Characters Known
- **Harbourmaster Esteban Vault** — now seen in person. Barrel-chested, beard going grey, faded velvet coat, wooden leg planted on his right side. Gave the sunset toast with the polished ease of long practice. Excellent host, extremely generous in public. Appointment set for **10 AM tomorrow at the customs house** to discuss the *Sable Pernoux* claim. Jay has observed: (a) his wooden leg tapped once when she said the company's name and did not tap again; (b) he drank slightly faster from his cup midway through their conversation; (c) his warmth in his face did not always reach his eyes; (d) he asked her a probe-question about *why* the Compagnie had assigned the file now — offering "anniversary" and "hanging" as possible motivations — which was dressed as idle curiosity but phrased with too much care; (e) he then offered her a lodging recommendation that included a "Widow Mercier whose mother-in-law was a Cordelier too, a common enough name" — a classic mirror-surname test for a reaction. Player has NOT learned any secret about him; she has learned he is a careful, practised man who noticed her arrival.
- **"Agathe"** — small old woman, late sixties, grey shawl, pale eyes, cold hands. Sells charms and dried herbs in the **Fish Market Arcade, second stall in from the landward end**. Was watching Vault throughout the toast. When asked about lost sailors, said: *"I knew some, mademoiselle. Most of us along this quay knew some. There has been a long time of long times."* Made an oblique comment about Vault: *"He gives a fine toast, the Harbourmaster. He has had a great deal of practice."* Took Jay's hand when offered, held it a moment longer than necessary, gave only her first name. Invited Jay to visit her stall "for a charm, if you like — they are not all foolishness." Did not visibly react to the surname Cordelier. Left for the arcade immediately after the exchange.
- **"Mother Gasca"** — the wide-shouldered innkeeper-woman who stood on the lower step during the toast. Named to Jay by Vault as the keeper of the **Brass Anchor** tavern. During the toast's silence her free hand was curled into her apron in a way that suggested she was gripping something other than fabric. Did not look at Vault during his "Callen Rook" announcement — looked instead at the priest. Vault described her as "warmer than the harbour wine."

When he descended from the steps his shoulders were pulled in tight. He left alone up the alley-mouth toward the chapel.
- **Captain Beauvais of the *Trefoil*** — coastal captain, pale scar across one jaw, named to Jay in Vault's circle. Headed toward the gangplanks after the conversation.

- **Goris** — heavy-set man with a visible gold chain, trade described by Vault as "not a polite one for the festival." Named to Jay in Vault's circle. Stood slightly closer to Vault than he was at first. Watched Jay with an unreadable expression as he left. Headed into the Brass Anchor.

His attention was on Vault or the priest (Jay could not tell which). Later walked west along the quay on the seaward side, heading toward the gangplanks, with an easy wasteless gait.

- **Two red-coat guards at the customs house door** — one older and visibly drinking (leaning slightly on his musket), one younger and straight-backed. Both reported to Vault; the older gave Vault a nod when he went inside, the younger held the door open without meeting his eye.
- **A watchman at the tobacco-sign warehouse** — grimy coat, grey-shot beard, clay jug of cider at his feet, jug-deep already. Told Jay *"Festival's the other way, mademoiselle. Nothin' to see down here."*

Three ships moored along the quay: a workmanlike merchantman loading crates, a scarred privateer schooner with no visible activity, a sleek black-hulled ship at the seaward end flying no colours.

- **The alley-mouth to the chapel** — the alley leading up from the Lantern Walk toward the chapel where the priest went.

- **The Rue des Treilles** — in the lower town; Vault recommended Widow Mercier's lodging house on that street.
