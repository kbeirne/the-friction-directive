## ROUND 1 — "THE SUNSET TOAST" (6:00 – 6:30 PM)
### Context
The Feast of Saint Mallon opens at sunset. Tradition demands all "respectable persons" of the quay attend the Harbourmaster's toast on the customs house steps. This is **Reconnection Beat 1** — the only scene tonight where the entire major cast will be in one place.

Dead-end check: run on every character. None stymied. All paths open.

---

**JAY CORDELIER (PLAYER)**
- Location: Customs House Steps (just arrived from the lower town).
- Action: Stands at the edge of the crowd. Has not introduced herself.
- Plan: Determined by player.

**HARBOURMASTER VAULT**
- Location: Top of the Customs House Steps.
- Action: Performs the host. Wooden leg planted, third cup of harbour wine in hand. Greets gentry by name. Glances at the customs house door twice — once at the red-coats, once at nothing. Gives the toast as he gives it every year: a bawdy story about Saint Mallon and a fishwife, a cheer for the king, a cheer for the harbour, a long silence for "the men still at sea." Then announces with deliberate gravity that "the king's justice will be served at dawn upon the mutineer Callen Rook."
- Watching for: Anyone who reacts to that name.
- Plan: Retreat into the customs house briefly to confirm Tomás is secure and pass an instruction to Vick. Then return to the festival.

**MOTHER GASCA**
- Location: Lower step of the Customs House Steps, pewter cup in hand.
- Action: Toasts with the crowd. Watches Vault's face during the silence for the lost. Watches it again at "Callen Rook." Her face does not move. Her free hand curls into the apron in a way that suggests she is holding something other than fabric.
- Plan: Return to the Brass Anchor, set Piper to follow Vault, perform business-as-usual.

**FATHER IUSTIN BERN**
- Location: Halfway up the Customs House Steps in his black cassock, wooden cross in hand.
- Action: Performs the festival blessing immediately before the toast. His voice cracks on the line about "souls upon the sea who shall not be forgotten." When Vault names "Callen Rook," Iustin's eyes close for a moment and his fingers tighten on the cross. He does not look at Vault. He looks at the customs house door.
- Plan: Return to the chapel, fetch the parish register, prepare himself for what he intends to do at 11 PM. Write a short letter to be left on his desk.

**CAPTAIN ALARIC SOREN**
- Location: Back edge of the crowd just in front of the customs house steps. In plain dark clothes. Has come ashore from the *Vespertine* alone.
- Action: Listens to the toast without expression. Watches Vault. Watches Father Iustin watching Vault. Notes Mother Gasca's posture. Files Jay (a stranger he doesn't recognise) as "to be identified later." Notes Ophir and Sable's arrival without turning his head. Does not drink the offered wine.
- Plan: Walk the length of the quay alone, mapping foot-traffic between the customs house and the water. Send word to Keddie that the auction is "go."

**TOMÁS ELÍRA**
- Location: The Cell beneath the Customs House.
- Action: Cannot hear the toast directly but hears the crowd's cheers as a muffled wash through stone. Hears his own name when it is shouted. Sits with his back against the cell wall and listens to people celebrate his death. Has done his weeping. Picks at a scratch in the cell door with a fingernail.
- Plan: Wait. Listen for footsteps on the stair.

**SABLE LARK**
- Location: Lower edge of the crowd on the Quay, between the Customs House Steps and the Brass Anchor. Feathered hat held under one arm in mock respect.
- Action: Listens to half the toast. Spends the other half scanning the rooftops, the alley mouths, the *Vespertine*'s gangplank. Stands six feet from Ophir at all times.
- Watching for: Anyone showing too much interest in Ophir.
- Plan: Escort Ophir to the Brass Anchor, take a position with sight-lines to both doors.

**OPHIR LUNE**
- Location: Edge of the crowd, just landward of Sable. Charcoal-grey coat that looks plain until you notice the silk.
- Action: Listens to the toast politely. Smiles at the bawdy fishwife joke. Eyes flick once to the customs house windows. Touches the small inner pocket of his coat where a folded banker's letter waits.
- Plan: Withdraw to his private booth at the Brass Anchor. Wait for "Keddie." Order something light he won't actually eat.

**KEDDIE WREN**
- Location: The Quay, near the back of the crowd, half-hidden behind a fish-stall.
- Action: Came down from the Tobacco Loft to be seen at the toast. Sweating. Picks at a hangnail. Leaves before the toast finishes. Hurries back along the quay toward the warehouse. Piper notices.
- Plan: Finish auction setup at the Tobacco Loft. Light the loft lanterns. Check that the watchman is sufficiently drunk. Wait for Soren's word.

**NETTIE SHARROWS**
- Location: Her stall in the Fish Market Arcade. Has not come to the toast.
- Action: Lays out her cards by the light of a single tallow candle. Has had no customers yet. Hears the cheer from the steps and watches the crowd. Waiting for the woman in the sober coat from her vision. Becoming anxious that she will miss her.
- Plan: Wait at the stall. If the woman in the sober coat passes within reach, call out to her. If not, leave the stall and walk the quay looking for her.

**CALLUM HEFT**
- Location: Foot of the *Halcyon Lass* gangplank.
- Action: Did not attend the toast — never does. Drinks watered wine from a tin cup, gives the dock-hands their orders, marks the manifest in a small ledger. Noted Soren coming ashore alone earlier and filed it as "interesting." Has the recognition phrase from his courier-employer rehearsed in his head.
- Plan: Continue loading. Send a hand to the Brass Anchor for beer. Note who comes and goes from each ship.

**AGATHE BREL**
- Location: Edge of the crowd at the Customs House Steps, on the right side.
- Action: Watches Vault throughout the toast with the polite stillness of an old woman watching her grandchildren. When he names "Callen Rook" her shawl tightens at the shoulders by a fraction — to anyone watching her, she seems briefly cold. She is *almost* sure now. She wants one more confirmation.
- Plan: Return to her charm-stall in the Fish Market Arcade. Wait. If Vault enters the alleys behind the customs house at any point, follow.

**PIPER COLM**
- Location: Weaving through the crowd at the Customs House Steps with a tray of festival cups.
- Action: Partly real (he is delivering cups) and partly cover (Mother Gasca told him to be near the toast and watch where Vault looks). Fourteen, tray heavy, cheeks flushed with importance. Sees Keddie leave the crowd early and notes it. Sees Father Iustin's hand tighten on the cross and notes it without understanding it.
- Plan: Report to Mother Gasca at the bar. Receive his next instruction.

**VICK & HOLLAND (red-coats)**
- Location: Flanking the Customs House door at the top of the steps.
- Action: Stand at attention through the toast. **Vick** straight-backed, eyes on the crowd, uncomfortable in the formal posture. **Holland** has had two cups of festival wine already and a third tucked behind a stone urn at his feet. Both nod to Vault when addressed.
- Plan: Maintain post. Each accepts a half-hour relief in turn to eat at the Brass Anchor. Holland will stretch his. Vick will not.

**ROBIN KAFA**
- Location: A corner table in the Brass Anchor, by the fiddler.
- Action: Four cups deep in cheap brandy. Has been here since two in the afternoon. Has, in the last hour, started humming a sailor's lament he hasn't sung aloud in eight years. Mother Gasca's barmaid has been quietly diluting his brandy. He hears the cheer from the steps and the name "Callen Rook" carried on the wind, and he flinches as if struck. Does not stand. Refills his own cup.
- Plan: Drink. If the brandy keeps coming, drink more.

## ROUND 2 — "THE CAST SCATTERS" (6:30 – 7:00 PM)
### Context
The sunset toast is over. The festival is fully under way. The single moment when the whole cast was in one place has passed. Plots begin to diverge. Jay has ended Round 1 alone on the customs house steps with an untouched cup, having just been weighed by the Harbourmaster.

Dead-end check on every character below. None stymied. All have viable paths.

**Agathe Brel's state has shifted.** She now has TWO reasons to stay in Saint-Mallon tonight: her original plan to kill Vault, and a new uncertainty about Jehanne Cordelier. Both of her goals now want information before action. This is a significant diary update.

---

**JAY CORDELIER (PLAYER)**
- Location at start: Customs House Steps, alone with her untouched cup.
- Action: Determined by player.

**HARBOURMASTER ESTEBAN VAULT**
- Location: Inside the Customs House, main floor.
- Action: Enters his own office. Closes the door behind him. Stands for a moment with his back to it. Pours himself a proper glass of the good wine from the sideboard (not the festival swill he was drinking outside) and drinks half of it standing. The slashed portrait of the *Sable Pernoux*'s old captain is on the wall opposite. He does not look at it. He goes to his desk, opens the middle drawer, takes out a folded paper copy of the *Sable Pernoux* file — his own private version, not the one in the harbour ledgers — and reads a specific line from it. Then he puts it back. He goes to the door, opens it, and calls Vick inside in a quiet voice. He tells Vick — straight-backed, uncomfortable Vick, NOT the older drunker Holland — that the prisoner is not to be admitted to ANY visitor tonight without Vault himself being sent for first, "not even His Grace the Bishop if he comes." He adds, casually: "and the clerk-woman, the southern one, in the sober coat — if she comes back to the customs house door at any time tonight, you send for me immediately and you do not let her inside. Even if she has papers." Vick nods, uneasy. Vault dismisses him.
- Then: Vault descends the short stair to the cell briefly. Does not speak to Tomás. Looks at him through the grille. Tomás sees him. Vault looks at him for a long moment and then turns and climbs back up. Locks the cell-house stair door behind him.
- Returns to the steps, his public face fully restored, and rejoins the festival.
- Plan for next round: Spend the mid-evening visibly on the quay among the gentry and the captains. Make a point of being seen. Check on the warehouse watchman ("one of my informal men") around 9 PM. Be seen drinking in the Brass Anchor briefly. Return to the customs house at 11 PM for his traditional "midnight round" — which tonight will be a proper visit to the cell.

**MOTHER GASCA**
- Location: Arriving back at the Brass Anchor. Goes behind the bar.
- Action: Exchanges a quick word with her senior barmaid. Takes the bar. Surveys the room: notes Robin Kafa still at his corner table, now singing quietly to himself between cups; notes Ophir Lune in the private booth at the back with Sable Lark seated at a small table a comfortable sight-line away. Pulls beer for the rush of festival-goers now pouring in. As Piper comes into the taproom she catches his eye and tilts her head — their private "come behind the bar" signal. Takes Piper into the small pantry off the bar. Piper tells her what he saw: Keddie leaving the toast early; the priest's hand on the cross; the stillness of the tall foreign man at the back of the crowd; and — *most important* — a stranger, young, woman, sober coat, insurance-clerk bearing, was standing on the customs house steps talking to Vault herself at the end of the toast; and earlier she had shaken hands with old Agathe from the charm stall on the right side of the crowd. Gasca listens without interrupting. Files all of it. Slips Piper a copper coin and sends him back out with instructions: go to the upper windows of the lower town alleys behind the customs house and watch who goes in and out of the back servant's door and the alley between 10 PM and midnight. A child's job, but an important one.
- Plan for next round: Work the bar through the evening rush. At around 9 PM she will take her usual hour's break in her private room above the tavern — that's when she will slip down into the cellar and prepare her packing for the *Halcyon Lass*. Meets Heft at 2 AM at the gangplank.

**FATHER IUSTIN BERN**
- Location: Ascending the alley from the quay to the Chapel of Saint Mallon.
- Action: Arrives at the chapel in the dark. Lights a single lamp in his upper room. Kneels at the small prie-dieu for ten minutes, silent. Rises. Goes to his desk. Opens the parish register (kept in the vestry cupboard, brought up to his room for what he must do tonight). Turns to the entries from eight years ago. Reads the notation for the *Sable Pernoux*. Reads it again. Begins, in his small careful hand, to write a letter — not yet the letter he will leave behind if he dies tonight, but a preamble to it, gathering his thoughts. He will take a small supper of bread and watered wine. He does not yet leave for the customs house. He has decided he will go at 11 PM as planned, not earlier.
- He does NOT yet know Jay exists.
- Plan for next round: Continue writing. Pray. Eat. Prepare.

**CAPTAIN ALARIC SOREN**
- Location: Walking west along the quay. Reaches the gangplank of the *Vespertine*.
- Action: Goes aboard his own ship briefly. Speaks to his first mate (name: Verin) on the quarterdeck. Orders a small signal: a single lamp to be hung at the stern rail the moment the auction is confirmed running. Then descends back to the quay and walks to Keddie's warehouse. Does NOT go inside. Stops in the street, looks up at the shuttered upper window, sees the lamplight brighter than when Jay walked past (because Keddie has now lit both lamps), and makes a small gesture Verin can see from the *Vespertine*'s deck. The signal lamp goes up. Soren then walks unhurriedly back to the *Vespertine*, goes aboard, and disappears into the great cabin. Will not be visible again until he moves on the auction.
- Incidentally: Soren passed the Customs House Steps coming back along the quay and saw — but did not approach — the young woman in the sober coat standing alone on the steps. He files her. He does not approach yet.
- Plan for next round: Remain in the great cabin preparing. Has two of his crew circulate quietly along the quay in plain clothes to identify who visits Keddie's warehouse between now and 10 PM.

**TOMÁS ELÍRA**
- Location: The Cell beneath the Customs House.
- Action: Sees Vault come down the stair. Stands against the back wall of the cell as Vault appears at the grille. They look at each other for a long moment. Neither speaks. Tomás understands what the visit means — that Vault has come personally to confirm his existence without confirming anything with words. Vault goes. Tomás lowers himself back to the floor against the wall. Picks at the scratch in the cell door. A while later — alerted by the crowd-sound dying down slightly — Tomás stands again. He has begun to believe that no one will come in time. He considers the fallback. Has not decided yet.
- Plan for next round: Wait. Listen for any footsteps at all.

**SABLE LARK**
- Location: The Brass Anchor. Small table with a clear sight-line to Ophir's private booth.
- Action: Nurses one cup of wine slowly. Watches the room. Ignores the three different drunks who approach her trying to buy her a drink or pick a fight; dispatches each with a single look and a quiet word. Notes with mild professional disappointment the Brass Anchor's security arrangements (one back door unguarded, the cellar trap visible from her table, the window behind Ophir's booth shuttered but only latched). Mentally rehearses escape routes. Observes Robin Kafa singing quietly at his corner table and recognises, with a slight tightening around the eyes, a face she last saw ten years ago aboard a privateer called the *Wren Gaol*. Does not approach him. Does not acknowledge.
- Plan for next round: Continue to watch. Will move to escort Ophir personally at 10:30 PM when he is due to leave for the Tobacco Loft.

**OPHIR LUNE**
- Location: The Brass Anchor — private booth at the back.
- Action: Accepts a plate of festival bread and cheese he will not eat. Drinks a single small cup of light wine. Stares at the grain of the wooden table. Goes over, mentally, the careful language he will use at the auction — he has rehearsed this twice already but wants to do it a third time. Touches the inner pocket where the banker's letter is. Breathes slowly. Waits for Keddie's contact-man to find him here — which Keddie has told him to expect at some point before 9 PM.
- Plan for next round: Continue to wait. Accept any message from Keddie. Will leave with Sable at 10:30 PM for the Tobacco Loft.

**KEDDIE WREN**
- Location: The upper loft of his warehouse. Both lamps lit. Three chairs arranged in a loose semi-circle facing a small draped table on which sits a wooden box (empty at present — it will hold the "item" during the auction, which Soren has told him will be delivered at the last minute). A sheet of paper with bid-tally lines drawn on it. A jug of better wine and three glasses.
- Action: Fusses with the arrangement. Moves a chair. Moves it back. Sees Soren's signal through the shutter-gap below — the *Vespertine*'s stern lantern going up. Feels a small lurch of relief and fear mixed. Pours himself a glass of the better wine and drinks it too fast. Sits down in one of the three chairs. Stands up again. Goes to the shutter and looks out at the quay below — sees Jay walking back toward the Customs House Steps and files her as "a face at the toast, probably nobody, probably not a bidder" but is not sure. Starts to get properly frightened. Considers for a moment — just a moment — going down and running to the Brass Anchor to find Mother Gasca and tell her what he knows about Vault's chests in exchange for safe passage on the *Halcyon Lass*. He does not act on it. Yet.
- Plan for next round: Wait for Ophir. Run the auction. Survive.

**NETTIE SHARROWS**
- Location: Her stall in the Fish Market Arcade, second row in, about six stalls away from Agathe's.
- Action: Has been increasingly frustrated that the woman in the sober coat has not walked past her stall. Decides her stall is not the right place. Gathers up her cards and her small tin of coppers, wraps the cards in a blue silk handkerchief, tucks them into the deep pocket of her coat. Leaves her candle burning as a signal that she is "out for a moment, back soon" — this is a fish-market convention. Walks down to the quay. Emerges from the arcade near the middle of the Lantern Walk, between the Customs House Steps and the Brass Anchor. Stands there with her hands in her pockets looking toward the customs house for a while. Then begins walking slowly westward along the quay, scanning faces. She is looking for the woman in the sober coat.
- Plan for next round: Keep walking. If she sees Jay, approach her.

**CALLUM HEFT**
- Location: Foot of the *Halcyon Lass* gangplank.
- Action: Supervises the last festival-day cargo going aboard — five more crates, a cask of salt pork, a small roll of sailcloth. Sends a dock-hand named Perry to fetch a pitcher of beer from the Brass Anchor. Notes — with the sideways attention of a man who watches the quay for a living — the tall foreigner (Soren) boarding the *Vespertine*, then descending to the quay, then boarding again. Files it as "unusual." Notes the signal lamp going up on the *Vespertine*'s stern. Files that too. Makes a small mark in his ledger — not the cargo ledger, his personal one. Does not know what it means.
- Plan for next round: Continue loading. Take a meal at the Brass Anchor between 8 and 9 PM (one of his rotation shifts).

**AGATHE BREL**
- Location: Fish Market Arcade, second stall from the landward end.
- Action: Has arrived at her stall but has NOT sat down. Stands at the stall's mouth, watching the Customs House Steps in the distance. Sees Jay finish her conversation with Vault, sees Vault go inside the customs house, sees Jay alone on the steps. Does not move. Continues to watch.
- Internal (not in ledger): she is deciding what to do. The Cordelier girl is an unknown variable. Killing Vault was her original plan; it remains her plan if the girl is irrelevant. But if the girl is a *Cordelier who wants what Agathe wants* she is a potential ally, and allies are rarer and more useful than revenge. If the girl is a Cordelier who has been captured by Vault's charm, she is an enemy. Agathe cannot tell which from this distance. She decides, provisionally, that she will let the girl come to her. The invitation to the stall was the first lure. If the girl does not come by 9 PM, Agathe will seek her out. In the meantime she will sit at her stall and perform her ordinary business.
- Finally sits down. Lays out her charms in their little rows. Waits for customers.
- Plan for next round: Wait. If Jay comes to the arcade, engage carefully. Otherwise, by 9 PM, leave the stall under the pretext of closing up and go looking for her at the Brass Anchor. Still has the knife in her shawl. If Vault walks into the alley behind the customs house alone tonight, that still overrides everything.

**PIPER COLM**
- Location: Brass Anchor → taken into the pantry by Mother Gasca → sent back out with new instructions.
- Action: Reported to Gasca what he saw at the toast (including the Jay-Agathe handshake and the Jay-Vault conversation). Received new instructions and a copper. Leaves the Brass Anchor by the back door. Climbs up through the alleys to a spot he knows — a flat roof above a fish-warehouse that looks down onto the back of the customs house. Settles in with a crust of bread and a quarter-apple he has pocketed. Begins his watch. Already bored within five minutes, then bullied himself back to attention. Will watch until midnight.
- Plan for next round: Watch. Report back if anyone uses the customs house servant's door or the alley.

**VICK & HOLLAND (red-coats)**
- Location: Customs house door.
- Action: Vick called inside briefly by Vault. Given his orders re: prisoner access and re: Jay. Comes back out uncomfortable, straighter than before, avoids meeting Holland's eye. Holland does not ask why — he is halfway through his third cup and is not asking questions tonight. The two of them flank the door. Vick watches the quay with renewed attention, scanning for a sober coat in particular.
- Plan for next round: Maintain post. Each takes a half-hour relief in turn at the Brass Anchor. Holland will go first. Holland will stretch his half-hour.

**ROBIN KAFA**
- Location: Corner table, Brass Anchor, by the fiddler.
- Action: Singing quietly to himself now — the same sailor's lament he has been humming for an hour, but with actual words surfacing intermittently. The words are in a dialect most of the tavern does not understand, but Mother Gasca does, and she has half an ear on him while she pulls beer. The lament is about a ship whose name is not the *Sable Pernoux* but whose circumstances are suspiciously similar — a ship that "sailed with a cargo the crew did not know" and whose bosun "took a coin and did not come home." Robin does not know he is singing this audibly. He refills his own cup. Tips the last of the brandy in. Asks for another. Gasca's barmaid, on Gasca's nod, brings him another diluted one. He does not notice. His eyes are wet.
- Sable Lark, across the room, is not looking at him. But her chair has been turned a quarter to the left of where it was before, which puts Robin in her peripheral vision.
- Plan for next round: Drink. Sing. If anyone sits across from him — particularly anyone who speaks the dialect of the lament — talk.

## ROUND 3 — "THE EVENING SETS" (7:00 – 7:30 PM)
### Context
The festival is fully into its working evening. The cast is now properly scattered across the quay. Plots are advancing in parallel. Several characters are reaching small inflection points this round — Robin is on the edge of speaking; Iustin is preparing his move; Vault is performing his public rounds while waiting for 11 PM; Keddie is alone with his fear; Soren's crew is now circulating in plain clothes.

Dead-end check on every character. None stymied.

---

**JAY CORDELIER (PLAYER)**
- Location at start: Foot of the Customs House Steps, having just finished her exchange with Vick.
- Action: Determined by player.

**HARBOURMASTER ESTEBAN VAULT**
- Location: The Quay, currently at one of the long festival tables near the centre. Has rejoined the public night.
- Action: Performing the host. Visible. Has settled at a table with three captains and a wealthy merchant — Linhardt is among them. Drinking the proper wine now (a private bottle), holding a small court. Has been told by his pot-boy runner (a different boy from Piper) that "the southern clerk woman went into the Anchor and then back to the customs house steps." Vault has heard this and his face did not move; he raised a small toast to the captain telling a story, and then asked for another bottle. Has decided to send a different lad to circle past the customs house steps every fifteen minutes for the next two hours and report. He intends to cross the quay to "casually take the air" near the customs house himself between Round 4 and Round 5 — partly to check Vick has held the line, partly to make sure his own face is the next face Jay sees there.
- Plan for next round: Continue to hold court. Have the runner check on the customs house. Drift toward the customs house at the start of Round 4 with a small piece of theatre about "checking the night's accounts."

**MOTHER GASCA**
- Location: Behind the bar of the Brass Anchor. The festival rush is at its peak.
- Action: Pulls beer two-handed without conscious effort. Mind is elsewhere. The southern clerk's surname has unlocked the small private room she did not know she was holding — the night, eight years ago, when Martin Cordelier sat at this same bar and told her about the wrongness on the *Sable Pernoux*. She remembers his face. She can see his sister's resemblance in retrospect now, which means it was there the whole time. She is calculating quickly: this complicates things. A Cordelier in town the night of the hanging means Vault may be more on edge than he has shown — and a more on-edge Vault is more dangerous. She decides her plan stands but the *timing* must move up. She will try to take the chest from the cell at 10 PM rather than midnight, while the festival is still loud enough to cover noise. She also decides she wants to *talk to Jay* before she moves — not to confess anything, but to take her measure properly.
