# GAME BIBLE — "THE LAST DIVIDEND"

## An Asymmetric Deduction RPG — 15 Characters, 1 Night, 1 Space Station

---

## 1. SETTING

### The Station
**Erebus Platform** — a corporate deep-space station operated by **Helios Consolidated**, the solar system's largest energy conglomerate. Erebus sits in the outer belt, officially a fuel-processing hub, but its real purpose is more layered than that. The station is mid-sized: 200 crew normally, but tonight most have been given shore leave for the occasion. Only essential personnel and the invited guests remain — roughly 40 people total, of whom 15 are our cast.

### The Occasion
**The Succession Gala.** Helios Consolidated's founding CEO, **Director Cassius Wren**, is stepping down tonight. He has called a formal gathering aboard Erebus — his flagship station — to announce his successor. The Board of Directors, key executives, investors, and a handful of hand-picked guests have been invited. The announcement is scheduled for 02:00 station time. The gala begins at 20:00. Departure shuttle leaves at 08:00.

### Time Frame
**20:00 to 08:00 — 12 hours.** Each round = approximately 1 hour. Target: 10 active rounds plus opening and endgame.

### Tone
Corporate noir. Polished surfaces, ruthless ambition, old debts, quiet violence. Think boardroom thriller meets locked-room mystery, set against the void of space.

### The Central Secret
**Project Lethe.** Buried in Erebus's restricted research wing is evidence of a program Helios ran 8 years ago: an experimental neural-conditioning technology designed to make workers more compliant and productive. It was tested on a population of miners at a Helios facility on Callisto. The tests caused irreversible neurological damage to over 300 people. Cassius Wren authorized it. The project was scrubbed from official records, the facility was decommissioned, and the survivors were relocated and paid off under NDA.

Tonight, Project Lethe's ghosts are converging on Erebus — some to bury the evidence forever, some to expose it, some to weaponize it, and some who don't yet know they're connected to it at all.

## 2. LOCATION MAP

### PUBLIC SPACES

**1. The Grand Atrium (Central Hub)**
- The main gathering space for the gala. High ceilings with a transparent dome showing the stars. A stage at one end for the succession announcement. Bar, seating areas, a string quartet (automated). This is where everyone begins.
- Environmental detail: A commemorative plaque on the east wall listing Helios's "founding principles." One of the bolts holding it is newer than the others — it's been removed and replaced.

**2. The Officers' Lounge**
- A quieter social space adjacent to the Atrium. Leather furniture, a private bar, viewscreens showing news feeds. Reserved for senior personnel and VIPs but accessible to anyone with a gala invitation.
- Environmental detail: A deactivated console terminal in the corner. It looks decorative but still has a functioning port behind the screen panel.

**3. The Observation Deck**
- Upper level, glass-walled, overlooking the station's exterior docking arms and the asteroid field. Beautiful and exposed — conversations here are visible from the Atrium balcony below.
- Environmental detail: Maintenance access panel behind a seating alcove leads to the exterior hull service crawlspace.

**4. The Mess Hall**
- Lower level, crew area. Basic but functional. Currently empty of regular crew (on shore leave). Has a kitchen, a medical supply cabinet (basic first aid), and a wall-mounted emergency comm unit.
- Environmental detail: Scratched into a table near the back: "THEY KNOW WHAT THEY DID TO US — C.M." Old, partially scrubbed.

### RESTRICTED SPACES

**5. Director Wren's Private Suite**
- Cassius's personal quarters aboard Erebus. Locked biometric + keycard. Contains his personal terminal (encrypted), a wall safe (combination lock), and personal effects.
- Access methods: (a) Wren's personal keycard, (b) Override code held by Chief of Security Harlow, (c) Engineering bypass through the maintenance shaft from Deck C, (d) Social engineering — convincing Wren to invite you in.
- Contents of interest: In the wall safe — a data chip containing the original Project Lethe authorization with Wren's signature. On his terminal — draft succession announcement (names his chosen successor). A handwritten letter in the desk drawer addressed to "the one I failed."

**6. Research Wing (Deck B-7)**
- The sealed section where Project Lethe's equipment was housed. Officially decommissioned and locked. Requires a Level 5 security clearance keycard OR manual override from Engineering.
- Access methods: (a) Level 5 keycard (Wren has one, Harlow has one, Dr. Okafor has one she shouldn't), (b) Manual override from the Engineering Bay, (c) Ventilation shaft from the Cargo Bay (physically demanding, requires tools).
- Contents of interest: Deactivated neural-conditioning equipment. A cracked specimen tank. Archived data cores in a locked cabinet (contain raw test data, subject names, outcomes). A second, hidden room behind a false wall panel — contains a functioning prototype of the Lethe device.

**7. The Server Room**
- Station's central data hub. Climate-controlled, restricted access. Contains Erebus's communication array controls and data archives.
- Access methods: (a) Engineering keycard, (b) Hacking the door panel (requires technical skill + time), (c) Maintenance crawlspace from Engineering Bay.
- Contents of interest: Communication logs showing encrypted transmissions between Erebus and an unknown receiver over the past 3 months. Station security footage archives. The ability to broadcast a station-wide or system-wide message.

**8. The Brig**
- Two holding cells and a security office. Currently "empty" — but Cell 2 has been recently occupied (bedding disturbed, food tray, a scratched tally on the wall).
- Access methods: (a) Security keycard (Harlow), (b) Wren's master keycard, (c) Forcing the lock (noisy, takes time).
- Contents of interest: In the security office — Harlow's personal log terminal. A locked weapons locker containing two stun pistols and a kinetic sidearm. Cell 2's recent occupant left behind a scrap of fabric caught in the door mechanism — it matches the uniform of Helios medical staff.

### TRANSITIONAL SPACES

**9. The Main Corridor (Spine)**
- The central artery connecting the Atrium to the lower decks. Well-lit, monitored by security cameras. Anyone moving through is visible on security feeds.
- Environmental detail: Camera 7 (near the Research Wing junction) has a 4-second blind spot during its rotation cycle.

**10. Service Tunnels (Deck C)**
- Maintenance passages running beneath the main decks. Poorly lit, no cameras. Connect Engineering Bay to the area near Wren's Suite and the Research Wing. Tight spaces — movement is slow but unobserved.
- Environmental detail: Tool storage alcove halfway through — contains maintenance tools (potential improvised weapons or lockpicking aids). A junction that leads to the exterior hull access (emergency only — hard vacuum beyond).

**11. The Docking Bay**
- Where shuttles dock. Currently holds the gala guests' arrival shuttle (locked, departure scheduled 08:00) and a small maintenance pod. The bay is pressurized but cold. 
- Environmental detail: The maintenance pod is technically functional but only has fuel for a short-range hop. Its launch would trigger station-wide alarms.

**12. Engineering Bay**
- The station's mechanical heart. Reactor controls, life support, gravity systems. Staffed by a skeleton crew (1 NPC engineer on duty). Contains tools, schematics, and the manual override panel for most restricted areas on the station.
- Environmental detail: A section of the reactor shielding has been jury-rigged with non-standard parts. Someone has been doing unauthorized modifications.

**13. Cargo Bay**
- Storage area on the lower deck. Crates of supplies, fuel cell containers, and personal luggage for the gala guests. Dimly lit, lots of cover.
- Environmental detail: One crate is marked with Helios Medical Division insignia and sealed with a tamper-evident lock. It arrived on a separate transport 6 hours before the gala guests.

## 3. CHARACTER ROSTER

### 1. CASSIUS WREN — The Retiring Director (HOST)
- **Cover story:** Beloved founder stepping down gracefully to hand the reins to a worthy successor.
- **Secret:** He authorized Project Lethe and has spent 8 years burying it. He's stepping down because someone has been sending him anonymous messages threatening exposure. He believes if he leaves power quietly, the threat will die. He's wrong.
- **Win condition:** Survive the night with Project Lethe still buried. Announce his successor. Depart on the morning shuttle with his legacy intact. (PROTECTION)
- **Abilities:** (1) *Master of the House* — can lock or unlock any door on the station once per game using his master override. (2) *The Old Man's Gravity* — when Cassius speaks publicly, all characters in the room must pause and listen. Creates social pressure that's hard to defy openly. (3) *Final Card* — Cassius has hidden a dead man's switch: a pre-programmed data burst that will transmit Project Lethe evidence to the press if he is killed. Only he knows the cancellation code.
- **Starting location:** Grand Atrium (hosting)
- **Key relationships:** Mentor to Yuki Tanaka (his chosen successor, though she doesn't know yet). Former collaborator with Dr. Okafor (who ran the Lethe experiments). Father figure to Harlow (who owes him everything). The anonymous threat is from someone at this gala.
- **Personality:** Warm, grandfatherly, effortlessly commanding. Speaks softly. Never raises his voice. The most dangerous person in the room because everyone underestimates how ruthless he can be when cornered.
- **Fallback plans:**
  - If succession announcement is disrupted: Delay announcement, claim he needs more time. Use the delay to identify and neutralize the disruptor.
  - If Lethe evidence is found: Activate Harlow to confiscate it. If Harlow fails, attempt to frame the evidence as fabricated by a corporate rival.
  - If directly threatened: Reveal the dead man's switch to his attacker. Negotiate.

### 2. YUKI TANAKA — The Chosen Successor
- **Cover story:** Helios VP of Operations, here as a senior executive attending the gala.
- **Secret:** She doesn't know she's been chosen as successor — but she suspects. She's also been secretly investigating irregularities in Helios's old Callisto mining records for the past year, following a data trail that she doesn't yet realize leads to Project Lethe. She's here to find answers.
- **Win condition:** Discover what Project Lethe was and decide what to do with the truth — expose it (even at cost to her career) or bury it (and accept the succession). The player must make this choice. (DISCOVERY → COMPLETION)
- **Abilities:** (1) *Corporate Access* — has legitimate Level 3 clearance, can access most non-restricted areas without suspicion and can request access to restricted areas through official channels. (2) *Analyst's Eye* — once per round, can study a document or data source and extract one hidden detail that casual reading would miss. (3) *Boardroom Presence* — NPCs with corporate loyalty find it difficult to refuse a direct professional request from Yuki. Doesn't work on characters with no stake in Helios.
- **Starting location:** Grand Atrium
- **Key relationships:** Protégée of Cassius (she respects him deeply, which makes the truth harder). Professional rival of Kwame Asante (both want the top job). Has met Dr. Okafor at conferences but doesn't know her real history.
- **Personality:** Precise, controlled, deeply ethical but pragmatic. Asks careful questions. Rarely shows emotion but feels things intensely. When she's working through a problem, she goes quiet.
- **This is the PLAYER'S ROLE.**

### 3. KWAME ASANTE — The Rival Executive
- **Cover story:** Helios CFO, the other leading candidate for succession. Here to ensure the transition goes smoothly.
- **Secret:** He already knows about Project Lethe — he discovered it 2 years ago while auditing old accounts. He's been using the knowledge to quietly blackmail Cassius into favorable decisions. He wants the succession for himself and will undermine Yuki's candidacy by any means necessary. He is the one sending anonymous threats to Cassius — not to expose Lethe, but to keep Cassius afraid and compliant.
- **Win condition:** Become Cassius's announced successor. If that fails, acquire the Lethe evidence to continue his leverage over whoever takes charge. (COMPLETION)
- **Abilities:** (1) *Follow the Money* — can trace any financial transaction or resource allocation on the station's systems. (2) *Silver Tongue* — once per round, can make a persuasive private argument that shifts an NPC's disposition one step in his favor. (3) *Contingency Investor* — has a hidden ally among the gala guests (Felicity Park) who will act on his signal.
- **Starting location:** Grand Atrium
- **Key relationships:** Rival of Yuki. Blackmailing Cassius. Secret alliance with Felicity Park. Despises Harlow (sees her as Cassius's attack dog).
- **Personality:** Charming, expansive, quick to laugh. The life of any party. Underneath: calculating and utterly amoral. He doesn't enjoy cruelty — he simply doesn't factor others' suffering into his decisions.
- **Fallback plans:**
  - If Yuki is announced as successor: Immediately pivot to acquiring Lethe evidence to blackmail Yuki instead.
  - If blackmail leverage is exposed: Attempt to frame it as "whistleblowing" — claim he was gathering evidence to expose Cassius, not exploit him.
  - If cornered: Has arranged for Felicity to create a diversion so he can access the Server Room and transmit damaging (unrelated) financial data about Yuki to the Board.

### 4. COMMANDER REN HARLOW — The Chief of Security
- **Cover story:** Station security chief ensuring the gala runs safely.
- **Secret:** She knows about Project Lethe because she helped Cassius cover it up 8 years ago. She was a junior officer then; Cassius promoted her in exchange for loyalty. She's haunted by it but has rationalized her silence as protecting the greater good (Helios employs millions; a scandal would destroy them). Tonight she's on edge — she knows threats are circling.
- **Win condition:** Protect Cassius through the night. Ensure no evidence of Project Lethe leaves the station. Maintain order. (PROTECTION)
- **Abilities:** (1) *Security Override* — can lock down any single area of the station for one round (no one in or out). Usable twice per game. (2) *Armed Response* — carries a stun pistol. Can incapacitate one character per round in a direct confrontation. Most characters cannot physically resist her. (3) *Eyes Everywhere* — can check security camera feeds from any terminal, seeing who passed through monitored corridors recently. Doesn't cover Service Tunnels or Cargo Bay.
- **Starting location:** Grand Atrium (on duty)
- **Key relationships:** Fiercely loyal to Cassius. Distrusts Kwame (instinct, no proof). Has authority over station crew including Milo Voss. Unaware that Dr. Okafor is aboard with a stolen keycard.
- **Personality:** Professional, terse, watchful. Stands near walls. Drinks water, not champagne. Speaks in short sentences. Underneath the composure: guilt, exhaustion, and a growing suspicion that tonight is when everything falls apart.
- **Fallback plans:**
  - If evidence is found by someone: Attempt to confiscate it under security authority. If refused, lock down the area.
  - If Cassius is directly threatened: Intervene with force. Will use the stun pistol.
  - If her own involvement in the cover-up is exposed: Deny, then if proof is undeniable, attempt to negotiate silence in exchange for cooperation.

### 5. DR. ADAEZE OKAFOR — The Architect
- **Cover story:** Retired Helios medical researcher, invited as an honored former employee.
- **Secret:** She designed and ran the Project Lethe experiments on Callisto. She was following orders but made choices that worsened outcomes — cutting safety protocols to meet deadlines. She has spent 8 years in self-imposed exile, drinking herself quiet. She's here tonight because she received a message: "I know what you did. Come to Erebus or the data goes public." She has a stolen Level 5 keycard (taken from a former colleague) and intends to destroy the archived data in the Research Wing before anyone else finds it.
- **Win condition:** Destroy all Project Lethe data in the Research Wing archives. Escape the station without being identified as the lead researcher. (COMPLETION)
- **Abilities:** (1) *Medical Expertise* — can sedate a character using supplies from the Mess Hall medical cabinet (requires preparation + opportunity). (2) *Lethe Knowledge* — understands the neural-conditioning equipment. Can activate, deactivate, or modify it. Others cannot. (3) *Guilt Fractures* — under sustained personal pressure (confrontation about her past), she will crack and reveal partial truths. This is a vulnerability, not an ability.
- **Starting location:** Officers' Lounge (avoiding the crowd)
- **Key relationships:** Former subordinate of Cassius. The person who actually harmed the Callisto miners. Has never met Soren Vex but he is hunting her specifically. The message that lured her here was sent by Kwame's ally Felicity Park (on Kwame's behalf, to flush her out).
- **Personality:** Haunted, precise, drinks too much. Speaks in medical terminology when nervous. Hands shake slightly. Still brilliant. Capable of shocking coldness when her survival is at stake.
- **Fallback plans:**
  - If Research Wing access is blocked: Attempt to access the Server Room instead and remotely purge the archived data cores.
  - If identified as the Lethe researcher: Attempt to negotiate — offer to testify against Cassius in exchange for protection.
  - If cornered physically: Use sedation if prepared. Otherwise, leverage her knowledge of the Lethe device as a bargaining chip ("I'm the only one who can safely dismantle it").

### 6. SOREN VEX — The Avenger
- **Cover story:** Independent journalist covering the succession for a major outlet.
- **Secret:** He's not a journalist. He's the son of a Callisto miner who was neurologically damaged by Project Lethe. His father can no longer recognize him. Soren has spent 3 years tracking the program's origins. He knows Lethe exists, he knows it happened on Callisto, and he knows someone at Helios authorized it — but he doesn't know who specifically. He's here to find the person responsible and kill them. **He has a concealed ceramic knife that won't trigger metal detectors.**
- **Win condition:** Identify the person who authorized Project Lethe AND execute them. Both parts are required. (DISCOVERY + COMPLETION — VIOLENT)
- **Abilities:** (1) *Investigative Instinct* — once per round, can ask the narrator one yes/no question about a character's connection to Callisto or Lethe (the answer is always truthful but minimal). (2) *Ceramic Blade* — carries a concealed weapon. Can attempt to kill one character if alone with them in a non-public space. (3) *Misdirection* — his journalist cover is extremely well-constructed. Casual investigation of his identity will confirm the cover. Only deep digging (accessing press guild databases or contacting his supposed outlet) will reveal cracks.
- **Starting location:** Grand Atrium
- **Key relationships:** Hunting Cassius and/or Okafor (doesn't yet know which one to target). Will initially investigate everyone connected to Helios leadership. May mistake Yuki (the player) for a target if she gets too close to Lethe information — or may see her as a potential ally.
- **Personality:** Controlled intensity. Asks probing questions disguised as professional curiosity. Warm smile that doesn't reach his eyes. When he finds his target, the warmth vanishes completely.
- **Fallback plans:**
  - If he identifies the target but can't get them alone: Attempt to engineer isolation by triggering a distraction (fire alarm, power cut via Engineering).
  - If his cover is blown: Drop the pretense and attempt to rally sympathetic characters to his cause — appeal to justice rather than vengeance.
  - If he's disarmed: Seek an alternative weapon from the Brig weapons locker or Engineering Bay tools.

### 7. ISLA CAINE — The Extractor
- **Cover story:** Helios Head of Human Resources, attending in an official capacity.
- **Secret:** She works for **Ouroboros**, a rival energy corporation. She was planted inside Helios 4 years ago as a deep-cover operative. Tonight is her extraction night — her mission is to steal the Project Lethe prototype from the Research Wing and deliver it to an Ouroboros handler who is also at the gala (Rook). The prototype is worth billions in military applications.
- **Win condition:** Acquire the Lethe prototype device from the hidden room in the Research Wing and deliver it to Rook before the morning shuttle. (COMPLETION)
- **Abilities:** (1) *Deep Cover* — her Helios credentials are genuine. She has Level 4 clearance and knows the station layout from personnel files. (2) *Social Camouflage* — can adopt the mannerisms and conversational style of whoever she's talking to, making her seem trustworthy and forgettable. Hard to read. (3) *Dead Drop* — can leave an item in a pre-arranged location for Rook to collect without direct contact.
- **Starting location:** Grand Atrium
- **Key relationships:** Secret operative paired with Rook (her handler, posing as wait staff). Knows of Dr. Okafor from intelligence files — Okafor is the only person who can operate the prototype, making her either an asset or a threat. Has no personal grudge against anyone; this is purely professional.
- **Personality:** Effortlessly likeable. Remembers everyone's name, asks about their families. The perfect HR persona — and none of it is real. Underneath: methodical, patient, and capable of extreme pragmatism.
- **Fallback plans:**
  - If she can't access the Research Wing: Attempt to recruit Dr. Okafor (by whatever means necessary) to retrieve the device.
  - If the prototype is destroyed or moved: Pivot to stealing the archived data cores instead — less valuable but still worth the extraction.
  - If Rook is compromised: Attempt independent extraction via the maintenance pod in the Docking Bay (risky — triggers alarms).

### 8. ROOK (DECLAN MARSH) — The Handler
- **Cover story:** Contract catering staff brought aboard for the gala.
- **Secret:** Ouroboros field handler. His job is to receive the Lethe prototype from Isla and ensure it leaves the station. He's the exit strategy. He has a concealed short-range signal jammer that can disrupt communications in a single room for 5 minutes — enough to cover a handoff or an escape.
- **Win condition:** Receive the Lethe prototype from Isla and smuggle it off-station (hidden in a catering supply crate that will be loaded onto the shuttle). If Isla fails, acquire it himself. (COMPLETION)
- **Abilities:** (1) *Service Invisibility* — as wait staff, he can move through most areas without arousing suspicion. NPCs tend to overlook him. (2) *Signal Jammer* — once per game, can disable all communications and cameras in one room for 5 minutes. (3) *Combat Training* — former military. Can physically contest Harlow and most other characters in a fight. **Carries no weapon but is dangerous unarmed.**
- **Starting location:** Grand Atrium (serving drinks)
- **Key relationships:** Paired with Isla Caine. Has no emotional connection to anyone on the station. Professional relationship with his Ouroboros controllers. Will prioritize the mission over Isla's safety if necessary.
- **Personality:** Quiet, efficient, watchful. Keeps his head down. When he speaks, it's brief and polite. His eyes are always cataloguing exits, obstacles, threats.
- **Fallback plans:**
  - If Isla is compromised: Attempt the Research Wing extraction himself, using the ventilation shaft from Cargo Bay.
  - If the prototype can't be acquired: Shift to data extraction — access the Server Room and download what he can.
  - If the mission is blown entirely: Ensure no evidence links back to Ouroboros. This may require silencing compromised assets — including Isla. (VIOLENT FALLBACK)

### 9. MILO VOSS — The Whistleblower
- **Cover story:** Junior station engineer on duty tonight.
- **Secret:** Milo found fragments of Project Lethe data while performing routine maintenance on the station's archived systems 3 months ago. He's been quietly copying evidence and has compiled a partial dossier. He contacted an investigative journalist (who he believes is Soren Vex's outlet) and arranged to hand over the evidence tonight. He's terrified — he knows Harlow monitors everything and that his unexplained time in the archives has already drawn attention.
- **Win condition:** Successfully transmit or deliver his Project Lethe dossier to someone who will make it public — a journalist, a regulator, or via system-wide broadcast. (COMPLETION)
- **Abilities:** (1) *Station Rat* — knows every shortcut, maintenance tunnel, and blind spot on Erebus. Can move through Service Tunnels quickly and access areas via unconventional routes. (2) *System Access* — has an engineering keycard granting access to the Server Room and Engineering Bay. Can perform technical tasks (overrides, data transfers, communications) faster than other characters. (3) *Jury-Rig* — can improvise technical solutions from available materials (e.g., create a data transmitter from spare parts, disable a lock mechanism).
- **Starting location:** Engineering Bay (on duty, will drift to the Atrium)
- **Key relationships:** Under Harlow's authority (she scares him). Knows Cassius only as the untouchable Director. Believes Soren is his journalist contact (Soren is not — the real contact never made it aboard). Has never met most of the gala guests.
- **Personality:** Nervous, young, idealistic. Talks too fast when anxious. Fidgets with tools. Brave in principle but physically shaky when danger is real. The kind of person who does the right thing and immediately regrets how frightening it is.
- **Fallback plans:**
  - If he can't reach Soren: Attempt a station-wide broadcast from the Server Room.
  - If the Server Room is locked down: Use his jury-rig ability to create a makeshift transmitter in Engineering Bay (slower, lower range — might only reach the shuttle's comm system).
  - If his dossier is confiscated: He memorized key details. He can reconstruct a partial version given time and access to a terminal.

### 10. FELICITY PARK — The Fixer
- **Cover story:** Independent corporate consultant and Helios board advisor.
- **Secret:** She's Kwame Asante's secret weapon. A professional fixer who specializes in making problems disappear — through legal manipulation, social engineering, and when necessary, more direct methods. She lured Dr. Okafor to Erebus on Kwame's orders (the anonymous message threatening exposure). Her job tonight: ensure Kwame gets the succession by neutralizing threats and, if needed, acquiring leverage against Yuki.
- **Win condition:** Ensure Kwame Asante is announced as Cassius's successor. Secondary: acquire compromising material on Yuki Tanaka. (COMPLETION)
- **Abilities:** (1) *Social Engineering* — can convincingly impersonate authority (legal counsel, board representative, regulatory agent) to extract information or compliance from NPCs. (2) *The Dossier* — has pre-prepared compromising information on 3 characters (Okafor's Lethe involvement, Harlow's cover-up role, Milo's unauthorized archive access). Can deploy this information strategically. (3) *Clean Hands* — expert at acting through others. When she manipulates events, the trail leads away from her and toward whoever she sets up.
- **Starting location:** Grand Atrium
- **Key relationships:** Secret alliance with Kwame. Lured Okafor to Erebus. Has dossiers on Harlow, Okafor, and Milo. Views Yuki as the primary target. Has no direct relationship with Rook or Isla but may notice their operational behavior (she's trained to spot professionals).
- **Personality:** Polished, warm, impeccably dressed. Laughs easily. Gives compliments that feel genuine. She is the most socially dangerous person at the gala — every interaction is tactical. Never visibly angry; setbacks are just new data.
- **Fallback plans:**
  - If Kwame's candidacy is torpedoed: Pivot to ensuring Yuki can't take the role either — create a power vacuum that benefits Kwame long-term.
  - If her connection to Kwame is exposed: Cut ties publicly, deny everything, frame Kwame as having deceived her too.
  - If directly threatened: She has no combat ability but will use her dossier information as a deterrent — mutually assured destruction through exposure.

### 11. THEO MARCHETTI — The Heir
- **Cover story:** Young tech entrepreneur and minority Helios shareholder, invited as a rising star in the industry.
- **Secret:** Theo is Cassius Wren's biological son. Cassius doesn't know — Theo's mother (a former Helios engineer, now deceased) never told Cassius, but she told Theo on her deathbed. Theo has DNA evidence proving the connection. He's not here for the succession — he's here to confront the father who never knew he existed and claim acknowledgment. But he's also discovered fragments of his mother's old work files that reference something called "Lethe," and he's beginning to suspect his mother was silenced, not just transferred.
- **Win condition:** Confront Cassius with proof of their relationship AND discover the truth about his mother's connection to Project Lethe. (DISCOVERY)
- **Abilities:** (1) *Digital Forensics* — can hack into personal terminals and extract data if given 10+ minutes of uninterrupted access. (2) *Mother's Legacy* — carries a data chip with his mother's partial work files. When combined with Research Wing archives, reveals that his mother was a Lethe engineer who tried to stop the experiments and was reassigned to silence her. (3) *Youth and Charm* — NPCs tend to underestimate him. He can ask naive-seeming questions that more guarded characters might actually answer.
- **Starting location:** Grand Atrium
- **Key relationships:** Cassius's unknown son. His mother worked under Dr. Okafor. Has no existing relationships with other guests — he's the outsider.
- **Personality:** Bright, restless, alternates between cocky tech-bro energy and moments of raw vulnerability. When he talks about his mother, the act drops. He's in over his head and knows it.
- **Fallback plans:**
  - If he can't get a private audience with Cassius: Write a message and have it delivered publicly — force the confrontation.
  - If Cassius denies him: The DNA evidence is irrefutable. Theo can threaten to go public.
  - If his mother's files lead nowhere: Seek out Dr. Okafor (if he learns of her connection) or access the Research Wing archives directly.

### 12. PRIA DASGUPTA — The Regulator
- **Cover story:** Interplanetary Commerce Commission auditor performing a routine compliance check on Erebus's operations. Boring, procedural, nothing to see here.
- **Secret:** The ICC has received an anonymous tip (from Milo's initial inquiries, routed through channels) about potential human rights violations connected to Helios deep-space operations. Pria is not here for a routine audit — she's an investigator with authority to seize evidence and issue detention orders. But her authority only applies if she can find actionable evidence. Without it, she's just an auditor with a clipboard.
- **Win condition:** Acquire sufficient evidence of Project Lethe to issue a formal detention order on Cassius Wren and impound Erebus's data archives. She needs physical or digital evidence + at least one corroborating witness. (DISCOVERY + COMPLETION)
- **Abilities:** (1) *Legal Authority* — if she has sufficient evidence, she can issue binding legal orders that even Harlow must obey (or defy openly, creating consequences). (2) *Forensic Audit* — can analyze financial records and data logs to trace hidden transactions, transfers, and project funding. (3) *Immunity* — harming or detaining a sitting ICC investigator is a federal crime that would bring catastrophic legal consequences to Helios. This makes her physically safer than most characters — but not invulnerable to someone with nothing to lose.
- **Starting location:** Officers' Lounge (reviewing documents)
- **Key relationships:** None initially — she's an outsider. Will naturally seek out anyone with information. Milo is her ideal witness but neither knows the other's significance. Harlow views her as a bureaucratic nuisance (for now).
- **Personality:** Patient, methodical, dry humor. Doesn't rush. Asks the same question three different ways. Infuriatingly calm under pressure. She's seen every corporate evasion tactic in the book and none of them impress her.
- **Fallback plans:**
  - If evidence is destroyed: Focus on witness testimony — find someone willing to make a sworn statement.
  - If she's physically isolated (locked down): Her absence will be noticed by the ICC. She has a check-in protocol — failure to check in by 04:00 triggers an automatic investigation.
  - If she can't build a full case tonight: Settle for enough to launch a formal investigation — lower bar, but Helios survives.

### 13. JUNO — The Ghost
- **Cover story:** None. Juno is not on the guest list.
- **Secret:** Juno is a surviving Callisto miner — one of the 300+ people neurologically damaged by Project Lethe. Unlike most survivors, her damage was partial: she experiences episodes of dissociation and memory fragmentation but retains most cognitive function. **Trigger:** Someone discovers that Cell 2 in the Brig was recently occupied.
**Consequences:**
- Harlow becomes more aggressive in monitoring movement (she knows who was there — a detained Callisto survivor she was "holding for processing")

**Trigger:** Anyone checking security feeds notices the 4-second blind spot on Camera 7, OR someone is caught on camera near the Research Wing.
**Consequences:**
- Harlow investigates personally
- Characters with Research Wing goals become more cautious or more desperate
- The blind spot becomes a known resource — multiple characters may try to exploit it simultaneously

- Characters in the Cargo Bay area may encounter Juno directly

- Soren may accelerate his kill timeline
- Isla and Rook's mission becomes more urgent (the prototype's value just skyrocketed)

## 7. ACCELERATION EVENT: THE BLACKOUT

**Timing:** Narrator-triggered, approximately Round 6-7 (around midnight).

**What happens:** The station experiences a sudden, brief power failure — lights die, gravity fluctuates for 2 seconds, emergency lighting kicks in. It lasts 3 minutes. During the blackout:

- All electronic locks reset to default (locked)
- Security cameras go offline and take 10 minutes to fully reboot
- Characters in the dark may collide, overhear things, or lose track of where others went
- When lights return, at least one character is not where they were before
- Something is discovered in the aftermath (narrator chooses based on game state): a door left open that shouldn't be, an item missing, or a character injured

**The blackout was caused by:** Juno, testing her reactor modifications. It's a warning shot — she's preparing her nuclear option.

**Narrative purpose:** Forces partial disclosure (characters must explain where they were and what they were doing), creates chaos that multiple factions exploit, resets the map (characters scatter and regroup), and raises stakes dramatically.

## 8. GAME MECHANICS

### Time Structure
- 20:00 to 08:00 = 12 hours of game time
- Each round ≈ 1 hour (compressed or expanded based on narrative density)
- Target: 10 active rounds
- Announcement at 02:00 (Round 8) is the narrative climax; Rounds 9-10 are aftermath/resolution

### Actions Per Round
- MOVE to an adjacent/connected location + ONE significant action + incidental interactions
- Significant: use an ability, search a room, deep conversation, hack a terminal, perform a task
- Incidental: brief exchanges, observation — no action cost

### Violence Rules
- Killing requires: weapon/method + target alone or incapacitated + non-public space
- 3 characters have lethal capability: Soren (knife), Juno (plasma cutter), August (poison)
- Rook is dangerous in hand-to-hand but has no weapon
- Harlow can stun (non-lethal) and is the primary counter to violence
- Any killing in a monitored area is caught on camera (unless cameras are down)
- Violence creates cascading consequences: investigation, lockdown, panic

### Hard Difficulty Modifiers
- NPCs advance their plans aggressively — they don't wait for the player
- Key items and locations may be claimed by NPCs before the player reaches them
- Felicity will actively work to undermine Yuki from Round 2 onward
- The blackout occurs earlier if the narrator senses player comfort
- NPCs form alliances quickly and may lock the player out of information networks
- At least 2 NPC plotlines will resolve without player involvement by the midgame
