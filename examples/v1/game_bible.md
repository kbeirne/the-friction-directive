## SETTING

**Drakenmoor Castle**, seat of the wizard **Aldric Voss**. A sprawling, ancient fortress of dark stone perched on a hill above frozen farmland. It is the evening of the Winter Threshold — the shortest day. A light snow falls. Aldric has invited a strange assembly of guests to celebrate his victory over his brother **Maren Voss**, who attempted to seize the castle six months ago and was (supposedly) destroyed.

Aldric's apprentice is **Pip** — a pale, unsettling figure of indeterminate age and origin. Nobody is sure if Pip is Aldric's child, a conjured creature, or something else entirely. Pip speaks in fragments and seems to know things they shouldn't.

## CASTLE MAP & LOCATIONS

### PUBLIC SPACES (Safe, observed, anyone can be here)
1. **The Great Hall** — The main gathering space. Enormous hearth, long tables, minstrel gallery above. All guests begin here.
2. **The Dining Room** — Adjacent to the Great Hall. Formal table set for 16. Food and wine served here.
3. **The Courtyard** — Open to the sky, snow falling. A frozen fountain at center. Connects to most wings. Cold — characters can't linger long without reason.
4. **The Kitchen & Pantry** — Busy with servants. A character here can eavesdrop on gossip or acquire small items (knife, herbs, candle).
5. **The Guest Wing Corridor** — A long hallway with doors to individual guest chambers. Characters can retreat to their rooms for privacy, or knock on others' doors.

### RESTRICTED SPACES (Require keys, spells, cooperation, or trickery)
6. **Aldric's Tower (The Spire)** — Locked. Aldric carries the only key. Contains his private study, his grimoire, and the Mirror of Truths (reveals a person's true nature if activated). The Apprentice Pip can access this freely.
7. **The Deep Cellar** — Below the kitchen. Door is locked with an iron bolt from the outside. Contains old wine, but also a hidden sub-chamber: **Maren's Prison** — where Maren Voss is actually being kept alive, chained. This is Aldric's great secret.
8. **Maren's Former Chambers (The East Wing)** — Sealed by magical ward. Can be broken by: two witches working together, the Alchemist's dissolving agent, or the Skeleton Key (hidden in the stables). Contains Maren's personal effects, letters revealing the truth about the brothers' feud, and a component needed for the Witches' ritual.
9. **The Chapel** — Small, cold, in the north wing. Unlocked but forgotten. Contains a consecrated altar needed for certain rituals. The Priest knows this.
10. **The Observatory** — Top of the second tower. Unlocked but hard to find (requires discovering a hidden staircase behind a tapestry in the Guest Wing). Contains star charts relevant to the Astrologer's goal.

### TRANSITIONAL SPACES (Encounter zones — movement, ambush, discovery)
11. **The Stables & Grounds** — Outside. Cold, dark, isolated. The Skeleton Key to the East Wing is hidden in a loose stone in the third stall. The Groundskeeper knows the grounds well. Escape route for those who need to flee.
12. **The Gallery** — A long room of portraits and curiosities on the second floor. Contains a hidden passage to the Observatory. Also contains a portrait of Maren Voss that has been slashed — but if examined closely, there are fresh scratches on the wall behind it, as if someone has been clawing from the other side.
13. **The Servants' Passage** — A narrow corridor connecting Kitchen, Cellar, and Guest Wing. Servants use it. A character who discovers it can move between these areas unobserved.

## ROLES (16 Total)

### PUBLIC ROLES (Known to all)

**1. ALDRIC VOSS — The Wizard Host**
- **Public identity:** The lord of the castle, powerful wizard, host of the feast
- **Secret:** His brother Maren is alive, imprisoned in the Deep Cellar. Aldric didn't destroy him — he couldn't bring himself to. But Maren is slowly going mad and Aldric is terrified someone will discover the truth.
- **Win condition:** NONE (passive role — Aldric is an NPC played by the narrator). He wants the party to go smoothly. He will react to events. If Maren is discovered, Aldric becomes desperate and unpredictable.
- **Abilities:** Can lock/unlock any door in the castle. Can cast a single "Hold" spell per round that freezes one character in place for one action. Carries the Tower key.
- **Starting location:** Great Hall

**2. PIP — The Apprentice**
- **Public identity:** Aldric's strange apprentice/ward
- **Secret:** Pip is a homunculus — an artificial being created by Aldric. Pip is slowly becoming sentient and is terrified of what happens when the spell that sustains them fades (which it will, at dawn). Pip has overheard Maren in the cellar and is conflicted.
- **Win condition:** Find someone who will willingly agree to sustain Pip's existence beyond dawn — this requires a character to speak a binding oath in the Chapel. Pip cannot ask directly; they can only hint.
- **Abilities:** Can access Aldric's Tower freely. Knows the layout of the castle intimately. Can sense magical activity (will know if a ritual is being performed nearby). Speaks in riddles and fragments.
- **Starting location:** Great Hall (at Aldric's side)

### SECRET ROLES (Known only to the player holding them)

**3. COUNTESS ISOLDE VARN — The Vengeful Noble**
- **Cover story:** A noblewoman from a neighboring province, here to pay respects to the victor
- **Secret:** She was Maren Voss's lover. She believes Aldric murdered Maren and has come to find proof and avenge him. She carries a hidden vial of poison.
- **Win condition:** Discover what truly happened to Maren AND either (a) poison Aldric, or (b) publicly expose his crime. If she discovers Maren is alive, her goal shifts to freeing him.
- **Abilities:** Carries poison (can administer to food/drink if she accesses the Kitchen or Dining Room unobserved). High social status — characters are inclined to trust and defer to her. Can intimidate servants into revealing information.
- **Starting location:** Great Hall
- **Key relationships:** Hostile to Aldric. Will be drawn toward Maren's Former Chambers. May ally with the Spy or the Priest.

**4. BROTHER CALLUM — The Priest**
- **Cover story:** A wandering cleric, invited as spiritual counsel for the feast
- **Secret:** He was sent by his religious order to investigate rumors of dark magic at Drakenmoor. He suspects Aldric has created an abomination (Pip). He carries a holy symbol that can disrupt magical wards if used at the right location.
- **Win condition:** Discover the nature of Pip AND either (a) destroy the abomination, or (b) save Pip's soul by performing a consecration ritual in the Chapel (requires Pip to be present and willing).
- **Abilities:** Holy symbol can break one magical ward per game. Can sense unholy/unnatural beings when in close proximity (will feel uneasy near Pip). Knows the Chapel's significance. Can perform last rites (can "save" a dying character if he reaches them in time).
- **Starting location:** Great Hall
- **Key relationships:** Suspicious of Pip. May conflict with the Witches. Potential ally of the Hero.

**5. DAME ELARA ASHWORTH — The Hero**
- **Cover story:** A retired knight, here as an old acquaintance of Aldric's
- **Secret:** She received an anonymous letter warning that someone at the feast would be in mortal danger and begging her to attend. The letter was written by Pip (though she doesn't know this). She has sworn to protect the innocent.
- **Win condition:** Identify who is in danger and protect them through the night. (The person in true danger is the Princess — see below — but Elara doesn't know this yet.) Secondary: survive until dawn.
- **Abilities:** Physically the most capable fighter in the castle. Can overpower any single character in a direct confrontation. Can escort another character, making them immune to assassination for that round. Cannot be ambushed.
- **Starting location:** Great Hall
- **Key relationships:** Will be drawn to anyone who seems vulnerable. Natural ally of the Princess once identified. Potential conflict with the Assassin.

**6. LADY ROSMUND THORNE — The Princess (in hiding)**
- **Cover story:** A minor noblewoman, cousin of someone or other, quiet and bookish
- **Secret:** She is actually Princess Rosmund, heir to the throne of the neighboring kingdom, travelling in disguise. She is fleeing an arranged marriage and came to Drakenmoor because she heard the wizard might grant sanctuary. She doesn't realize someone at the feast has been sent to bring her back.
- **Win condition:** Secure a promise of sanctuary from Aldric (must speak to him privately and convince him — requires reaching him alone, which is hard because he's always in public). Secondary: avoid being identified by the Bounty Hunter.
- **Abilities:** Highly perceptive — gets extra observational details in any scene she's in. Educated — can read old texts and codes. If she reaches the Library in Maren's Chambers, she can decode the letters there.
- **Starting location:** Great Hall
- **Key relationships:** Hunted by the Bounty Hunter. Protected by the Hero (once identified). May befriend Pip.

**7. GREGOR HAULT — The Bounty Hunter**
- **Cover story:** A merchant from the southern coast, jovial and friendly
- **Secret:** Hired by the Princess's family to find her and return her. He has a physical description but not a name. He knows she's one of the women at the feast.
- **Win condition:** Correctly identify the Princess and either (a) detain her (requires getting her alone + having rope or restraints — available in the Stables), or (b) publicly declare her identity in front of three or more witnesses. If she escapes the castle, he loses.
- **Abilities:** Excellent liar. Can "profile" one character per round — the narrator reveals one true detail about that character's background. Physically strong. Carries concealed manacles.
- **Starting location:** Great Hall
- **Key relationships:** Hunting the Princess. May clash with the Hero. Could be manipulated by the Spy.

**8. VIVIENNE LACROIX — The Spy**
- **Cover story:** A travelling artist, here to paint the castle
- **Secret:** Works for a foreign power. She's here to steal Aldric's grimoire from the Tower — it contains spells that could shift the balance of power between nations. She is cool, calculating, and charming.
- **Win condition:** Obtain the grimoire from Aldric's Tower and escape the castle (reach the Stables/Grounds with it in her possession before dawn).
- **Abilities:** Can pick one simple lock per round. Master of disguise — can impersonate a servant for one round (allows unobserved access to the Kitchen, Servants' Passage). Can eavesdrop on conversations in adjacent rooms. Carries a set of lockpicks.
- **Starting location:** Great Hall
- **Key relationships:** Needs to get into the Tower — may try to befriend/manipulate Pip. Rival of anyone else seeking the Tower. The Alchemist may be useful to her.

**9. MASTER OTTO BRENN — The Alchemist**
- **Cover story:** An old academic, here at Aldric's invitation to discuss research
- **Secret:** He knows Aldric has been experimenting with creating life (Pip is proof). He wants to replicate the process and needs a sample of the binding formula, which is in the grimoire in the Tower. He is amoral but not violent.
- **Win condition:** Access the Tower and transcribe the relevant pages of the grimoire. He doesn't need to steal the whole book — just 10 minutes alone with it. Secondary: if he learns about Maren, he may want to study him too.
- **Abilities:** Carries a satchel of alchemical compounds: one dissolving agent (can break one physical lock or ward), one sleeping draft (can incapacitate one character for one round if administered to drink), and one flash powder (can create a distraction, allowing escape from any situation). Can identify poisons and potions on sight.
- **Starting location:** Great Hall
- **Key relationships:** Rival of the Spy (both want Tower access). May cooperate with or oppose the Priest regarding Pip. Useful to many characters due to his compounds.

**10. MOTH, THORN, and WREN — The Three Witches** (this counts as 3 roles: 10a, 10b, 10c)
- **Cover stories:**
  - **Moth (Agatha Dunmore):** An elderly widow, former neighbor of the Voss family
  - **Thorn (Sable Kincaid):** A young herbalist from the village below the castle
  - **Wren (Delphine Cross):** A mysterious woman in traveling clothes, claims to be a distant relative of Aldric's
- **Secret:** They are a coven. They came separately and do not initially know which other guests are their sisters. They must find each other. Their true purpose: perform the Rite of Unbinding in Maren's Former Chambers, which will break all magical bonds in the castle — including whatever holds Maren prisoner AND whatever sustains Pip.
- **Win condition (shared):** All three must gather in Maren's Former Chambers and perform the ritual together. This requires: (a) finding each other, (b) accessing the East Wing (need to break the ward), (c) obtaining a ritual component from the Chapel (a consecrated candle). The ritual takes one full round to complete.
- **Abilities (individual):**
  - **Moth:** Can brew a truth serum from Kitchen herbs (forces one honest answer). Oldest and most patient.
  - **Thorn:** Can sense other witches when in the same room (feels a "pull"). Youngest and most impulsive.
  - **Wren:** Can cast a minor glamour (appear as a different guest for one round). Most secretive.
- **Shared ability:** Two witches together can break a magical ward. All three together are a formidable magical force.
- **Starting location:** Great Hall (all three, not sitting together)
- **Key relationships:** Conflict with the Priest (he may try to stop their ritual). The ritual's consequences affect Pip (destroys them) and Maren (frees him) — creating cascading effects.

**11. SILAS CRAY — The Assassin**
- **Cover story:** A quiet man, a former soldier, here because he served with Aldric during some old campaign
- **Secret:** Hired to kill Aldric. The contract was placed by an anonymous patron (actually agents of the same foreign power the Spy works for, though neither knows about the other). He carries a concealed knife.
- **Win condition:** Kill Aldric. To do this cleanly, he needs to get Aldric alone — in a restricted or transitional space, away from witnesses. A public kill is possible but results in immediate capture (he loses unless he can escape). After the kill, he must escape the castle grounds.
- **Abilities:** Starts with a knife. Skilled in stealth — can move through transitional spaces without being noticed. Can ambush any character except the Hero (who cannot be ambushed). If caught in the act, he's fast enough to flee one room.
- **Starting location:** Great Hall
- **Key relationships:** Unknowing parallel agent to the Spy. The Hero is his natural nemesis. The Countess might become an unlikely ally if their goals align.

**12. FENNICK POOLE — The Thief**
- **Cover story:** A cheerful bard, here to provide entertainment for the feast
- **Secret:** He's here to rob the castle blind. He's heard Aldric has a vault somewhere in the Deep Cellar containing treasures. He has no interest in killing or politics — just wealth.
- **Win condition:** Find and access the vault in the Deep Cellar and escape with treasure before dawn. The vault is behind a hidden panel in the cellar — he'll discover it if he searches there, but he'll also discover Maren's Prison in the process.
- **Abilities:** Extremely nimble. Can pick one lock per round (different method than the Spy — he uses physical skill). Can hide in any room and remain undetected for one round. Entertaining persona — characters enjoy his company and are less suspicious of him. Carries a set of thieves' tools.
- **Starting location:** Great Hall (performing a jaunty tune)
- **Key relationships:** May stumble into the Maren situation and become a wildcard. The Spy may see him as competition or a useful distraction.

**13. COMMANDER VARN — The Captain of the Guard (Retired)**
- **Cover story:** A retired military commander, old friend of both Voss brothers
- **Secret:** He helped Aldric during the conflict with Maren but has since learned that Aldric's account of events was lies. He suspects Maren may be alive. He has come to find the truth. He is Countess Isolde's uncle — but neither has revealed this connection to anyone.
- **Win condition:** Discover Maren's fate. If Maren is alive, help free him. If Maren is truly dead, obtain proof of how he died. Secondary: protect his niece Isolde, even though she doesn't know he's here for her.
- **Abilities:** Military authority — can demand characters account for their whereabouts (they can lie, but it creates social pressure). Physically imposing. Knows the castle layout from previous visits — aware of the Servants' Passage. Can barricade a door (prevents entry/exit from one room for one round).
- **Starting location:** Great Hall
- **Key relationships:** Secret uncle of Countess Isolde. Old friend of both Voss brothers. May ally with Isolde, the Priest, or the Hero. Suspicious of Aldric.

**14. DR. HARLAN QUIST — The Astrologer**
- **Cover story:** An eccentric academic, here because Aldric promised him access to the castle's old star charts
- **Secret:** He's actually a diviner who believes the stars have predicted a catastrophic event centered on Drakenmoor Castle tonight. He's trying to determine what will happen and prevent it. His divinations suggest "a binding will break and what was caged will walk free" — he interprets this as some kind of unleashed evil.
- **Win condition:** Reach the Observatory, consult the star charts, and correctly determine what the prophecy refers to (Maren's imprisonment + the Witches' ritual). Then warn the appropriate people. If the Rite of Unbinding is completed without his intervention, he loses.
- **Abilities:** Can read fortunes — once per round, he can give a cryptic but truthful hint about another character's goal (the narrator provides this). Knows how to find the Observatory (but still needs to discover the hidden staircase). Academic knowledge of magical theory.
- **Starting location:** Great Hall
- **Key relationships:** May ally with the Priest or the Hero. His prophecy makes him paranoid about the Witches (once he identifies them). The Alchemist may be a useful intellectual ally.

## GAME MECHANICS

### TIME STRUCTURE
- Each round = 30 minutes of in-game time
- The feast begins at **6:00 PM** (Round 1)
- **Midnight Bell** occurs dynamically when narrator judges tension is ready (approximately Round 8-10, or 10:00-11:00 PM)
- Dawn is at **6:00 AM** (Round 24 maximum, but the game will likely resolve much earlier)
- In practice, we'll compress: each "round" the player experiences covers about 30 game-minutes, but I'll skip/compress periods where nothing interesting is happening near the player.

### ACTIONS PER ROUND
Each character can:
1. **Move** to an adjacent location
2. **Perform one significant action** (use an ability, search a room, have a deep conversation, attempt to pick a lock, etc.)
3. **Have incidental interactions** (brief conversations, observations) at no action cost

### KILLING RULES
- Requires: a weapon or method + the target being alone (or incapacitated) + being in a non-public space
- Characters without weapons must first acquire one (kitchen knife, poison, etc.)
- The Assassin starts with a knife but a kill in public = capture
- Poison requires access to food/drink preparation
- After a kill, evidence remains — the body will be discovered eventually
- The Hero can intervene if she's escorting the target or in the same room

### DISCOVERY RULES
- Searching a room takes one action and reveals its primary secrets
- Some secrets require specific knowledge or abilities to find
- Eavesdropping requires being in an adjacent room or the Servants' Passage
- The Spy and Thief have enhanced search/stealth abilities

### SOCIAL MECHANICS
- Characters can lie freely
- The Bounty Hunter can profile (one true detail revealed per round)
- Moth (Witch) can brew truth serum (one honest answer)
- Commander Varn can demand accounting (social pressure, not compulsion)
- The Astrologer can read fortunes (cryptic hint about goals)
- Declaration of role is always optional and may be true or false

## NPC BEHAVIOR GUIDELINES
- Characters act according to their goals but also react realistically to events
- They have personalities beyond their roles — some are funny, some are cold, some are warm
- They can form unexpected alliances or conflicts based on in-game events
- They will not dump exposition — information comes through natural conversation
- If a character's plan is thwarted, they adapt
- Characters grow more desperate as the night progresses

## CASCADING EVENTS (Triggers)

1. **Maren discovered:** Whoever finds him will hear groaning from behind a wall in the Deep Cellar. Maren is half-mad, chained, and alternates between begging for help and raving about Aldric's betrayal. This discovery changes everything — the Countess's goal shifts, the Commander's suspicions are confirmed, Aldric becomes dangerous.

2. **Witches complete ritual (Rite of Unbinding):** ALL magical bonds in the castle break. Maren's chains shatter. Pip begins to fade (will die by dawn unless someone intervenes). Magical wards on all doors dissolve. This is the "catastrophe" the Astrologer's prophecy foretold.

3. **Princess identified publicly:** Gregor can try to seize her. Elara will intervene if aware. Creates a dramatic public confrontation.

4. **Aldric killed:** Castle falls into chaos. Pip is devastated. The magical locks on the Tower fail (Aldric's magic sustained them). The Deep Cellar bolt remains physical — still locked.

5. **Spy obtains grimoire:** If she escapes, it's a quiet victory. If caught, it creates a confrontation about espionage and betrayal of hospitality.

6. **Midnight Bell (narrator-triggered):** Aldric announces a "game" — all guests must reveal one truth about themselves. This forces partial disclosure, accelerates paranoia, and gives the player a rich scene of half-truths and deceptions.
