# THE WIZARD'S FEAST — GAME DIARY
## (Record of all character movements and events, by round)

## ROUND 1 — THE ARRIVAL (6:00 PM)
### Location: The Great Hall

**All 16 characters are present.**

**Aldric Voss** stands at the head of the hall before the great hearth, firelight making his silver-streaked beard glow. He is expansive, loud, clearly enjoying himself. He raises a goblet and delivers a booming welcome, referencing his "late" brother Maren with a toast that is equal parts tribute and gloating. "To victory, and to those wise enough to celebrate it with the victor!" His eyes are bright but there's a tightness around them when he says Maren's name.

**Pip** stands just behind Aldric's left shoulder, motionless. Pale skin, dark eyes that are slightly too large. Dressed in grey. When Aldric toasts, Pip does not drink. Pip's gaze moves across the guests methodically, pausing on Brother Callum (sensing his holy nature?) and on Lady Rosmund (why?). Pip whispers something to Aldric that makes the wizard's smile falter for just a moment.

**Countess Isolde Varn** accepts wine graciously. She is dressed in deep burgundy, composed, regal. Her eyes never leave Aldric during his speech. When he toasts Maren's destruction, her hand tightens almost imperceptibly on her goblet stem. She positions herself near the Dining Room entrance — noting exits. She exchanges a polite nod with Commander Varn across the room but shows no special recognition.

**Brother Callum** offers to bless the gathering. Aldric accepts with amused condescension. During the blessing, Callum passes near Pip and visibly hesitates — he feels something wrong. He covers it smoothly but his eyes keep drifting back to the apprentice. He takes a position near the hearth where he can observe.

**Dame Elara Ashworth** is tall, weathered, carries herself like a soldier despite her formal attire. She accepts wine but doesn't drink it. She is scanning the room systematically — exits, windows, the positions of every guest. She notes Lady Rosmund's quiet nervousness and files it away. She also notes Silas Cray's watchfulness — one soldier recognizing another.

**Lady Rosmund Thorne** is doing her best to be invisible. She is dressed simply compared to the Countess, clutching a small book to her chest. She positions herself near a window alcove. She studies Aldric, clearly wanting to approach him but unable to find an opening while he's surrounded by well-wishers. She watches Pip with curious sympathy rather than fear.

**Gregor Hault** (Bounty Hunter) is loud, warm, working the room like a seasoned merchant. He claps the Alchemist on the back, compliments the Countess's dress, laughs at his own jokes. But his eyes are working — he's studying the women. He spends his Round 1 "profile" ability on **Vivienne Lacroix** — and learns she is not actually a painter (her hands show no paint stains, her "portfolio" case is the wrong shape for canvases). He files this away with a merchant's smile.

**Vivienne Lacroix** (Spy) is charming and gracious, complimenting the castle's architecture. She notes the locked door at the far end of the hall (leads toward the Tower wing), the servants entering from a side door (the Servants' Passage), and the general layout. She engages Pip in brief conversation — "What a marvelous home you have" — and notes that Pip responds with a strange non-sequitur: "It has many doors. Some should stay closed." Vivienne smiles and remembers this.

**Master Otto Brenn** (Alchemist) corners Aldric after the toast and tries to engage him about his research. Aldric is evasive but flattered — "All in good time, Otto, all in good time! Tonight is for celebration!" Otto notes Pip's unusual nature with academic interest. He adjusts his satchel of compounds — it never leaves his side.

**Moth (Agatha Dunmore)** sits quietly at the edge of the hall, nursing wine. She watches everyone with patient, hooded eyes. She is waiting, listening. She does not yet feel the pull of her sisters — they're all in the same large room and the sensation is diffuse.

**Thorn (Sable Kincaid)** is restless, moving through the crowd. She's young, pretty, and visibly out of place among the nobility. She feels a vague magical pull but can't pinpoint it — the room is too crowded. She finds herself gravitating toward Moth without knowing why, then veers away when the old woman seems too ordinary.

**Wren (Delphine Cross)** stands apart, near the Gallery entrance, watching with cool grey eyes. She has told anyone who asks that she's a distant cousin of Aldric's on his mother's side. She is cataloguing faces and names, building her map of the gathering. She is the most cautious of the three witches and will not act hastily.

**Silas Cray** (Assassin) is quiet, positioned near a wall. He responded to introductions with clipped politeness. He is mapping Aldric's movements — noting when the wizard is surrounded, when he steps away, who flanks him. He notes the Tower door's lock, the Courtyard doors, the main entrance. He has identified two possible approaches: lure Aldric to a private meeting, or wait until the wizard retires for the night. He takes wine but barely sips it.

**Fennick Poole** (Thief/Bard) has produced a lute and is playing a lively tune near the hearth. Guests are tapping their feet. He's good — genuinely talented — and his performance gives him cover to observe the entire room. He notes the silver candlesticks, the jeweled goblets, and more importantly, the servants' door. He catches a glimpse of stone stairs descending when a servant emerges with more wine. The cellar.

**Commander Varn** stands with military bearing near the main doors. He studies the hall — noting changes since his last visit years ago. Several tapestries are new. The portrait of Maren that used to hang above the hearth has been removed. He exchanges that careful nod with Isolde — both aware of each other, neither revealing the connection. He wants to speak with her privately but not yet. First, he wants to see how Aldric behaves.

**Dr. Harlan Quist** (the Player) arrives and takes in the scene. The weight of his prophecy sits heavy on him.

### ROUND 1 CONTINUED (Post-toast mingling, ~6:15 PM)

**While Quist observes from the periphery, the following unfolds:**

**Gregor Hault** drifts toward the cluster of women. He's already dismissed Vivienne as "not the Princess" (wrong profile — too confident, too calculating). He turns his attention to **Lady Rosmund**, offering her wine with merchant charm. "You look like you could use a warmer cup, my lady. Gregor Hault — imports, mostly. Dreadfully boring. What brings you to this heap of stones?" Rosmund is polite but guarded. She says she's a distant connection of the family. Gregor notes: she's the right age, the right bearing, educated speech disguised under plain clothes. He files her as his top candidate.

**Countess Isolde** approaches **Commander Varn** at the main doors. Their exchange is brief and carefully public — two strangers making small talk. But Isolde murmurs beneath the pleasantries: "You shouldn't have come." Varn replies: "Neither should you." They separate. Isolde moves toward the Dining Room entrance, studying the layout beyond. Varn remains at his post, jaw tight.

**Vivienne Lacroix** has cornered a servant near the side door. She's sketching the hearth (maintaining her cover) while chatting casually. She learns: the servants use a passage behind that door connecting to the kitchen and the cellar. The Tower is in the north wing, accessed through a locked door off the main corridor. Aldric keeps the key on his person. She thanks the servant with a dazzling smile and files all of it.

**Silas Cray** has moved to the windows. He's studying the Courtyard through the glass — mapping exits to the grounds. He notes: the main doors (guarded impression but actually unattended), the Courtyard doors, and the side servant entrance. He also watches Aldric's pattern — the wizard is a pacer, circling the hall, never staying still. Hard to get alone. Silas decides he'll need to find a way to lure Aldric somewhere private later.

**Dame Elara** has positioned herself near **Lady Rosmund's** window alcove — not intruding, just... nearby. She noticed the young woman's nervousness and Gregor's pointed interest in her. Her protective instinct is stirring. She doesn't speak to Rosmund yet but she's watching.

**Brother Callum** approaches **Pip** directly. "A fine gathering your master has assembled. Tell me, child — how long have you been in his service?" Pip stares at him for an uncomfortable beat, then says: "Since the beginning. Before the beginning. There is no before." Callum's hand drifts to the holy symbol beneath his robes. He forces a smile. "How... philosophical." He withdraws, troubled. His suspicions are deepening.

**Master Otto Brenn**, rebuffed by Aldric, turns to the room at large. He spots Quist across the hall and considers approaching a fellow academic, but sees Quist is absorbed in observation. Instead, Otto drifts toward **Moth (Agatha Dunmore)**, recognizing a fellow old soul at the edge of things. They exchange pleasantries. Agatha asks harmless questions about his work. Otto, flattered, mentions his interest in "the boundaries of creation." Agatha's eyes sharpen almost imperceptibly, but she nods along like a polite old woman.

**Thorn (Sable Kincaid)** is fidgeting near the food tables. She reaches for bread at the same moment as **Fennick Poole**, who grins at her. "The bard always gets first pick — house rules," he says. She laughs despite herself. They chat briefly — she says she's an herbalist, he says he's here to play music and drink free wine. Both are lying about why they're really here, and neither suspects the other. Thorn moves on, still feeling that vague pull in the room but unable to locate its source.

**Wren (Delphine Cross)** has drifted to the Gallery entrance and is examining the doorframe with apparent idle curiosity. She peers into the darkened room beyond — portraits, display cases, a long shadowed space. She notes the Gallery for later exploration. She returns to the hall and takes a position where she can see both Moth and Thorn, though she doesn't yet know they're her sisters. She's watching for signs.

**Fennick Poole** finishes his tune to applause. During the clapping, he slips behind a pillar near the servant's side door and peers through when it swings open. He catches a glimpse: stone steps descending, cool air rising from below. The cellar. He grins. Later.

**Aldric** announces that dinner will be served in the Dining Room in a few minutes. He gestures grandly toward the open doors. "Come! Eat! I promise my cook is better than my brother was at — well, at anything!" The joke lands poorly. Several guests wince. Pip, behind him, mouths that silent word again.

### END OF ROUND 1 DIARY

## ROUND 2 — THE DINNER (6:30 PM - 7:00 PM)
### Location: The Dining Room (all characters, seated)

**Seating arrangement around the long table:**
Head: Aldric. To his right: Pip, then Quist (player maneuvered this). To his left: Countess Isolde (by social rank), then Commander Varn. Further down one side: Dame Elara, Lady Rosmund, Gregor Hault, Fennick Poole. Other side: Brother Callum, Otto Brenn, Vivienne Lacroix, Agatha Dunmore (Moth), Sable Kincaid (Thorn), Delphine Cross (Wren), Silas Cray (far end, by choice).

**Quist** takes the seat beside Pip and engages the apprentice in conversation, eventually offering a divination. (See player scene below.)

**Countess Isolde** is seated near Aldric — close enough to study him. She eats little. During conversation she asks Aldric, seemingly casually, about the castle's history. "It must have been difficult, the business with your brother. Were his things kept? Or was it too painful?" Aldric stiffens. "Everything of Maren's was sealed away. I don't care to revisit it." Isolde notes the word "sealed" — not "destroyed." She files this. She also notices the servants bringing wine from the side door — and wonders where it leads.

**Commander Varn**, beside Isolde, eats methodically and says little. He watches Aldric's reactions to Isolde's questions with careful attention. When Aldric mentions sealing Maren's things, Varn's fork pauses mid-air for just a moment. He remembers the East Wing. After dinner, he intends to find it.

**Gregor Hault** has maneuvered himself near Lady Rosmund. Over dinner, he uses his PROFILE ability on her. He asks friendly questions about her home, her family, her travels. **Narrator reveals one true detail to Gregor:** Rosmund is not who she claims — her table manners and deportment are those of someone raised in a royal household, not a minor noble's. Gregor is now almost certain she's his target. He remains friendly, not wanting to spook her. He'll need to find rope or restraints — he remembers passing stables outside.

**Lady Rosmund** is uncomfortable under Gregor's attention. She answers his questions with practiced half-truths but she can feel him circling. She glances down the table at Aldric, hoping dinner conversation might offer a moment, but the wizard is too far away and flanked by the Countess and his own apprentice. She notices Dame Elara seated nearby and feels oddly reassured by the older woman's solid presence.

**Dame Elara** has positioned herself within earshot of Rosmund and Gregor. She doesn't like how the merchant keeps pressing. She can't articulate why — just instinct. She eats quietly, listening, and makes a mental note to find an opportunity to speak with the young woman privately after dinner.

**Brother Callum** is seated across from Otto Brenn, and the two academics fall into conversation about magical theory. Callum steers it toward "unnatural creation" — Otto takes the bait enthusiastically, speaking about the theoretical possibility of constructing life. Callum asks: "Have you ever seen such a thing attempted?" Otto glances toward the head of the table — toward Pip — and says, "I have my suspicions." Both men look at Pip. A silent understanding passes between them, though their intentions diverge wildly: Callum wants to save or destroy; Otto wants to study and replicate.

**Vivienne Lacroix** is charming the table around her, but her real attention is on the servants. She counts how many there are (four), notes their movements, and identifies the one who seems most senior — a grey-haired woman named Marta. Vivienne plans to impersonate a servant later tonight. She also overhears Callum and Otto's exchange about "unnatural creation" and files it as potentially useful leverage.

**The Three Witches** are spread across the table. Moth (Agatha) eats slowly and watches. Thorn (Sable) keeps fidgeting — she can feel the pull more strongly now, in the closer quarters of the dining room. At one point she drops her napkin and, bending to retrieve it, locks eyes with Moth across the table. Something passes between them. Thorn's breath catches. Moth gives the faintest nod. *First contact.* Wren (Delphine), further down, notices neither of them yet — she's focused on cataloguing the room's exits and the location of the kitchen door.

**Silas Cray** sits at the far end and eats in silence. He has a clear sightline to Aldric. He's counting how many people stand between himself and the wizard at all times. He notices the servants' door and considers: after dinner, when guests disperse, Aldric might retire through one of several routes. Silas needs to learn the castle's layout before he can plan properly. He intends to explore after dinner.

**Fennick Poole** entertains his section of the table with a comic story about a duke's cat. People laugh. Under the table, his hand traces the underside of his chair — testing, feeling. Old habit. He's thinking about that cellar entrance. After dinner, when the crowd disperses, he'll make his move.

**Aldric** presides over dinner with aggressive cheer. He drinks heavily. He tells stories about his travels, his victories, his magical accomplishments. He does NOT mention Maren again. When Quist engages Pip (see below), Aldric is drawn into the conversation with amused interest.

**Pip** responds to Quist's approach. (See player scene.)

### KEY DEVELOPMENTS:
- Thorn and Moth have made first contact (witches finding each other: 2 of 3)
- Gregor is nearly certain Rosmund is the Princess
- Callum and Otto have a tentative intellectual alliance regarding Pip
- Isolde has learned Maren's belongings are "sealed," not destroyed
- Commander Varn is planning to find the East Wing after dinner
- Silas and Fennick are both planning post-dinner exploration

### END OF ROUND 2 DIARY

## ROUND 2 CONTINUED — DINNER PROGRESSES (6:45 PM)
### Location: The Dining Room

**Quist** gives Pip a small nod and a warm look — a silent promise — before turning to Aldric. He reads Aldric's fortune with the bone dice. (See player scene below.) Quist sees troubling symbols but deliberately offers a flattering, vague interpretation. Aldric is pleased but watchful. Quist uses the opening to ask about the Observatory and star charts. Aldric agrees to let Quist visit "later, later — after we've had our fun."

**Pip** retreats into silence after Quist's nod. But for the rest of dinner, Pip's gaze keeps returning to Quist. When Aldric is distracted by conversation, Pip's lips move — forming words too quiet to hear. At one point Pip reaches toward Quist's bone dice, still on the tablecloth, then pulls back as if afraid to touch them.

**Aldric**, after his fortune is read, becomes more expansive. He drinks more wine. The flattering reading has loosened him. He turns to his left to engage Countess Isolde, whom he treats with flirtatious condescension. "My lady, you grace my humble table. Your province was wise to send its most beautiful representative." Isolde's smile could cut glass.

**Countess Isolde** endures Aldric's attention. She uses it. During their exchange, she asks about "the sealed wing — the one that was your brother's." Aldric's face clouds. "That wing is sealed for good reason. There's nothing there but dust and bad memories." He changes the subject abruptly. Isolde now knows there IS a sealed wing, and that Aldric is defensive about it. She plans to find it after dinner.

**Commander Varn** overhears this exchange (seated next to Isolde). He catches Isolde's eye for a fraction of a second. They both heard "sealed." Varn remembers the East Wing from previous visits. He knows roughly where it is. He and Isolde do not speak to each other about this — not yet — but both are now pointed at the same target.

**Gregor Hault** continues working on Lady Rosmund during dinner. He tells amusing stories about his trading voyages, trying to get her comfortable. Rosmund laughs at his stories but deflects every personal question. Gregor is now 90% certain she's his target. He begins thinking about logistics — he needs restraints, and he needs her isolated. The stables would have rope. He plans to excuse himself after dinner and visit the stables.

**Lady Rosmund** feels the walls closing in. Gregor is too persistent, too interested. She excuses herself to refill her water glass and whispers to a servant: "Is there a way I might speak to the master privately? A quiet room, perhaps?" The servant looks uncertain and says she'll ask. Rosmund returns to her seat. Dame Elara, nearby, noticed the whispered exchange with the servant.

**Dame Elara** has been watching the Gregor-Rosmund dynamic all through dinner. Her protective instinct is fully engaged. She doesn't know why this young woman needs protecting — only that she does. After dinner, Elara plans to approach Rosmund directly.

**Brother Callum** and **Otto Brenn** continue their conversation about magical theory. Callum learns that Otto has a "dissolving agent" that can break magical wards — Otto mentions this casually as an academic capability. Callum files this: if the ward on Maren's chambers is magical, Otto could break it. But Callum's primary focus remains Pip. He asks Otto directly: "That apprentice — what do you make of it?" Otto replies carefully: "I think Aldric has accomplished something remarkable. Whether it's an abomination or a miracle depends on your theology." They regard each other — allies of convenience, separated by philosophy.

**Vivienne Lacroix** eats, charms, observes. She has identified the senior servant (Marta) and plans to acquire a servant's uniform from the Guest Wing laundry after dinner. She overhears Aldric tell Quist he can visit the Observatory "later" — she notes the Observatory's existence as a point of interest but her focus remains the Tower and the grimoire.

**Moth (Agatha) and Thorn (Sable)** — their eye contact during the napkin incident was brief but electric. Moth knows. Thorn knows. Neither speaks to the other during dinner — too risky, too many ears. But Moth adjusts the herb-sprig brooch on her shawl (a coven symbol) so it's more visible. Thorn sees it. Confirmation. Now they need to find the third. Thorn begins studying **Wren (Delphine Cross)** further down the table — is it her? Wren gives nothing away.

**Wren (Delphine Cross)** eats methodically and makes polite conversation with those near her. She has not yet identified either sister. She's playing the long game. She does notice Thorn staring at her and files it as potentially significant, but she won't act on it yet.

**Silas Cray** finishes eating quickly. He is planning his post-dinner movements: explore the corridors, find the route to the Tower, identify where Aldric might be isolated. He notes that Aldric is drinking heavily — by later tonight, the wizard may be impaired. This could be useful.

**Fennick Poole** entertains the table, then excuses himself to "tune his lute" before the after-dinner entertainment. He actually slips toward the servants' side door, intending to investigate the cellar stairs he glimpsed earlier. He gets as far as the Kitchen corridor before a servant shoos him back: "Guests aren't allowed back here, sir." He retreats with a grin and an apology, but he's confirmed the route: Kitchen → Servants' Passage → Cellar stairs. He'll try again when the staff is busier.

### KEY DEVELOPMENTS:
- Quist has made a silent pact with Pip and gained Aldric's provisional permission for the Observatory
- Moth and Thorn have confirmed each other as coven sisters (2 of 3 found)
- Isolde and Varn are both independently targeting the sealed East Wing
- Gregor is nearly certain about Rosmund; plans to get rope from stables
- Rosmund has asked a servant about speaking to Aldric privately
- Elara is watching Rosmund protectively
- Callum learned Otto can break magical wards
- Fennick has confirmed the cellar route but was turned back
- Silas is planning to exploit Aldric's heavy drinking later

### END OF ROUND 2 CONTINUED

## ROUND 3 — DISPERSAL (7:00 PM - 7:30 PM)
### The castle opens up. Characters scatter.

**Quist** follows Pip out of the Dining Room. Pip leads him through the Guest Wing corridor toward the Observatory. They encounter Vivienne Lacroix in the corridor. Pip shows Quist the hidden staircase behind a tapestry. They ascend toward the Observatory. Along the way, Pip drops cryptic hints about the Chapel and about sounds beneath the kitchen. (See player scene.)

**Pip** is quietly elated to have a companion. Guides Quist eagerly. Drops hints about needing help — mentions "a place where words have weight" (the Chapel) and that "there are sounds below the kitchen that the master pretends not to hear." Pip is testing how much Quist can be trusted before revealing more.

**Countess Isolde** leaves the Great Hall heading east through the main corridors. She's looking for the sealed wing Aldric mentioned. She finds a long corridor ending in a heavy oak door — the entrance to the East Wing. The door appears ordinary but when she reaches for the handle, her fingers tingle and she's repelled — a magical ward. She can't get through. She stands in the corridor, frustrated, and considers her options. She needs someone who can break magical barriers. She remembers the alchemist mentioned "research" — could he help? She turns back.

**Commander Varn** takes a different approach. He exits through the Courtyard into the cold and snow. He circles the castle exterior toward the East Wing, remembering the layout from years ago. From outside, he can see the windows of Maren's old chambers — dark, shuttered. No way in from the exterior; the windows are barred. But as he stands in the snow looking up, he notices something: a faint light flickering behind one shutter. Brief, guttering, like a candle about to die. Then gone. Someone — or something — is in there? But the ward should prevent entry... He files this, deeply troubled, and returns inside through the Courtyard, shaking snow from his coat.

**Gregor Hault** has slipped outside to the Stables. It's dark, cold, the snow thickening. The stables smell of hay and horse. He finds rope coiled on a wall hook — perfect for restraining someone. He takes it, coils it inside his coat. While there, he kicks at a loose stone in the third stall by accident and stumbles. He doesn't investigate further — he's focused on his mission and heads back inside. (He was inches from discovering the Skeleton Key to the East Wing but missed it.)

**Lady Rosmund** remains in the Great Hall. The servant returns and whispers to her: "The master is occupied, my lady. Perhaps later in the evening." Rosmund nods, disappointed. Then **Dame Elara** approaches, direct and warm: "Forgive me for being blunt — you seem uncomfortable, and that merchant fellow was bothering you at dinner. Is there anything I can do?" Rosmund is startled by the directness but something in Elara's steady gaze makes her feel safe. "I... I need to speak with our host. Privately. It's important." Elara nods. "Then I'll help you find the moment. Stay near me." An alliance forms — though Rosmund has not revealed why she needs Aldric, and Elara hasn't explained what drew her here.

**Brother Callum** leaves the Great Hall and makes his way to the Chapel in the north wing. He finds it easily — small, cold, forgotten. Dust on the pews. But the altar is still consecrated — he can feel it. He kneels and prays, then examines the space. He notes: this altar has power. If certain rites were performed here, they would be binding. He lights a candle and leaves it burning. The Chapel is ready for whatever needs to happen. He intends to return to confront Pip later.

**Master Otto Brenn** remains in the Great Hall, nursing brandy by the fire. He sees Pip leave with Quist and frowns — he'd wanted to study the apprentice. He considers following, but decides against it. Instead, he engages **Aldric** in conversation again, this time more carefully. He praises the castle, the feast, Aldric's achievements. He asks, casually, about the Tower: "I'd love to see your study sometime. The instruments alone must be remarkable." Aldric, wine-loose, is flattered but still guarded: "The Tower is my private space, Otto. Perhaps tomorrow." Otto smiles and doesn't push. But he begins considering other ways in. The apprentice has access...

**Vivienne Lacroix** has slipped into the Guest Wing. She finds the small laundry room where servants store spare uniforms. She's in the process of selecting one when she hears footsteps — Quist and Pip approaching in the corridor. She steps out, holding a cloth, and delivers a smooth excuse about wine stains. After they pass, she completes her acquisition: one grey servant's dress, one white cap. She bundles them under her artist's satchel. She can now impersonate a servant when the time is right. She heads back toward the main corridors, planning her approach to the Tower.

**Moth (Agatha) and Thorn (Sable)** find each other in a quiet corner of the Great Hall as the crowd thins. Their exchange is brief and electric:
- Thorn: "I felt you. At the table."
- Moth: "And I you. How long?"
- Thorn: "Since the solstice."
- Moth: "Longer than you've been alive, girl. There must be a third."
- Thorn: "The woman in the travelling clothes — Cross. I feel something."
- Moth: "Patience. We must be sure. And we'll need access to the old chambers — the East Wing. The rite requires it."
They separate before anyone notices. Two of three witches confirmed. They begin watching Wren more carefully.

**Wren (Delphine Cross)** has gone to the Gallery on the second floor. A long, shadowed room of portraits and display cases. She walks its length, examining. She finds a large portrait that's been violently slashed — the nameplate reads "Maren Voss." She studies the damage: old, deliberate, angry. But then she leans closer. Behind the ruined canvas, on the wall itself, there are scratches. Thin, frantic lines scored into the stone. And they're FRESH — weeks old at most, not months. Someone or something has been scratching at this wall from the other side. She presses her ear to the cold stone. Silence. But the scratches are real. She backs away, unsettled. Further down the Gallery, she feels a draft from behind a heavy tapestry — she peeks behind it and finds a narrow staircase spiraling upward. She doesn't climb it yet, but notes it. (This is the second route to the Observatory — Quist is approaching from the Guest Wing side.)

**Silas Cray** prowls the ground floor. He moves through the main corridor, the Courtyard (briefly, noting exits to the grounds), and back through the hall toward the Kitchen wing. He finds the locked door to the Tower wing — solid, iron-reinforced. He can't pick it. He needs the key, which Aldric carries. He also notes the Servants' Passage entrance near the Kitchen. He doesn't enter — too many staff moving about. He retreats to a shadowed alcove in the main corridor to think. His best window: late night, when Aldric is drunk and retiring to bed.

**Fennick Poole** plays one more song in the Great Hall (earning genuine applause), then excuses himself to "stretch his legs." He finds the Servants' Passage and slips through to the Kitchen. The Kitchen staff are busy with dessert cleanup. He moves past them — his cheerful, unassuming manner makes him nearly invisible — and finds the cellar door. Heavy. Iron bolt on the outside. He listens. From below, very faintly: a sound. Could be old pipes. Could be a groan. He reaches for the bolt—

A footstep behind him. He turns. A grey-haired servant (Marta) is staring at him. "You shouldn't be here, sir." Fennick grins: "Got lost looking for the privy! Don't suppose you could point me...?" She ushers him out firmly. He goes, but he's heard something below that cellar door. He'll be back.

**Aldric** holds court in the Great Hall but his energy is flagging. He drinks steadily. When most guests have dispersed, he makes his own quiet exit — not through the main doors but through the servants' side door. He walks to the cellar door. Checks the iron bolt. Presses his ear to the wood. His face, in the torchlight of the empty Kitchen corridor, is haggard. He whispers something — it might be a name — then straightens, composes himself, and returns to the Great Hall.

**Fennick**, who was doubling back for another attempt, catches a glimpse of this from the far end of the Servants' Passage. He sees Aldric — the master of the castle — standing at the cellar door, ear pressed to the wood, whispering. Then leaving. Fennick's grin fades. What is down there?

- Wren has found the slashed portrait and fresh scratches in the Gallery
- Isolde found the East Wing but can't break the ward — needs help
- Varn saw a flickering light in the sealed East Wing windows from outside
- Gregor has rope; almost found the Skeleton Key but missed it
- Rosmund and Elara have formed an alliance
- Callum has prepared the Chapel
- Fennick has heard sounds below the cellar AND witnessed Aldric checking the cellar door
- Silas has mapped the ground floor, needs the Tower key from Aldric
- Vivienne has a servant disguise ready

### END OF ROUND 3 DIARY

## ROUND 4 — THE WORK (7:30 PM - 8:00 PM)
### Quist in Observatory. Castle in motion below.

**Quist** conducts deep study of the star charts with Pip's assistance. Discovers critical information about the Unbinding ritual. (See player scene.)

## ROUND 9 — ERUPTION (9:45 PM - 10:15 PM)
### Everything detonates at once.

**Quist and Pip** free Maren, give him the warning about Silas, and race for the Chapel. They pass through/near the Great Hall and witness the Isolde confrontation. En route, Pip senses the Unbinding ritual beginning. (See player scene.)

**Maren Voss**, freed, climbs from the cellar on shaking legs. He is emaciated but driven by six months of fury and grief. He stumbles through the Kitchen, through the Servants' Passage, and emerges near the Great Hall. He hears a woman's voice — shouting, accusing. He recognizes the name she's saying. His name. He walks toward the light.

**Countess Isolde — THE CONFRONTATION (10:00 PM):**
Isolde enters the Great Hall with Maren's letters held before her like a sword. The room still holds Aldric (barely conscious), Fennick (frozen mid-song), Dame Elara and Lady Rosmund (in their corner), Gregor (by the Guest Wing entrance), and Otto Brenn (by the fire).

Isolde's voice rings off the stone walls:
"Aldric Voss. You told this castle, this province, this WORLD that your brother was dead. That he attacked you and you were forced to destroy him. I have here, in his own hand, letters that prove otherwise. Letters that prove YOU attacked HIM. Letters written from the room you SEALED HIM INSIDE."

Aldric lurches to his feet. The drunkenness falls away — not entirely, but fear is a powerful sobering agent. "Isolde — my lady — you don't understand—"

"I understand PERFECTLY. You imprisoned your own brother and told the world he was dead so you could steal his castle, his legacy, his LIFE—"

"He's ALIVE!" Aldric shouts. The hall goes silent. Aldric is shaking, tears streaming. "He's alive. I couldn't — I couldn't kill him. I couldn't. He's below — in the cellar — I've been keeping him alive, feeding him, I was going to let him go when things calmed down, I just needed TIME—"

And then Maren appears in the doorway.

The Great Hall goes absolutely still. Every person in the room stares at the gaunt, filthy, hollow-eyed figure standing in the entrance. Maren Voss. The dead man. Alive.

Aldric sees his brother and makes a sound like an animal in a trap.

Isolde drops the letters.

Fennick's lute falls from nerveless fingers and hits the floor with a discordant crash.

Maren stares at Aldric. His voice, when it comes, is raw and wrecked and terrible:
"Hello, brother."

- Aldric backs away, babbling, "I was going to let you go, I swear, I was going to—"

Maren's Former Chambers. The door is open. Inside:
