# SCRATCH — THE OATH & CLIMAX

## QUIST'S DECISION
Quist chooses Pip. He sends Varn and Callum to stop the Unbinding while he speaks the oath alone with Pip in the Chapel.

This is the emotional climax of the entire game. Everything has been building to this.

## THE OATH — MECHANICS
From the grimoire:
- Requires: a spoken oath of willing anchoring
- Performed on: consecrated ground (the Chapel altar — still consecrated, divine not magical)
- By: one who gives freely
- Words: "I bind my will to your thread. I anchor you to the world."
- Must be TRULY willing — not coerced, not transactional

Quist IS truly willing. He's demonstrated this all night:
- Protected Pip from Callum's scrutiny
- Preserved the Chapel's power specifically for Pip (without saying so)
- Read Pip's nature in the grimoire and didn't recoil
- Said "I can see the spirit of life in you"
- Chose Pip over stopping the ritual

The oath should WORK. This is earned.

## WHAT HAPPENS WHEN THE OATH IS SPOKEN
The binding renews. Pip is anchored to the world by Quist's will. The thread that was unraveling stabilizes. Pip becomes MORE solid, not less. The translucence fades. Pip is... saved. Bound to existence by an act of freely given love.

Physical manifestation: warmth, light, the altar responds. The Chapel's old stones do what Pip said they would — they hold the promise. Pip gasps — not in pain but in something like being born. The thread catches. The dawn deadline is no longer a death sentence.

Even if the Unbinding completes, the oath is a NEW binding — created AFTER the Unbinding began. The Unbinding dissolves existing bonds. Quist's oath is being forged in the moment. It would survive the Unbinding because it's not one of Aldric's old bonds — it's something new, something different, an act of will, not wizardry.

This is important: Pip survives regardless of whether Varn and Callum stop the witches.

## PIP'S EXPERIENCE
Pip is fading. Translucent, light, barely there. Being held/supported by Quist at the altar. Terrified but trusting completely.

When the words are spoken, Pip FEELS it. The pull of the Unbinding doesn't stop (it's still happening in the East Wing) but something ELSE takes hold — stronger, warmer, different in nature. Not a chain but a hand. Not a cage but an embrace. The thread catches on something new — on Quist's will, freely given.

Pip should cry again. Real tears this time. Solid tears.

## VARN AND CALLUM — EAST WING ASSAULT
They run. Varn is a military commander; Callum is a priest with a holy symbol that can disrupt one magical ward.

The East Wing: the three witches are mid-ritual. Maybe 10-15 minutes in. Moth is bleeding, the blood-consecrated candle burns red, all three are chanting, the air is thick with power.

Varn arrives first. He's a physical force — he can break the circle by brute disruption. Kick the ceremonial bowl, scatter the components, physically separate the witches.

Callum arrives and uses his holy symbol — it can disrupt magical working. He holds it up and speaks a prayer of disruption.

The combination: Varn's physical intervention + Callum's holy disruption should be enough to INTERRUPT the ritual. Not permanently stop it (the witches are powerful) but break the chain of the incantation.

HOWEVER: Moth's blood consecration means Moth pays the price of failure. She bet herself. If the ritual is interrupted, Moth loses her power. She becomes "hollow" — an empty vessel. This is the consequence she accepted.

The ritual breaks. The Unbinding DOES NOT COMPLETE. But:
- Moth collapses, power burned out. She's alive but hollow.
- Thorn is furious, grief-stricken — her elder sister has been destroyed (magically)
- Wren is cold, calculating — she saw the risk. She accepts the loss.
- The partial Unbinding may have weakened some bonds in the castle but not shattered them entirely. Some wards flicker. Some locks loosen. But the catastrophic total dissolution doesn't happen.

## THE AFTERMATH
Multiple resolutions:
1. PIP IS SAVED — Quist's oath holds. Pip is anchored.
2. MAREN IS FREE — already done. Maren saved Aldric from Silas.
3. ALDRIC IS WOUNDED — stabbed by Silas, tended by Fennick and Maren.
4. THE UNBINDING INTERRUPTED — Moth sacrificed her power. The ritual failed.
5. ISOLDE'S TRUTH IS PUBLIC — everyone knows what Aldric did.
6. SILAS FLED — escaped into the storm. Professional to the end.
7. VIVIENNE ESCAPED — with the grimoire. Gone into the night.
8. GREGOR FAILED — Elara blocked him all night. Rosmund is safe (for now).
9. ROSMUND — never got her sanctuary from Aldric, but Aldric is in no position to grant anything now. Her story continues beyond this night.
10. OTTO — lost the grimoire. Failed. Will leave empty-handed.

## SCENE SHAPE
This should be the emotional peak. Focus almost entirely on:
1. Quist sending Varn and Callum away (brief, urgent)
2. The oath (the heart of everything)
3. Pip's transformation
4. Brief aftermath — what happened in the East Wing (told to Quist later or briefly cut to)
5. Dawn — the natural ending

## TONE
Quiet. After all the chaos, the running, the shouting in the Great Hall, the climax should be QUIET. Two people in a cold dark chapel. Thirteen words. A creature learning it gets to live.
