# GAME BIBLE — "THE LION'S COLLECTION"

## An Asymmetric Deduction RPG — 15 Characters, 13 Locations, One Weekend in Venice

---

## 1. SETTING

**Location:** Palazzo Leoni — a crumbling but magnificent 16th-century palazzo on the Grand Canal in Venice, Italy. Three stories of marble, frescoed ceilings, and hidden rooms built over older foundations that predate the Republic. The palazzo sits on its own small island, accessible only by private water taxi or a narrow footbridge to the Dorsoduro district — a bridge the host controls.

**Time Period:** Late autumn, 1923. The Great War is a fresh scar. The old European aristocracy is fracturing. Fascism is rising in Italy. Venice is a crossroads of exiles, opportunists, scholars, and spies. Art is both treasure and currency.

**Social Occasion:** Count Enzo Leoni, the last of his line, has invited a select group of collectors, scholars, dealers, and friends to a weekend viewing of his family's legendary private collection — paintings, manuscripts, and antiquities accumulated over four centuries. He has announced he intends to sell the entire collection at a private auction on Sunday evening. The viewing begins Friday at sunset. The auction closes the weekend.

**The Host:** Count Enzo Leoni — a 68-year-old Venetian aristocrat, charming but melancholy, walking with a cane. His family built the palazzo and filled it with art. He claims he is selling because he can no longer afford the upkeep. This is partially true.

**The Central Secret:** Hidden within the Leoni collection is the *Manus Aurea* — the "Golden Hand" — a small bronze reliquary from the 4th century alleged to contain the hand of Saint Mark himself. The Leoni family has secretly guarded it for centuries. It is not listed in any catalogue. The Count has hidden it somewhere in the palazzo, and its location is known only to him. The *Manus Aurea* is worth more than everything else in the collection combined — not just as a relic, but because the bronze casing contains a sealed compartment with a document that could reshape the political map of the Adriatic. Multiple parties want it for very different reasons.

**Time Frame:** Friday 6:00 PM to Sunday 6:00 PM. 48 hours. Each round = approximately 2 hours of in-game time. Plan for 10-12 active rounds (compressing sleeping hours and quiet periods).

**Atmosphere:** Golden candlelight on dark water. The smell of old stone and canal damp. Murano glass chandeliers throwing fractured light across Renaissance frescoes. Gondolas arriving in fog. Whispered conversations in alcoves. The distant sound of church bells marking the hours. Beautiful, decaying, and dangerous.

## 2. LOCATION MAP

### PUBLIC SPACES

**1. The Salone Grande (Great Hall)**
The palazzo's centrepiece — a vast hall with a painted ceiling depicting the Fall of Troy, marble floors, and tall windows overlooking the Grand Canal. The collection is displayed here on easels, in glass cases, and on draped tables. This is where the viewing takes place and where the auction will be held Sunday evening. Always lit. Staff present during viewing hours.
- *Environmental detail:* One section of the painted ceiling shows a figure carrying a golden box away from burning Troy. The figure's face has been deliberately scratched away — old damage, possibly centuries old.

**2. The Dining Room (Sala da Pranzo)**
An intimate room for 20, with a long oak table, Venetian glassware, and portraits of Leoni ancestors on every wall. Meals are served here — Friday dinner, Saturday lunch and dinner, Sunday lunch. A sideboard holds decanters of wine and grappa available at all hours.
- *Environmental detail:* One portrait — a woman in a red dress, 17th century — hangs slightly crooked. Behind the frame, faint scratches in the plaster form a pattern that could be letters or could be nothing.

**3. The Courtyard (Cortile)**
An open-air interior courtyard with a well at its centre, surrounded by arched colonnades on all four sides. Stone benches, potted lemon trees, a broken sundial. Open to the sky. Sound carries here — conversations echo off the stone. The main staircase to the upper floors opens onto the courtyard.
- *Environmental detail:* The well is sealed with an iron grate and a heavy lock. The Count has said it is unsafe — the stones are crumbling. The iron grate is old but the lock is new.

**4. The Loggia (Canal-Side Terrace)**
A covered terrace overlooking the Grand Canal, furnished with wicker chairs and small tables. The water taxi dock is here — the only way to arrive or depart by water. Beautiful views. Exposed to weather. At night, lanterns are lit along the railing.
- *Environmental detail:* A mooring post at the far end of the dock has deep gouges — rope burns, recent, suggesting something heavy was tied here and pulled away with force.

### RESTRICTED SPACES

**5. The Count's Study (Lo Studio)**
Second floor, locked. The Count's private office — bookshelves floor to ceiling, a massive walnut desk, a safe behind a painting of Saint George. Contains the collection catalogues, the Count's private correspondence, and financial records.
- *Access methods:* (a) The Count's key, which he keeps on his person; (b) The study window can be reached from the canal-side balcony with climbing; (c) Orla, the housekeeper, has a spare key she keeps in the kitchen
- *Environmental detail:* The safe is a Chubb double-lock, British-made. Unusual for a Venetian household. The painting concealing it — Saint George slaying the dragon — is inferior work, unlike everything else in the palazzo. It was chosen to be unremarkable.

**6. The Chapel (La Cappella)**
Ground floor, north wing. A small private chapel with an altar, six pews, votive candles, and a painted altarpiece of the Ascension. The door is unlocked during the day but locked at night by the housekeeper.
- *Access methods:* (a) The main door (unlocked by day, locked at night — Orla has the key); (b) A hidden door behind the altarpiece leads to the crypt stairs (requires knowing it exists and finding the mechanism — a rotating candlestick on the altar); (c) A window high on the north wall, reachable only with a ladder or by climbing from the courtyard colonnade
- *Environmental detail:* The altar stone is cracked down the middle. On close inspection, the crack is too clean to be natural — it was cut, then disguised. The altar can be opened, but not without tools or significant force.

**7. The Crypt (La Cripta)**
Below the chapel, accessible only through the hidden door. A vaulted stone chamber containing Leoni family tombs — stone sarcophagi from five centuries. Cold, damp, and unlit. This is where the *Manus Aurea* is currently hidden — sealed inside the tomb of Alessandra Leoni (d. 1614), the Count's ancestor who first acquired the relic.
- *Access methods:* (a) Through the chapel's hidden door; (b) A flooded passage from the old well in the courtyard (extremely dangerous — requires unlocking the well grate and descending into waist-deep water in the dark)
- *Environmental detail:* Alessandra Leoni's tomb has a Latin inscription: *"Quod custodit, liberat"* — "What she guards, she sets free." The sarcophagus lid shows signs of having been moved recently — fresh scratches in centuries-old dust.

**8. The Library (La Biblioteca)**
Second floor, east wing. A magnificent room of old books, manuscript cases, and reading desks. Nominally open to guests during the day for scholarly consultation, but the Count asks that visitors sign a register and be accompanied by his secretary. Contains rare manuscripts, maps, and the Leoni family genealogy.
- *Access methods:* (a) The main door, during the day with the secretary's knowledge; (b) At night, locked — the secretary Tomasso has the key; (c) A servants' stair from the kitchen passes the library landing — the library door can be forced from the landing without being heard from the main corridor
- *Environmental detail:* A manuscript case near the window contains a 15th-century map of Venice with annotations in code. One annotation marks the palazzo with a small golden hand symbol. Most visitors would overlook it — it requires knowledge of cartographic conventions or considerable time studying the map.

**9. The Storeroom (Il Magazzino)**
Ground floor, behind the kitchen. Contains crates of recently uncrated artwork, packing materials, tools, rope, and supplies for the auction. Also contains several paintings not deemed worthy of display — lesser works, damaged pieces, items the Count considers unsellable. Locked, but casually — the lock is flimsy.
- *Access methods:* (a) The housekeeper's key; (b) Forcing the lock (easy — a firm shoulder or a basic tool); (c) Through the kitchen when staff are absent (late at night)
- *Environmental detail:* Among the lesser paintings is a small panel painting wrapped in oilcloth and placed face-down in a crate. Unlike the others, it has no inventory tag. It is a study by Bellini — worth a fortune — and the Count has hidden it here in plain sight, intending to keep it after the sale. Only someone with genuine art expertise would recognise it.

### TRANSITIONAL SPACES

**10. The Gallery (La Galleria)**
A long corridor on the second floor connecting the study, library, and guest bedrooms. Lined with smaller paintings, busts on pedestals, and display cases of coins and miniatures. Well-lit by day, dimly lit by candles at night. Anyone walking the gallery can be seen from either end.
- *Environmental detail:* A display case of Roman coins includes one gold aureus that is actually a modern forgery. A trained eye would notice the milling is wrong.

**11. The Servants' Passage (Il Passaggio)**
A narrow corridor running behind the kitchen, past the storeroom, with a stair connecting ground floor to second floor. Used by staff. Dimly lit, easy to move through unobserved. Connects the kitchen to the library landing and the gallery.
- *Environmental detail:* The walls are thin here — voices from the dining room and the study can be heard faintly through the plaster.

**12. The Garden (Il Giardino)**
A walled garden behind the palazzo, accessible from the courtyard through an iron gate. Overgrown with climbing roses, ivy, and a few cypress trees. A stone path leads to a small boat shed on the canal side — a secondary water access point that the Count hasn't mentioned to guests. The boat shed contains an old rowing boat.
- *Environmental detail:* The boat shed door has been oiled recently — it opens silently, unlike every other door in the palazzo. Someone has prepared this as an exit.

**13. The Footbridge (Il Ponte)**
The only land connection to the Dorsoduro district. A narrow stone bridge, about 30 metres, with a locked iron gate on the palazzo side. The Count holds the key. Crossing the bridge at night is conspicuous — the bridge is exposed and visible from the loggia.
- *Environmental detail:* The gate lock is ornate but old. A skilled hand could pick it, but it would take time and be audible to anyone in the courtyard.

### HIDDEN PASSAGE

A concealed passage runs from behind a false bookcase in the library, down through the walls, to the chapel. It was built in the 16th century as an escape route. The entrance in the library requires pulling a specific book (a copy of Machiavelli's *Il Principe*) to release the mechanism. The exit in the chapel is the same hidden door behind the altarpiece. Very few people alive know this passage exists — the Count knows, and it is referenced obliquely in the Leoni family genealogy kept in the library.

## 3. CHARACTER ROSTER

### THE PLAYER

**0. "Lark" — The Sparrow**
- **Real name:** Up to the player to decide. Known to the underworld as "Lark."
- **Cover story:** Émile Doucet, a French art dealer from Lyon, representing a private buyer interested in Venetian Renaissance paintings. Has a convincing portfolio of past transactions (all forged).
- **Secret:** Lark is a professional thief — one of the best in Europe. They were hired by an anonymous client (communicated only by letter, payment in Swiss francs) to steal a specific item from the Leoni collection: a "small bronze reliquary of exceptional age." The client's letter described it only vaguely and gave no name. Lark doesn't know it's the *Manus Aurea* or what it contains. They only know: it's bronze, it's old, it's small, it's somewhere in the palazzo, and it's not in the catalogue.
- **Win condition:** Find the bronze reliquary and escape the palazzo with it before the auction closes Sunday evening.
- **Abilities:**
  1. **Light Fingers** — Lark can pick pockets or palm small objects during social interactions. Requires proximity and a distraction. Risky in public spaces — if observed, the target or witnesses may react.
  2. **Silver Tongue** — Once per round, Lark can attempt to charm or misdirect a single NPC during conversation, steering the topic or extracting a piece of information the NPC would normally guard. Works best one-on-one; NPCs who are suspicious of Lark or who have strong reason to keep their secret may resist or give partial/misleading information.
  3. **Cat's Step** — Lark can move through transitional spaces without being noticed by other characters, provided no one is specifically watching for them. Does not work in public or restricted spaces — only corridors, passages, and the garden.
- **Starting location:** The Loggia — arriving by water taxi, Friday evening.
- **Key relationships:** None at game start. Lark has never been to Venice and knows none of these people. That's part of why they were hired — no connections to trace.
- **Personality notes:** Quick-witted, observant, enjoys the game as much as the prize. Adopts personas easily and reads people well. Underneath the charm is a pragmatist who knows when to cut and run. Has a weakness for beautiful art — occasionally conflicted about stealing it.
- **What the player knows at game start:**
  - You are Lark, a thief posing as Émile Doucet, French art dealer.
  - You were hired by an anonymous client to steal a "small bronze reliquary of exceptional age" from the palazzo.
  - It is NOT in the collection catalogue. It is hidden somewhere in the palazzo.
  - Your client's letter warned: "You will not be the only one looking."
  - You must escape with it before the auction closes Sunday at 6 PM.
  - You have forged credentials, a set of lockpicks (hidden in your luggage), and 500 Swiss francs in cash.
  - You know the palazzo's public layout from a tourist guidebook but nothing about restricted areas.
  - The footbridge and the water taxi dock are the only exits you know about.

### NPCs

**1. Count Enzo Leoni — The Lion**
- **Cover story:** Ageing aristocrat forced to sell his family's art collection to cover debts. Gracious host. Sentimentalist.
- **Secret:** The Count is dying — cancer, months to live. The sale is real, but it's a cover. His true purpose this weekend is to ensure the *Manus Aurea* passes to someone he deems worthy — not the highest bidder, but the person who proves they understand what it truly is. He has set this weekend as a test. He will observe his guests, judge their character, and at the end, reveal the relic to his chosen heir. He does NOT want it to leave Italy.
- **Win condition (Protection + Completion):** Identify a worthy inheritor for the *Manus Aurea* and pass it to them before he dies or before it is stolen. The relic must remain in Italy.
- **Abilities:** (1) **Lord of the House** — controls access to all locked spaces; can lock or unlock any door. (2) **The Old Fox** — decades of reading people; the Count can detect when someone is lying to him about 70% of the time (narrator adjudicates). (3) **The Final Card** — the Count knows every secret passage and hidden feature of the palazzo.
- **Fallback — if the relic is stolen before he can pass it on:** The Count will reveal the hidden passage system to his most trusted remaining guest and ask them to pursue the thief. If no guest is trusted, he will attempt to seal the palazzo (lock the bridge, disable the boat) to trap the thief inside until he can recover it.
- **Starting location:** Salone Grande, greeting guests.
- **Key relationships:** Tomasso (his secretary, trusted), Orla (his housekeeper, utterly loyal), Professoressa Casal (an old friend he respects), Cardinal Borghetti (a man he fears).
- **Personality notes:** Warm, courtly, self-deprecating. Tells anecdotes that seem rambling but always have a point. Watches everything with sharp, sad eyes. Drinks very little despite the available wine. Moves slowly but misses nothing.

**2. Tomasso Gallo — The Secretary**
- **Cover story:** The Count's private secretary and curator of the collection. Efficient, precise, loyal.
- **Secret:** Tomasso has been embezzling from the Count for years — skimming from art transactions, falsifying insurance valuations. The sale this weekend will expose his theft unless he can alter the financial records in the Count's study before the auditor arrives Monday. He is also genuinely fond of the Count and feels deep guilt.
- **Win condition (Completion):** Access the Count's study safe, retrieve the real financial ledger, and replace it with the falsified version he has prepared. Must be done before Sunday evening.
- **Abilities:** (1) **The Catalogue** — encyclopaedic knowledge of every item in the collection; can authenticate or identify any artwork instantly. (2) **Trusted Access** — has keys to the library and moves freely through the palazzo without suspicion. (3) **Forger's Hand** — can produce convincing copies of documents, signatures, and seals.
- **Fallback — if the study safe cannot be accessed:** Tomasso will attempt to steal the real ledger from the Count's person (the Count sometimes carries a small notebook with summary figures) or, as a last resort, will try to destroy the study records entirely by starting a small fire — risking the entire collection.
- **Starting location:** Salone Grande, managing the collection display.
- **Key relationships:** Count Leoni (employer, father figure), Orla (knows she watches him; fears she suspects), Professoressa Casal (she has requested access to documents he doesn't want anyone to see).
- **Personality notes:** Meticulous, nervous energy disguised as efficiency. Adjusts his spectacles constantly. Polite to everyone but deflects personal questions with practiced ease. When stressed, his Venetian dialect slips through his careful Italian.

**3. Orla Brún — The Housekeeper**
- **Cover story:** The Count's Irish housekeeper of 20 years. Manages the household, the staff, and the Count's health.
- **Secret:** Orla is a former operative of Irish Republican intelligence who fled Ireland after the Civil War. She took the position with the Count to disappear. She has been fiercely protective of him ever since — not just as an employer but as the man who gave her sanctuary without asking questions. She knows the *Manus Aurea* exists (the Count told her years ago) and she knows roughly where it is. She has sworn to protect it.
- **Win condition (Protection):** Ensure the *Manus Aurea* is not stolen. If the Count successfully passes it to an heir, she wins. If it is taken by force or theft, she loses.
- **Abilities:** (1) **The Keys** — has copies of most keys in the palazzo (all except the Count's study safe and the bridge gate). (2) **Hard Woman** — trained in close combat and the use of improvised weapons; physically dangerous if provoked. (3) **House Eyes** — knows the servants' passage intimately and can move through it silently; also knows which walls are thin enough to eavesdrop through.
- **Fallback — if the relic is stolen:** Orla will pursue the thief physically. She will use violence if necessary. If she cannot recover it, she will attempt to seal the palazzo exits.
- **Starting location:** Kitchen, preparing for dinner.
- **Key relationships:** Count Leoni (devoted to him), Tomasso (suspects his embezzlement but lacks proof), Saoirse (does not know her — see below).
- **Personality notes:** Brisk, practical, sharp-tongued. An accent she hasn't lost in 20 years. Doesn't smile often but when she does it transforms her face. Carries herself like someone who knows how to handle trouble. Dislikes Cardinal Borghetti on sight.

**4. Cardinal Vittorio Borghetti — The Red Hand**
- **Cover story:** A senior Vatican official and art patron. Here to view the collection on behalf of the Holy See, which may bid on religious works. Dignified, pious, politically connected.
- **Secret:** The Cardinal knows the *Manus Aurea* exists and has been tasked by a powerful Vatican faction to acquire it — by purchase if possible, by theft or coercion if necessary. The document sealed inside the reliquary is a 4th-century letter that, if made public, would undermine a key historical claim underpinning Vatican authority in the Adriatic region. The Cardinal's faction wants it destroyed, not preserved. He has brought an agent with him (Brother Silvan) to do the dirty work.
- **Win condition (Completion):** Obtain the *Manus Aurea* and the document inside it. Destroy the document. Return the relic to the Vatican.
- **Abilities:** (1) **Authority of Rome** — can invoke papal authority to pressure Catholic NPCs; creates significant social pressure. (2) **The Confessional** — if an NPC confides in him or confesses, he learns their secret but is bound by the seal of confession (cannot reveal it publicly, but can act on the knowledge). (3) **Political Network** — can send messages to contacts outside the palazzo via his own private courier (a gondolier waiting at the dock).
- **Fallback — if he cannot locate the relic:** The Cardinal will attempt to coerce the Count directly, threatening to expose the Leoni family's centuries of concealing a Church relic. If the Count resists, the Cardinal will order Brother Silvan to search by force — even if it means ransacking restricted areas.
- **Starting location:** Loggia, arriving by private gondola.
- **Key relationships:** Brother Silvan (his agent, obedient), Count Leoni (an adversary he treats with courtesy), Professoressa Casal (a rival scholar whose research threatens him).
- **Personality notes:** Smooth, urbane, impeccably dressed in clerical finery. Speaks softly but every word is chosen. Never raises his voice. Has the patience of a man who represents an institution that thinks in centuries. Drinks excellent wine and compliments the Count's taste — while planning to rob him.

**5. Brother Silvan — The Blade**
- **Cover story:** The Cardinal's personal secretary and aide. Quiet, devout, deferential.
- **Secret:** Brother Silvan is not a real monk. He is a former soldier and enforcer recruited by the Cardinal's faction for exactly this kind of work. He is skilled with a knife and comfortable with violence. His real name is Marco Tessari, and he has a criminal past in Naples. He follows the Cardinal's orders without question — but he has noticed the hidden Bellini in the storeroom and is considering stealing it for himself.
- **Win condition (Completion):** Follow the Cardinal's orders to secure the *Manus Aurea* by any means. Secondary: steal the hidden Bellini painting from the storeroom for personal profit.
- **Abilities:** (1) **The Knife** — carries a concealed stiletto; trained and willing to use it. (2) **Soldier's Eye** — can assess a room's exits, defensible positions, and hiding spots instantly. (3) **Invisible Servant** — in his monk's habit, he is easily overlooked; can move through public spaces without drawing attention.
- **Fallback — if the Cardinal's plan fails:** Brother Silvan prioritises the Bellini theft and his own escape. He knows about the garden boat shed (he scouted it on arrival).
- **Starting location:** Loggia, carrying the Cardinal's luggage.
- **Key relationships:** Cardinal Borghetti (master, feared), Orla (she has clocked him as dangerous on instinct).
- **Personality notes:** Still, watchful, speaks only when spoken to. When he does speak, his voice is surprisingly soft for his build. His hands are scarred. He stands near walls and doorways — never in the centre of a room.

**6. Professoressa Elena Casal — The Scholar**
- **Cover story:** Professor of Art History at the University of Padua. Here to study and potentially bid on manuscripts from the Leoni collection.
- **Secret:** Elena has spent 15 years researching the *Manus Aurea*. She is the world's foremost expert on the relic — she knows what it is, what it contains, and roughly what the sealed document says (she found a partial transcription in a monastery archive). She is here because she believes the Count has it and intends to prove its existence to the academic world. She wants to study it, not steal it. But she also knows the Cardinal is here to destroy the document, and she will do almost anything to prevent that.
- **Win condition (Discovery + Prevention):** Locate the *Manus Aurea*, verify its contents, and prevent the document from being destroyed. She wins fully if she can study the document and publish her findings. She wins partially if the document survives, even if she doesn't get to study it personally.
- **Abilities:** (1) **Scholar's Eye** — can read Latin, Greek, and medieval Italian; can date and authenticate manuscripts, relics, and inscriptions on sight. (2) **Academic Network** — has contacts at universities across Europe who can verify claims or provide information by telegram (takes one full round for a response). (3) **Persuasion of Truth** — Elena's genuine passion and knowledge can sometimes convince NPCs to share information they'd otherwise guard, particularly on scholarly topics.
- **Fallback — if the Cardinal obtains the relic first:** Elena will attempt to photograph or transcribe the document before it is destroyed, even at personal risk. She will seek allies — anyone who opposes the Cardinal.
- **Starting location:** Library, already examining manuscripts (she arrived early).
- **Key relationships:** Count Leoni (old friend, respects him), Cardinal Borghetti (her nemesis — she knows he wants to destroy what she wants to preserve), Yusuf (a fellow scholar she's heard of but never met).
- **Personality notes:** Passionate, direct, impatient with small talk. Pushes her reading glasses up her nose when excited. Speaks quickly when discussing her research, slowly when being careful. Dresses practically — she is here to work, not socialise. Can be blunt to the point of rudeness but is fundamentally kind.

**7. Yusuf al-Rashid — The Cartographer**
- **Cover story:** An antiquarian book dealer from Istanbul, here to bid on maps and manuscripts.
- **Secret:** Yusuf is an agent of the Turkish government, sent to acquire the *Manus Aurea* document — not the relic itself, but specifically the sealed letter. The document reportedly contains territorial claims that could strengthen Turkey's legal position in ongoing Adriatic border disputes with Italy and Greece. His government wants the letter, and they want it kept quiet. He is a scholar by training and a spy by necessity.
- **Win condition (Completion):** Obtain the document from inside the *Manus Aurea* — ideally the original, but a verified transcription or photograph would suffice. Deliver it to his government contact in Venice by Sunday evening.
- **Abilities:** (1) **Map Reader** — expert in historical cartography; can decode map annotations, ciphers, and navigational markings. (2) **Quiet Diplomacy** — skilled negotiator; can propose mutually beneficial deals that NPCs find hard to refuse. (3) **Cipher Work** — can encode and decode messages; carries a small codebook.
- **Fallback — if he cannot access the relic directly:** Yusuf will attempt to ally with Professoressa Casal (their goals partially overlap — she wants the document preserved, he wants a copy) or with Lark (if he identifies the thief — he could propose splitting the contents).
- **Starting location:** Courtyard, examining the architecture.
- **Key relationships:** Professoressa Casal (aware of her research, potential ally), Cardinal Borghetti (a rival — both want the document), his government contact waiting in a pensione in Dorsoduro.
- **Personality notes:** Calm, courteous, wryly humorous. Dresses well. Listens more than he speaks. Has a habit of studying his surroundings as if reading a text. Genuinely loves old books and maps — the espionage is a burden he carries with quiet dignity.

**8. Contessa Lucrezia Morel — The Widow**
- **Cover story:** A wealthy Franco-Italian socialite and art collector. Recently widowed. Here to bid on paintings — particularly Venetian masters.
- **Secret:** Lucrezia is not here for the art. Her late husband, Henri Morel, was a forger — one of the best in Europe. Before his death, he told her that one of his forgeries was hidden in the Leoni collection, substituted for the original decades ago. The original — a priceless Titian — is hidden somewhere in the palazzo, swapped out by Henri during a restoration visit in 1910. Lucrezia is here to find the original Titian, reclaim it, and sell it to fund her escape from her husband's creditors, who are closing in.
- **Win condition (Discovery + Completion):** Find the original Titian hidden in the palazzo and escape with it.
- **Abilities:** (1) **Forger's Widow** — can identify forgeries with near-perfect accuracy (she spent years watching her husband work). (2) **Society Charm** — moves easily in high society; can get invited to private viewings, dinners, and conversations through sheer social gravity. (3) **Henri's Notes** — carries a small notebook of her husband's cryptic notes about his forgeries, including clues to where he hid the original Titian.
- **Fallback — if the Titian cannot be found:** Lucrezia will attempt to steal the hidden Bellini from the storeroom instead (lesser prize, but still valuable) or will try to acquire leverage over another guest to secure financial protection.
- **Starting location:** Salone Grande, examining the paintings.
- **Key relationships:** None at start — she is new to this circle. Will gravitate toward anyone who seems knowledgeable about art.
- **Personality notes:** Elegant, composed, deflects questions about her late husband with practised grace. Smokes with a long holder. Her composure cracks when she's alone — she is desperate and frightened beneath the polish. Has a dry, dark sense of humour.

**9. Saoirse Ní Fhaoláin — The Ghost**
- **Cover story:** A young Irish journalist covering the Venetian art scene for the *Irish Independent*.
- **Secret:** Saoirse is here for Orla. She is the daughter of a man Orla served with during the Irish War of Independence — a man who was killed in the Civil War under circumstances Saoirse believes Orla was responsible for. She has tracked Orla across Europe for three years. She is not here for revenge — she wants the truth. She wants to know what happened to her father and why Orla disappeared. The journalism cover is thin but functional.
- **Win condition (Discovery):** Learn the truth about her father's death from Orla. She wins if she gets the full story, regardless of what it reveals.
- **Abilities:** (1) **The Notebook** — compulsive note-taker; can recall details of conversations and events with near-perfect accuracy. (2) **Press Credentials** — her journalist cover gives her a plausible reason to ask intrusive questions of anyone. (3) **The Trail** — she has researched Orla's background extensively; she knows details about Orla's past that even the Count doesn't know.
- **Fallback — if Orla refuses to speak:** Saoirse will threaten to publish what she already knows — exposing Orla's real identity and past, which would destroy Orla's life in Venice and potentially endanger the Count.
- **Starting location:** Loggia, arriving with her luggage and a camera.
- **Key relationships:** Orla (her target — Orla does not recognise her), Count Leoni (Orla's employer, potential leverage).
- **Personality notes:** Young, intense, fidgets with her pen. Asks questions that seem casual but are precisely aimed. Tries to project confidence she doesn't fully feel. Has her father's stubbornness. Smokes too much when nervous.

**10. Viktor Lensky — The Collector**
- **Cover story:** A White Russian émigré and private art collector based in Paris. Wealthy, eccentric, passionate about Byzantine art.
- **Secret:** Viktor is working for a consortium of Russian émigré aristocrats who believe the *Manus Aurea* relic was stolen from a Byzantine church by Venetian crusaders in the 13th century. They want it returned — not to Turkey, not to the Vatican, but to the Russian Orthodox community. Viktor has been authorised to pay any price or use any means. He is also, genuinely, a man who loves beautiful things and hates seeing them locked away.
- **Win condition (Completion):** Acquire the *Manus Aurea* and deliver it to the émigré community in Paris. Method — purchase, persuasion, or theft — is flexible.
- **Abilities:** (1) **Deep Pockets** — Viktor has more money than anyone else at the gathering; he can outbid anyone at the auction or bribe NPCs effectively. (2) **Aristocrat's Bearing** — his natural authority and charm open doors; staff and servants tend to defer to him. (3) **Byzantine Expert** — can identify Byzantine artefacts and religious objects on sight, and can read Old Church Slavonic inscriptions.
- **Fallback — if the relic cannot be purchased:** Viktor will attempt to form an alliance with whoever finds it — offering substantial money and protection in exchange for the relic. If that fails, he will try to steal it himself, though he is no thief and would likely need help.
- **Starting location:** Salone Grande, admiring the Byzantine pieces.
- **Key relationships:** None at start — but he will quickly identify Professoressa Casal and Yusuf as people who know something about what he seeks.
- **Personality notes:** Boisterous, generous, emotional. Laughs loudly and weeps at beautiful art without embarrassment. Wears a fur-collared coat indoors. Tells long stories about lost Russia. Underneath the flamboyance is a shrewd negotiator and a man carrying deep exile grief.

**11. Madame Feng Lihua — The Broker**
- **Cover story:** A Shanghai-based art broker representing unnamed Asian collectors. Here to bid on the collection's Oriental pieces — of which there are few. She claims curiosity about the rest.
- **Secret:** Feng Lihua is a professional intermediary — not for art, but for intelligence. She brokers information between governments, corporations, and private interests. She is here because THREE separate clients have hired her for tasks related to this weekend: one wants the relic, one wants the document, and one wants to know who else is looking. She intends to play all sides and sell to the highest bidder. She does not care who wins — she cares about maximising her fee.
- **Win condition (Completion):** Identify what the *Manus Aurea* is and who wants it, then sell that information to whichever client offers the most. She wins fully if she brokers a deal involving the relic or the document. She wins partially if she escapes with valuable intelligence to sell.
- **Abilities:** (1) **The Web** — Lihua can identify when someone is being evasive or hiding something (not what they're hiding, just that they are). (2) **Double Deal** — can make binding agreements with multiple parties simultaneously without any of them knowing about the others. (3) **The Exit** — always plans her escape route; she has already arranged a private water taxi for Sunday (separate from the palazzo's boat service).
- **Fallback — if she is exposed as a broker:** Lihua will offer her services openly to whichever faction seems strongest, positioning herself as indispensable rather than untrustworthy.
- **Starting location:** Dining Room, sampling the wine.
- **Key relationships:** None at start — she has dossiers on several guests but has met none of them.
- **Personality notes:** Poised, unhurried, amused by everything. Speaks five languages fluently. Dresses immaculately. Gives the impression of knowing more than she says — which is usually true. Has a genuine appreciation for beauty but would never let it interfere with business.

**12. Dottor Augusto Conti — The Appraiser**
- **Cover story:** A Florentine art appraiser hired by the Count to value the collection for the auction. Professional, methodical, above reproach.
- **Secret:** Conti has been hired by Lucrezia Morel's creditors — a Marseille crime syndicate — to find and report on her movements. They suspect she is here to recover an asset (the Titian) and they want it. Conti's secondary task is to locate the Titian himself and mark it for the syndicate's agents to collect after the weekend. He is in debt to these people and has no choice. He is terrified.
- **Win condition (Discovery + Completion):** Find the hidden Titian and report its location to his handlers via a dead drop at the footbridge. He must also ensure Lucrezia does not escape with it.
- **Abilities:** (1) **The Expert's Eye** — can authenticate, date, and value any artwork in the collection. Recognised authority — his word carries weight with the Count. (2) **Professional Access** — has legitimate reason to examine every room, crate, and storage area. Can access the storeroom freely. (3) **The Appraisal** — can plausibly spend long periods alone with artworks, studying them — a perfect cover for searching.
- **Fallback — if Lucrezia finds the Titian first:** Conti will attempt to follow her and report her location to his handlers. If she escapes, he will try to flee Venice himself before the syndicate comes for him.
- **Starting location:** Storeroom, beginning his inventory.
- **Key relationships:** Count Leoni (hired him — trusts his expertise), Lucrezia Morel (his secret target — he does not know her personally but has her description), his handlers in Marseille (contacted via dead drop at the footbridge).
- **Personality notes:** Methodical, slightly fussy, adjusts his cuffs and his monocle. Tries to project calm authority but his hands tremble when he's anxious. Drinks too much grappa. Would rather be anywhere else.

**13. Lady Isabelle Ashworth — The Collector's Daughter**
- **Cover story:** A young English aristocrat and aspiring art collector. Her father, Lord Ashworth, was a legendary collector who died last year. She is here to continue his legacy and bid on pieces he would have wanted.
- **Secret:** Isabelle's father was a member of a secret society — the *Custodes Leonis* (Guardians of the Lion) — a centuries-old order dedicated to protecting certain relics from falling into the wrong hands. The *Manus Aurea* is one of these relics. Lord Ashworth was the Count's secret contact in England. Before dying, he told Isabelle about the society, the relic, and the Count's role as its guardian. He charged her with ensuring the relic's safety — specifically, that it must not fall into Vatican hands. Isabelle has a signet ring bearing the society's seal, which the Count will recognise.
- **Win condition (Protection + Completion):** Make contact with the Count, prove her identity as Ashworth's daughter and a member of the *Custodes Leonis*, and help him ensure the relic passes safely to a worthy inheritor. The relic must not go to the Cardinal or leave Italy.
- **Abilities:** (1) **The Signet** — her father's ring, bearing the seal of the *Custodes Leonis*. The Count will recognise it and trust her. Other guests may notice the unusual ring. (2) **Ashworth's Training** — her father taught her to assess character; she can spend a full round observing someone and gain a reliable read on whether they are trustworthy (not what their secret is, but whether they are honest). (3) **Society Connections** — can send telegrams to her father's network for background information on any guest (takes one round for a response).
- **Fallback — if the Count is incapacitated or dies:** Isabelle will attempt to find and secure the relic herself, using the hidden passage knowledge her father passed to her (he knew of the passage but not of the crypt's exact configuration).
- **Starting location:** Courtyard, arriving on foot across the bridge.
- **Key relationships:** Count Leoni (her father's ally — he does not yet know she is Ashworth's daughter), Cardinal Borghetti (her primary antagonist — she knows the Vatican wants the relic).
- **Personality notes:** Young but composed beyond her years. Speaks with quiet authority. Observes before acting. Has her father's steel but her own warmth. Dresses simply for an aristocrat — prefers functionality. Keeps her signet ring on a chain inside her collar, not on her hand.

**14. Enzo "Il Gatto" Ferraro — The Cat**
- **Cover story:** A Venetian gondolier and local guide hired by the Count to manage water transport for guests this weekend. Cheerful, talkative, knows every canal.
- **Secret:** Il Gatto is a small-time Venetian smuggler and fence who has been casing the palazzo for months, waiting for exactly this opportunity. He doesn't know about the *Manus Aurea* — he's after the collection itself. He plans to identify the most portable, most valuable pieces during the weekend and return Monday night with his crew to rob the palazzo after everyone has left. This weekend, he's mapping the layout, testing the locks, and noting which pieces are worth taking. However, if he discovers during the weekend that something far more valuable is hidden here, his plans may change rapidly.
- **Win condition (Completion):** Map every room, test every lock, identify the 5 most valuable portable items, and establish an entry/exit route for his crew's return on Monday. If he discovers the *Manus Aurea* and can take it himself, that replaces his original plan.
- **Abilities:** (1) **Canal Rat** — knows Venice's waterways intimately; can pilot any boat and access the palazzo from multiple water-level entry points that other characters don't know about. (2) **Locksmith** — skilled with locks; can pick most locks in the palazzo given time and tools (carries a set hidden in his gondola). (3) **The Local** — as a Venetian native, he is trusted by servants and staff; can gather gossip and information from them freely.
- **Fallback — if his casing is discovered:** Il Gatto will claim he was just being nosy and play dumb. If pressed hard, he'll flee by water — he keeps his gondola moored at the dock and can disappear into Venice's canals in minutes.
- **Starting location:** Loggia, tying up his gondola.
- **Key relationships:** Orla (she's hired him before; they're on friendly terms), the Count's household staff (he knows them from the neighbourhood).
- **Personality notes:** Charming, fast-talking, physically nimble. Sings Venetian folk songs while he rows. Winks too much. Genuinely funny. Beneath the bonhomie is a sharp, calculating mind. He is not violent and would rather run than fight — but he is very, very good at not getting caught.

**15. Dottoressa Maria Volpe — The Doctor**
- **Cover story:** The Count's personal physician. Here for the weekend to monitor his health. Quiet, professional, discreet.
- **Secret:** Maria knows the Count is dying and has been managing his condition with morphine. She is also the Count's illegitimate daughter — a secret known only to the two of them. The Count has never publicly acknowledged her. Maria's true purpose this weekend is to convince her father to acknowledge her in his will before he dies — not for the money, but for the recognition she has craved her entire life. She doesn't care about the *Manus Aurea*; she cares about her father's love.
- **Win condition (Completion):** Convince the Count to acknowledge her as his daughter, either publicly or in a legal document, before the weekend ends.
- **Abilities:** (1) **The Medicine Bag** — carries medical supplies including morphine, sedatives, smelling salts, and bandages. Can treat injuries or sedate someone. (2) **Clinical Eye** — can assess someone's physical and emotional state with medical precision (fatigue, intoxication, fear, pain). (3) **Trusted Confidant** — as the Count's doctor, she has access to him at all hours and no one questions her presence at his side.
- **Fallback — if the Count refuses to acknowledge her:** Maria will threaten to withhold his morphine (she controls his pain management) or to reveal the secret herself — destroying his reputation but forcing the truth.
- **Starting location:** Count's Study, checking his medication supply.
- **Key relationships:** Count Leoni (her secret father), Tomasso (she suspects he is hiding something financial), Orla (Orla senses Maria has an unusual attachment to the Count but doesn't know why).
- **Personality notes:** Reserved, precise, watchful. Keeps her emotions under clinical control — until they break through, usually as quiet fury or sudden tenderness with the Count. Dresses in dark, practical clothes. Has the Count's eyes, which observant guests might notice.

## 5. CASCADING EVENTS

**TRIGGER 1: The Forgery Is Discovered**
Someone with art expertise (Tomasso, Conti, Lucrezia, or the Professoressa) examines the displayed collection closely and identifies the forged Titian.
**Consequences:**
- The Count is humiliated and demands an investigation
- Lucrezia panics — the trail now leads toward her husband
- Conti sees an opportunity to locate the real Titian faster
- Trust among guests erodes — if one painting is forged, what else is?
- The storeroom becomes a location of intense interest

**TRIGGER 2: Brother Silvan Is Caught Somewhere He Shouldn't Be**
Silvan is discovered in a restricted area (the study, the chapel at night, the crypt).
**Consequences:**
- The Cardinal must either protect him or disavow him
- Orla's suspicions are confirmed — she escalates her watch
- The Count may lock down restricted areas more tightly
- Other guests realise someone is searching for something hidden

**TRIGGER 3: The Count Collapses**
The Count's illness causes a visible medical episode (coughing blood, fainting, severe pain).
**Consequences:**
- Maria rushes to his side, creating an emotionally charged scene
- The weekend's social fabric frays — some guests want to help, others see opportunity
- If the Count is incapacitated, Tomasso gains more freedom to access the study
- Isabelle must act faster — the Count may die before she can reveal herself
- The Cardinal may push more aggressively, sensing time running out

**TRIGGER 4: A Body of Water Rises**
An autumn storm causes the canal to flood the ground floor of the palazzo (acqua alta — common in Venice).
**Consequences:**
- The crypt floods more deeply — accessing it becomes dangerous or impossible
- The ground floor (chapel, storeroom, kitchen) is partially submerged
- The footbridge may become impassable
- Characters on the ground floor must move upstairs — forced proximity
- The garden boat shed becomes the only viable water exit
- Environmental tension spikes

**TRIGGER 5: Someone Discovers the Well Leads Somewhere**
A character (Il Gatto most likely, or Lark if exploring) examines the sealed well in the courtyard and realises it connects to something below.
**Consequences:**
- A new route to the crypt is revealed
- The Count becomes alarmed if he learns of this — he sealed the well deliberately
- Multiple characters may race to explore the passage
- The well route is dangerous (flooded, dark, narrow) — creates a high-risk, high-reward option

**TRIGGER 6: Lucrezia's Creditors Arrive**
On Saturday evening, two men arrive by water taxi claiming to be art buyers. They are actually agents of the Marseille syndicate, following Conti's reports. They are not violent — yet — but their presence terrifies both Conti and Lucrezia.
**Consequences:**
- Conti's dual role becomes more precarious
- Lucrezia must find the Titian urgently or face ruin
- Two new, unfamiliar faces in the palazzo raise everyone's suspicion
- The Count may refuse them entry (he didn't invite them), creating a confrontation at the loggia
- NOTE: These two men are not full characters — they are an environmental pressure. The narrator plays them as a looming presence, not as active agents with complex plans.
