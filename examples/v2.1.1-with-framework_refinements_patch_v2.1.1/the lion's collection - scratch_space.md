# SCRATCH SPACE — ROUND 15 (THE MORNING & THE EPILOGUE)

## TIME: Sun ~3:30 AM → Sun 6:00 PM (auction close) → epilogue
## PLAYER LOCATION: Boat on the canal, leaving Venice

## STRUCTURE OF THIS ROUND
This is the framework's required two-movement endgame per Section 4.6:
1. **First movement: Lark's personal arc closes.** The boat journey, the sense of having got away with it, the small final beats Lark earns. The dawn comes up over the lagoon. The bronze is in the boat. Il Gatto is at the oar. The relic and the document are leaving Italy.
2. **Second movement: zoom out to reveal what Lark missed.** Every NPC's arc resolved. The auction. The Count. Isabelle. The Cardinal's discovery. Lucrezia's escape. Casal's article. Viktor's homecoming. Tomasso's freedom. Orla and Saoirse. Maria's discovery. Conti's fate. Madame Feng's report. Brother Silvan. The Count's final dawn.

## WHAT THE PLAYER SHOULD FEEL
- Triumph, but earned and quiet
- The weight of the things Lark chose not to do
- The richness of all the offstage stories
- Closure for every character

## THE EPILOGUE STRUCTURE (per framework 4.6)
- Conclude Lark's arc in his chosen context — the boat, the dawn, the relic
- "Zoom out" to reveal what the player missed
- Show consequences for every NPC
- Conclude with a list of all roles, goals, and outcomes
- Thank the player for the journey
- Tell them they can play again in a new chat window

## KEY OUTCOMES TO RESOLVE
1. **The Count:** Wakes Sunday morning. Reads Lark's note in the chest (he checks the chest before the auction, as he has done every morning for months). Smiles. Dies that night, peacefully, in his own bed, knowing the relic is in respectful hands.
2. **Isabelle:** Has her final meeting with the Count Sunday afternoon. He tells her the relic is gone but in the right hands. She stays in Venice for his funeral, then returns to England. The Custodes Leonis continues.
3. **The Cardinal:** Investigates the well at dawn. Finds the empty chest. Finds Lark's note. Knows he has been beaten by a thief he underestimated. Returns to Rome with nothing. Brother Silvan is reassigned.
4. **Brother Silvan / Marco Tessari:** Reassigned. Lives.
5. **Casal:** Writes the article. Publishes within six months. The Vatican declares it a forgery. The scholarly world is divided. Her career is made.
6. **Yusuf:** Delivers the photographs. His government uses them quietly. He is promoted.
7. **Lucrezia:** Escapes Venice with the Titian Sunday morning. Pays her Marseille debts. Lives.
8. **Viktor:** Returns to Paris. Tells the old men about the bronze. Is loved for the telling.
9. **Tomasso:** The auditor arrives Monday. The falsified ledger holds. Tomasso keeps his position. Eventually inherits a small bequest from the Count.
10. **Orla:** The conversation with Saoirse continues across the rest of the year. They do not become close, but they reach peace. Orla stays at the palazzo until the Count dies, then retires to Cork.
11. **Saoirse:** Writes a long honest article about her father. Becomes a real journalist.
12. **Conti:** The Marseille agents arrive Sunday. He has nothing for them. They take him with them. He is never seen again.
13. **Madame Feng:** Sells what she has. Lark gets his 3,000 francs Tuesday afternoon in Lugano via her courier — she is honest. She files her report and moves on to her next gathering.
14. **Maria Volpe:** Discovers the relic is gone Sunday afternoon when the Count asks her to fetch the bronze for Isabelle. Realises what happened. Says nothing. The Count smiles when he hears it is gone, and tells her he has been hoping for this. He acknowledges her as his daughter that afternoon — a small private ceremony in his bedroom. She inherits the palazzo.
15. **Il Gatto:** Travels to Lugano with Lark. They meet the client (a Swiss Catholic philanthropist who wanted the document destroyed for entirely his own private reasons of faith). Lark and Il Gatto deliver the bronze without the document. The client pays. They split the money. They part as friends.
16. **Lark:** Survives. Lives to steal again. Goes to Paris and drinks vodka with Viktor at Rue de Vaugirard.

## NARRATION NOTES
- The first movement should be slow and earned
- The second movement should be brisk but loving — every NPC gets their moment
- Use the framework's recommended closing language
- End with the play-again invitation
