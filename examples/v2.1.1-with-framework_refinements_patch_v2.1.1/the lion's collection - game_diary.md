**Orla Brún** — Ran the dinner service from the kitchen door. Saw Lark's table choreography but read it as charm, not threat (yet). As she set Saoirse down on her bed, Saoirse murmured a name in her drunken stupor: "Liam." Orla froze. Liam was the name of Saoirse's father — Orla's commanding officer in the IRA, killed in 1922. Orla left the room in silence, closed the door, and stood for a long minute in the corridor. She now knows. Plans for Round 3: she will not approach Saoirse tonight. She will spend the late hours pacing the kitchen, deciding what to do. By morning she will have a plan — most likely confront Saoirse directly before the girl can act publicly.

He has known of Lord Ashworth for years as a man who interfered in Vatican affairs through his collecting. Retired to his room around 9:30 PM with Brother Silvan in attendance.
