# THE HARGROVE AFFAIR — SCRATCH SPACE

## ROUND 9 (FINAL) — The Gathering, Saturday Night ~9:30 PM – 10:30 PM

---

### THE WALK DOWNSTAIRS

Frank and Arthur descend the main staircase together. Arthur is carrying the briefcase with both wills. He's moving with a strange dignity — the weight he's been carrying hasn't gone, but he's chosen to carry it differently. Walking toward consequence rather than away from it.

The Great Hall: they encounter activity. Fenton has returned from the grounds. He's in the hall speaking with the constable. Mrs. Pugh is nearby — she's left Reg in the kitchen with Sally. When Fenton sees Arthur descending the staircase with Frank, his eyes sharpen. He reads the situation instantly — Arthur's rolled sleeves, the briefcase held open, Frank's expression.

Frank's proposal: assemble everyone in the Dining Room. Fenton will consider this. He's methodical — he'd normally interview people separately. But the night is moving fast, the Collaros may flee, and having everyone in one room gives him control. He'll agree — but on HIS terms. This is his investigation. Frank is proposing the venue; Fenton is running the show.

### FINDING DOLORES

Dolores is in the Drawing Room or her bedroom. Frank said they'd bring her along. When Arthur and Frank approach Dolores, the dynamic is:
- Arthur standing upright for the first time all weekend, looking at Dolores with the expression of a man who has finally chosen a side
- Frank beside him
- Dolores reading the situation — Arthur's posture, the open briefcase, Frank's presence. She'll know instantly that Arthur has broken.

Dolores's response: Social Grace activates. "What is this? Arthur, what are you doing?" Composed. Controlled. But her eyes go to the briefcase. She sees the documents inside. Her hand — the hand that grips things too tightly — finds the back of a chair.

She won't confess. She won't break. She'll come to the Dining Room because refusing would be conspicuous. She'll perform innocence until Fenton makes it impossible.

### THE DINING ROOM GATHERING

Mrs. Pugh and Sally gather the household. The Dining Room — Frank's territory, the room he catalogued, the room with the fake Constable above the fireplace and the replaced silver on the sideboard. Everyone assembles:

Present:
- Fenton (at the head of the table — Leonard's empty chair, now claimed by the law)
- Frank (standing, or seated near Fenton)
- Dolores (seated at the foot, her usual position — composure intact but isolated)
- Arthur (seated, briefcase on the table, ready to talk)
- Diana (sharp, watchful — she knows more than most)
- Philip (pale, sober for the first time today — the gravity of the night has burned through the alcohol)
- Margot (notebook somewhere close, eyes wide, the journalist witnessing history)
- Mrs. Pugh (standing by the kitchen door — her post, her territory)
- Sally (beside Mrs. Pugh — she knows Reg is safe now)
- Dr. Webb (at the far end, humming has stopped, very still)
- Reg (brought in from the kitchen — shaky but present, the key witness)

Absent / uncertain:
- Vinnie and Tommy. THIS IS THE QUESTION. Has Fenton sent someone to the stables? Did the constable secure them? Or have they fled?

DECISION: Vinnie is present — he came back to the house after his phone call, calculating that fleeing looks guilty and that he might still brazen it out. His solicitor instincts (criminal version) tell him to be present and deny everything. Tommy is NOT present. Tommy was told to have the car ready. When the gathering is called, Vinnie comes. Tommy stays at the stables. This sets up: Vinnie in the room denying, Tommy as the flight risk. Fenton will note Tommy's absence and send the constable.

### FENTON'S ADDRESS

Fenton doesn't do drama. He's methodical, quiet, devastating. He stands at the head of the table and lays out what he knows — piece by piece, in the order he assembled it:

1. Leonard Hargrove died of poisoning. The substance was administered in his whisky. The study was locked from inside; the killer left through the window.

2. Financial records found in the east wing cellar show Leonard was involved in a long-running criminal financial operation with Vincent Collaro. (Vinnie's face — the smile, for once, is gone.)

3. Items from the estate were systematically sold and replaced with reproductions. The proceeds were connected to the financial records. (Arthur's head bows.)

4. A witness saw Dolores Hargrove in the greenhouse handling oleander — a toxic plant — on Friday night. The same witness saw her leaving the greenhouse at midnight carrying a wrapped object and re-entering the house through the conservatory. (Dolores goes very still. Reg, across the room, is staring at the table.)

5. An unsigned will, drafted last week, would have cut Dolores Hargrove out of the estate entirely, leaving everything to Diana and Philip. Leonard intended to sign it Saturday morning. He was killed Friday night. (Arthur places the wills on the table with shaking hands. Diana's face goes white, then hard.)

6. Dolores Hargrove was seen tonight at a clandestine meeting in the hedge maze — a meeting arranged with the witness she knew could place her at the greenhouse. (Dolores's hand tightens on the table's edge.)

Fenton pauses. The room is utterly silent.

"Mrs. Hargrove. I am placing you under arrest on suspicion of the murder of Leonard Hargrove."

### DOLORES'S FINAL MOMENT

This is where Dolores's character gets her ending. She doesn't crumble. She doesn't confess. She stands. She looks at Fenton. She looks at the room — at Diana (hatred), at Arthur (contempt), at Frank (a long, evaluating stare), at Reg (cold fury), at the table, at the empty chair at the head, at the painting above the fireplace that isn't a Constable.

And she says: "I want my solicitor."

The room exhales.

Arthur, from his chair, says: "I am your solicitor, Mrs. Hargrove. And I am no longer available."

### THE COLLAROS

Fenton: "Where is Mr. Collaro's nephew?"

Vinnie, from his seat, face like stone: "How should I know? He's a grown man."

Fenton sends the constable to the stables. If Tommy is still there — he's caught. If Tommy has fled — Fenton will issue a description to the county police. Either way, the Collaros are finished at Hargrove Hall.

Fenton then turns to Vinnie: "Mr. Collaro. I have some questions about your financial relationship with the deceased. I'd suggest you don't leave this room."

Vinnie's smile returns. But it's not the same smile. It's the smile of a man who knows the game is over and is deciding how much of the table to flip on his way out.

- Dr. Webb, sitting alone, staring at his hands.
- Frank, standing by the window, looking out at the grounds.
- The house, in the grey light of approaching dawn.
