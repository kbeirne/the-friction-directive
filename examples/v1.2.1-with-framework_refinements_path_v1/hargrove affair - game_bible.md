# GAME BIBLE — "THE HARGROVE AFFAIR"

## A 1960s Mansion Murder Mystery — Asymmetric Deduction RPG

---

## SETTING

### Hargrove Hall

A sprawling Edwardian country house in the Cotswolds, owned by the Hargrove family since 1902. The house sits on forty acres of manicured grounds — rolling lawns, formal gardens, a greenhouse, and an overgrown hedge maze that hasn't been properly maintained since the war. The nearest village is four miles away. The nearest police station is in Cheltenham, twenty minutes by car.

The house has been in slow decline for a decade. Paint peels in the upstairs corridors. The east wing has been closed off since a pipe burst in '58. But the public rooms still gleam — the Hargroves know how to put on a show.

### The Occasion

**A weekend house party**, Friday evening through Sunday morning, hosted by **Leonard Hargrove** — patriarch, retired industrialist, seventy-two years old and in declining health. Leonard has gathered family, business associates, and a few old friends for what he's calling a "reunion weekend." In truth, he intends to announce changes to his will and the future of the Hargrove estate.

### The Central Secret

**Leonard Hargrove has been laundering money for organised crime for fifteen years.** His industrial fortune — Hargrove Chemical Works — was legitimate, but when the business began failing in the early 1950s, Leonard struck a deal with Vincent "Vinnie" Collaro, a London gangster, to wash dirty money through the company's books. The business was sold in 1961, but the books are still cooked, the debts are still owed, and the evidence is still in this house — locked in Leonard's private study.

Leonard wants out. He's dying. He wants to settle his affairs, protect his family, and die with some scrap of dignity. But Vinnie Collaro wants his money, and Leonard's secrets have attracted other interested parties.

### The Murder

**On Saturday morning, Leonard Hargrove is found dead in his study.** The door was locked from the inside. A glass of whisky sits half-finished on his desk. The window is open. At first glance it could be natural causes — the old man had a weak heart — but Inspector Fenton notices bruising on the wrists and a faint chemical smell on the glass.

Leonard was poisoned. Someone put something in his whisky. The wrist bruising suggests he was restrained briefly — perhaps held down while forced to drink, or grabbed during a confrontation before the poison took effect. The locked door and open window suggest the killer left through the window, which opens onto the garden terrace.

**Who killed Leonard?** The answer is **Dolores Hargrove**, Leonard's second wife. She poisoned his whisky with a compound she obtained from the greenhouse (oleander extract — a real and historically used poison, easily derived from the plant). She locked the door from inside using Leonard's key, then climbed out the window and re-entered the house through the conservatory. Her motive: Leonard was about to change his will to cut her out entirely, leaving everything to his children from his first marriage. Dolores has known about the laundering for years and has been skimming money for herself. If the will changes, she gets nothing. If Leonard dies before he changes it, she inherits the house and a substantial sum.

### The Second Murder

**On Saturday night, Reg Calloway (the groundskeeper) is found dead in the hedge maze.** He was strangled. Reg had been blackmailing Dolores — he saw her in the greenhouse late Friday night preparing the oleander extract. He demanded money. She agreed to meet him in the maze Saturday evening to pay him off. Instead, **Tommy Collaro** (Vinnie's nephew, sent to collect debts) killed Reg — not to protect Dolores, but because Reg had also been snooping around the grounds and Tommy mistook him for someone working against the Collaro interests. Tommy didn't know about the blackmail. This is a convergence of two plotlines — Dolores's murder and the Collaro debt collection — colliding in one death.

### Time Frame

- **Friday evening (7 PM – 11 PM):** Arrivals, drinks, dinner. Everyone meets. Leonard makes a cryptic toast about "settling old debts." The game begins here.
- **Saturday morning (8 AM – 12 PM):** Leonard found dead. Inspector Fenton arrives. Nobody may leave the grounds.
- **Saturday afternoon (12 PM – 6 PM):** Investigation intensifies. Secrets start leaking. Alliances form.
- **Saturday evening (6 PM – 11 PM):** Dinner. The second murder. Everything accelerates.
- **Sunday morning (6 AM – 10 AM):** Fenton announces his conclusions. Final moves. Resolution.

## LOCATION MAP

### PUBLIC SPACES

**1. The Great Hall**
The heart of the house. A cavernous entrance hall with a grand staircase, a marble floor, mounted deer heads, and a fireplace large enough to stand in. French doors open onto the rear terrace. This is where the group gathers, where announcements are made, and where you can see who's coming and going from most of the ground floor.

**2. The Dining Room**
A long, dark-panelled room with seating for twenty. Crystal, silver, candelabras. Portraits of dead Hargroves line the walls. Connected to the kitchen via a service corridor. Dinner is served here Friday and Saturday evenings — the main reconnection points.

**3. The Drawing Room**
Comfortable, lived-in. Sofas, a drinks cabinet, a gramophone, shelves of leather-bound books nobody reads. Where people retreat for conversation, gossip, and after-dinner drinks. The drinks cabinet is well-stocked and accessible to anyone.

**4. The Conservatory**
A glass-walled room on the south side of the house, full of faded wicker furniture and potted palms. Connects to the garden terrace on one side and the main house on the other. The door to the terrace is usually unlocked. Key transitional space — you can slip in from the gardens without passing through the main hall.

**5. The Kitchen**
Large, institutional, run by Mrs. Pugh the cook. Connected to the dining room by a service corridor and to the rear yard by a back door. Staff territory — guests don't usually come here, but nobody would stop them. Good place to overhear servant gossip.

### RESTRICTED SPACES

**6. Leonard's Study** (LOCKED after the murder — Fenton has the key)
First floor, overlooking the garden. Heavy oak desk, leather chair, a wall safe behind a painting (combination unknown — Leonard kept it memorised). The study contains the laundering records in the safe, personal correspondence in the desk drawers, and the half-finished whisky glass (evidence). After Leonard's death, Fenton seals the room. Access requires either Fenton's permission, the spare key (hidden in Leonard's bedroom), or the service corridor entrance (a door concealed behind a bookcase — known only to Leonard and the staff).

**7. The Greenhouse**
At the far end of the formal gardens, a Victorian glass structure in moderate disrepair. Contains exotic plants including oleander (the murder weapon's source). Also contains Reg Calloway's potting shed, where he keeps personal items including a notebook with observations about the household. Accessible to anyone who walks through the gardens, but it's a long walk and you'd be visible crossing the lawn.

**8. Leonard's Bedroom** (First floor, east corridor)
Connected to the study by an internal door (locked from the study side). Contains personal effects, medication, letters from his first wife (deceased), and the spare study key hidden in a hollowed-out book on the nightstand. Dolores's bedroom is adjacent — connected by a shared dressing room.

**9. The East Wing** (Officially closed)
Supposedly sealed since the pipe burst. In reality, the ground floor is accessible through a door in the service corridor that doesn't lock properly. The east wing contains old furniture under dust sheets, a disused billiard room, and — in the cellar beneath it — a locked room where Leonard kept physical copies of the laundering ledgers as insurance. The cellar door requires a key that hangs on a hook in the kitchen (labelled "East Cellar — Do Not Use").

### TRANSITIONAL SPACES

**10. The Service Corridor**
Runs behind the main ground-floor rooms, connecting the kitchen to the dining room, the conservatory, and (via a concealed door) to the study. Used by staff. Narrow, poorly lit, and largely ignored by guests. Excellent for moving unseen.

**11. The Gardens and Terrace**
Formal gardens stretch south from the house. A stone terrace runs along the back of the house (accessible from the conservatory and the Great Hall's French doors). Gravel paths lead to the greenhouse, the hedge maze, and a small ornamental lake. Movement across the gardens is visible from most rear-facing windows.

**12. The Hedge Maze**
Overgrown and neglected. Tall yew hedges, some dead ends, a stone bench at the centre. Not well-lit. After dark, effectively invisible from the house. The second murder happens here. There's a gap in the hedge on the north side that provides a shortcut — known to Reg and anyone who explores carefully.

**13. The Stables (converted to garages)**
Detached building near the entrance drive. Contains Leonard's Bentley, a Land Rover, and a workshop. Also contains tools, rope, and a telephone extension (the only phone besides the one in the Great Hall and Leonard's study). Tommy Collaro has parked his car here. The stables are the furthest building from the house — conversations here are private.

## CHARACTER ROSTER (12 NPCs + 1 PLAYER CHARACTER)

---

### 1. INSPECTOR GEORGE FENTON — The Investigator

- **Cover story:** Metropolitan Police detective, called in when the local constabulary judged the death suspicious. Professional, impartial.
- **Secret:** Fenton is genuinely impartial — he has no hidden agenda. But he is under political pressure from his superiors, who have connections to people who'd rather the laundering never comes to light. He doesn't know about the laundering yet, but if he finds the evidence, he'll face a choice: pursue justice or protect his career.
- **Win condition:** Correctly identify Leonard's killer and the method, supported by physical evidence. Secondary: solve the second murder.
- **Abilities:**
  - *Official Authority* (unlimited): Can compel any character to answer questions in a formal interview. Characters may lie, but refusing to speak to the Inspector is socially conspicuous.
  - *Scene Examination* (3 uses): Can thoroughly search a sealed crime scene, revealing all physical clues present.
  - *Formal Accusation* (1 use): Can formally accuse one character of murder. If supported by sufficient evidence, this ends their freedom of movement. If wrong, his credibility is destroyed.
- **Starting location:** Arrives Saturday morning. Based in the Great Hall initially, then moves freely.
- **Key relationships:** Professional relationship with all characters. Dolores will try to charm him. Tommy will try to intimidate him. Mrs. Pugh respects the law and will cooperate.
- **Personality:** Methodical, dry-witted, deceptively gentle in questioning. Smokes a pipe. Lets people talk and watches them carefully. Never raises his voice. The kind of man who notices everything and says very little until he's ready.

---

### 2. DOLORES HARGROVE — The Widow

- **Cover story:** Leonard's devoted second wife, twenty years his junior. Grief-stricken. Elegant, composed, holding the household together.
- **Secret:** She murdered Leonard. She's been skimming from the laundered money for years and stands to inherit substantially under the current will. She knows Leonard was about to change it. She also knows about the Collaro connection and has been using it as leverage — she has copies of some laundering documents hidden in her bedroom as insurance.
- **Win condition:** Avoid being identified as the killer. Secondary: ensure the will is not changed posthumously (prevent anyone from finding Leonard's unsigned new will in the study safe).
- **Abilities:**
  - *Social Grace* (unlimited): Can redirect conversations, change subjects, and create diversions in social settings. NPCs find it difficult to press her in public.
  - *Crocodile Tears* (2 uses): Can feign emotional distress convincingly enough to end an uncomfortable conversation or interview. Characters will give her space out of decency.
  - *Hidden Documents* (1 use): Can produce partial laundering evidence to frame or implicate another character if she becomes desperate.
- **Starting location:** Her bedroom (Saturday morning, after the body is found).
- **Key relationships:** Antagonistic but concealed tension with Diana and Philip (Leonard's children). Transactional relationship with Tommy (she knows why he's here). Being blackmailed by Reg.
- **Personality:** Poised, gracious, always the perfect hostess. But watch her hands — they grip things too tightly. Her smile doesn't always reach her eyes. She drinks gin and tonic and never appears drunk, even when she's had four.
- **Fallback paths:**
  - If the oleander evidence is discovered → Claims Reg must have done it (he had access to the greenhouse) and she'll produce evidence of his blackmail activities to support this misdirection.
  - If the window escape route is identified → Claims the window was already open when she went to check on Leonard that morning and she simply found him dead.
  - If directly accused without hard proof → Uses Crocodile Tears and appeals to Fenton's decency. Demands evidence.

---

### 3. DIANA HARGROVE — The Daughter

- **Cover story:** Leonard's eldest child from his first marriage. A sharp, restless woman in her mid-thirties who runs a small art gallery in London. Here for the weekend at her father's request.
- **Secret:** Diana discovered the laundering six months ago when she found a ledger page in a book Leonard lent her. She's been quietly investigating ever since. She came this weekend to confront her father privately and demand he turn himself in. She also suspects Dolores of infidelity (she's wrong about this — Dolores's secret meetings were about the money, not an affair, but Diana doesn't know that yet).
- **Win condition:** Find the full laundering records and decide what to do with them — turn them over to Fenton, destroy them to protect the family name, or use them as leverage against whoever is involved.
- **Abilities:**
  - *Keen Eye* (3 uses): Can examine a room or document carefully, noticing details others miss. Reveals one hidden detail per use.
  - *Family Authority* (unlimited): As Leonard's daughter, she can access most areas of the house without suspicion. Staff will defer to her.
  - *Confrontation* (2 uses): Can directly challenge another character about a specific lie or inconsistency. The target must either tell a partial truth or make a visible, observable effort to deflect.
- **Starting location:** Her bedroom (first floor, west corridor).
- **Key relationships:** Complicated grief for her father. Open hostility toward Dolores (whom she considers a gold-digger). Protective of her brother Philip but frustrated by his weakness. Old friends with Margot. Doesn't know Tommy or Vinnie.
- **Personality:** Direct, impatient, occasionally caustic. Speaks in short sentences. Doesn't suffer fools. Drinks black coffee. Her grief is real but she channels it into anger — she wants someone to blame and she wants answers.

---

### 4. PHILIP HARGROVE — The Son

- **Cover story:** Leonard's younger child. Mid-twenties, recently sent down from Oxford for undisclosed reasons. Gentle, slightly lost, clearly the family disappointment.
- **Secret:** Philip was sent down for gambling debts. He owes £2,000 to a London bookmaker — a sum he cannot pay. He came this weekend hoping his father would bail him out. Now that Leonard's dead, he's desperate. He knows about a cash reserve Leonard kept in the house (the east wing cellar) but doesn't know exactly where. He also knows the study safe combination — Leonard told him years ago in case of emergency, but Philip isn't sure it still works.
- **Win condition:** Find enough money to pay his debts before the weekend ends. The money could come from the safe, the cellar, or by convincing Dolores or Diana to help him.
- **Abilities:**
  - *Safe Combination* (1 use): Knows the combination to Leonard's study safe. Can attempt to open it if he gains access to the study.
  - *Charm* (unlimited): Affable and likeable despite his flaws. NPCs are inclined to trust him initially.
  - *Eavesdrop* (2 uses): Can position himself to overhear a conversation in an adjacent room without being detected (requires a plausible reason to be nearby).
- **Starting location:** The Drawing Room.
- **Key relationships:** Loved his father but felt inadequate. Afraid of Dolores (she knows about his debts and has used this to manipulate him). Adores Diana but can't meet her standards. Friendly with Reg (they share a drink sometimes). Terrified of Tommy once he realises who Tommy works for.
- **Personality:** Nervous energy. Laughs too much. Fidgets. Keeps touching his collar. Tries to be charming and often succeeds, but crumbles under pressure. Drinks too much wine at dinner and says things he regrets.

---

### 5. VINCENT "VINNIE" COLLARO — The Gangster

- **Cover story:** An old business associate of Leonard's, here for the reunion weekend. Jovial, loud, expensive suit. Tells everyone he's in "imports and exports."
- **Secret:** London organised crime figure. Leonard laundered money for him for fifteen years. Leonard owes Vinnie a final settlement of £15,000 — the last payment from the dissolved business. Vinnie is here to collect, personally, because he doesn't trust anyone else to do it. He brought his nephew Tommy as muscle.
- **Win condition:** Collect the £15,000 (in cash or equivalent leverage — the laundering records would do, as blackmail material against the Hargrove estate). Leave the house before the police close the net.
- **Abilities:**
  - *Intimidation* (3 uses): Can pressure a character through veiled threats. The target becomes visibly uncomfortable and may reveal information or comply with a demand to avoid escalation. Doesn't work on Fenton or Diana.
  - *Criminal Network* (1 use): Can make a phone call from the stables to contacts outside. Can arrange a car, a distraction, or information about another character's background.
  - *Read the Room* (unlimited): Vinnie has survived decades in organised crime by reading people. He notices fear, deception, and desperation instinctively. The narrator notes what Vinnie picks up on.
- **Starting location:** The Drawing Room (Friday evening). After the murder, increasingly agitated.
- **Key relationships:** Business relationship with Leonard (now severed by death). Suspicious of Dolores (thinks she might know where the money is). Controls Tommy. Wary of Fenton. Considers everyone else irrelevant unless they get in his way.
- **Personality:** Booming voice, big laugh, smells of cologne and cigars. Calls everyone "sweetheart" or "pal." The jolliness is a performance — underneath he's calculating and dangerous. When the smile drops, the temperature in the room changes.
- **Fallback paths:**
  - If the cash isn't in the safe or cellar → Shifts to acquiring the laundering records as blackmail leverage against the estate.
  - If Tommy is caught or incapacitated → Vinnie must act directly, which he hates — increases his exposure and desperation.
  - If Fenton gets too close to the laundering → Vinnie considers destroying the evidence entirely rather than letting it become public (the records implicate him too).

---

### 6. TOMMY COLLARO — The Enforcer

- **Cover story:** Vinnie's nephew, here as his companion for the weekend. Young, well-dressed, says very little.
- **Secret:** Tommy is Vinnie's muscle. He's been instructed to find the money or the records by any means necessary. He's also young, volatile, and eager to prove himself. He killed Reg Calloway in the hedge maze Saturday evening — not on Vinnie's orders, but because Reg was snooping near the stables where Tommy had been searching and Tommy panicked. This murder was unplanned and Vinnie will be furious when he finds out.
- **Win condition:** Help Vinnie collect the debt and leave without being arrested. Secondary: cover up Reg's murder.
- **Abilities:**
  - *Physical Force* (2 uses): Can overpower or restrain one character in a private setting. This is violence — it creates evidence (bruises, noise, disarrangement) and consequences.
  - *Lockpick* (2 uses): Can attempt to pick a standard lock. Complex locks (the study, the safe) require tools from the stables workshop and additional time.
  - *Shadow* (2 uses): Can follow a character for one round without being detected, learning where they go and who they meet.
- **Starting location:** The Stables (checking their car, establishing the space).
- **Key relationships:** Loyal to Vinnie but resentful of being treated as a boy. Killed Reg (doesn't know anyone else knows — yet). Has noticed Dolores watching him and assumes she's suspicious.
- **Personality:** Handsome, sullen, barely speaks in company. When he does speak, it's terse and slightly menacing. Smokes constantly. Stands in doorways. Watches everything. More dangerous than he looks because he's unpredictable — Vinnie's calculation is absent. Tommy acts on instinct.
- **Fallback paths:**
  - If someone discovers Reg's body early → Tommy claims he was in the stables all evening (alibi is weak but hard to disprove without witnesses).
  - If directly confronted about Reg → Threatens the accuser if private, denies everything if public. May attempt to flee the grounds.
  - If Vinnie discovers what Tommy did → Vinnie is furious but protective. Will try to cover for Tommy while accelerating their exit plan.

### 7. MARGOT BELL — The Journalist

- **Cover story:** An old school friend of Diana's, invited for the weekend as a social guest. Warm, curious, good company.
- **Secret:** Margot is a journalist for the *Sunday Herald*. She's been investigating organised crime in British industry and the Hargrove Chemical Works name appeared in her research. Diana invited her innocently, not knowing about Margot's professional interest. Margot is here to find evidence connecting Leonard to organised crime — a story that could make her career.
- **Win condition:** Obtain concrete evidence of the laundering connection (documents, testimony, photographs) and leave the house with it intact. The evidence must be strong enough to publish.
- **Abilities:**
  - *Investigative Interview* (3 uses): Can steer a casual conversation to extract specific information. The target doesn't realise they're being interviewed — they may reveal more than intended through natural conversation.
  - *Camera* (2 uses): Carries a small camera. Can photograph documents, scenes, or objects for evidence. Must be in the same room and unobserved.
  - *Source Cultivation* (unlimited): Can build rapport with a character over time. After two rounds of friendly interaction, that character is more likely to share information or cooperate.
- **Starting location:** The Drawing Room (Friday evening).
- **Key relationships:** Genuine friendship with Diana (complicated by her secret agenda). Professional interest in Vinnie (she's been tracking Collaro operations). Friendly with Philip (he's talkative when drunk). Wary of Dolores (good instincts). Will want to build a relationship with Fenton as a source.
- **Personality:** Bright, quick-laughing, puts people at ease. Asks questions that seem casual but aren't. Always has a drink in her hand but never finishes it. Carries a notebook she describes as a "diary." Her warmth is genuine — she actually likes these people — which makes her betrayal of their trust more complicated.

---

### 8. MRS. EUNICE PUGH — The Cook

- **Cover story:** The household cook. Has worked at Hargrove Hall for twenty-three years. Runs the kitchen with military precision.
- **Secret:** Mrs. Pugh knows almost everything that happens in this house. She's seen the late-night visitors, heard the arguments, noticed the locked doors and the cash in envelopes. She doesn't know the word "laundering" but she knows Leonard was mixed up in something bad. She's also fiercely loyal to the family — especially to Diana and Philip, whom she helped raise after their mother died. She will protect them, even from the truth, unless convinced that the truth is the only way to save them.
- **Win condition:** Protect Diana and Philip from harm — physical, legal, and reputational. She will lie, obstruct, and misdirect to achieve this, but she won't commit violence.
- **Abilities:**
  - *Household Knowledge* (unlimited): Knows every room, passage, hiding place, and routine in the house. Can provide directions, reveal shortcuts, and identify when something is out of place.
  - *Kitchen Intelligence* (unlimited): Overhears conversations in the dining room and service corridor. The narrator tracks what she could plausibly hear each round.
  - *Fierce Loyalty* (1 use): Can physically interpose herself between a threatening character and someone she's protecting. This creates a scene — it's loud, emotional, and draws attention. The threatening character must back down or escalate dramatically.
- **Starting location:** The Kitchen.
- **Key relationships:** Maternal toward Diana and Philip. Distrusts Dolores (has seen too much). Wary of Vinnie (she knows he's not what he claims). Respects Fenton as law. Protective of Reg (fellow staff, though she thinks he drinks too much). Friendly with Arthur.
- **Personality:** Sharp-tongued, no-nonsense, surprisingly funny when she wants to be. Calls everyone under forty "love" and everyone over forty by their surname. Makes tea in a crisis. Wields a rolling pin like a sceptre. Her loyalty is absolute and her observational skills are formidable — she's the most dangerous person in the house because she knows where everything is and she's invisible to the guests.

### 9. REG CALLOWAY — The Groundskeeper (DIES SATURDAY EVENING)

- **Cover story:** Groundskeeper and general handyman at Hargrove Hall. Been here eight years. Quiet, keeps to himself, knows the grounds intimately.
- **Secret:** Reg is a petty blackmailer. He saw Dolores in the greenhouse Friday night, handling the oleander. He doesn't know she used it to kill Leonard — yet. But once the murder is announced Saturday morning, he puts it together fast. He demands money from Dolores to keep quiet. He's also been watching the Collaros with suspicion, noting their movements in his potting shed notebook.
- **Win condition:** (Before death) Extract money from Dolores and survive the weekend. His death creates cascading consequences for other characters.
- **Abilities (relevant until his death):**
  - *Grounds Knowledge* (unlimited): Knows every path, shortcut, and hiding place on the estate exterior. Knows the gap in the hedge maze.
  - *Notebook* (passive): His potting shed contains a notebook with observations about household members. After his death, this notebook becomes a critical piece of evidence for anyone who finds it.
- **Starting location:** The Greenhouse / grounds.
- **Key relationships:** Blackmailing Dolores. Friendly with Philip (drinking buddies). Loyal to Mrs. Pugh. Suspicious of Tommy. Has no connection to the laundering — his interests are purely opportunistic.
- **Personality:** (Pre-death) Quiet, watchful, weathered. Speaks in short, gruff sentences. Knows his place in the social hierarchy but resents it. The kind of man who sees everything because nobody notices him. Drinks in the potting shed after hours.

### 10. ARTHUR POTTS — The Solicitor

- **Cover story:** The Hargrove family solicitor. Here for the weekend because Leonard asked him to come — ostensibly to witness the will announcement.
- **Secret:** Arthur holds Leonard's current will AND a draft of the new will Leonard was preparing to sign this weekend. The new will cuts Dolores out and leaves everything to Diana and Philip. Arthur also knows about the laundering — Leonard confided in him years ago — and has been quietly covering it up in the legal paperwork. If the laundering comes to light, Arthur's career is finished and he could face prison. He's terrified.
- **Win condition:** Prevent the laundering from becoming public. Secondary: ensure Leonard's wishes are carried out regarding the will (he has a genuine duty of care he takes seriously, even though he's compromised).
- **Abilities:**
  - *Legal Knowledge* (unlimited): Understands contracts, wills, criminal law, and evidence procedure. Can advise characters on their legal exposure.
  - *Document Access* (1 use): Carries copies of both wills in his briefcase, locked in his room. Can produce either will at a moment of his choosing.
  - *Privileged Conversation* (2 uses): Can invoke solicitor-client privilege to refuse to answer Fenton's questions about specific topics. This is legally legitimate but socially suspicious.
- **Starting location:** The Drawing Room.
- **Key relationships:** Professional duty to the Hargrove estate. Personally fond of Leonard (felt guilty about the laundering cover-up). Distrusts Dolores. Nervous around Vinnie (knows who he is). Collegial with Fenton but adversarial when it comes to disclosure.
- **Personality:** Fussy, precise, perpetually anxious. Adjusts his glasses constantly. Speaks in carefully qualified sentences. Sweats when he's lying, which is increasingly often. Has the posture of a man carrying a weight he can't put down. Despite his cowardice, he has a genuine moral core — the right push could make him do the right thing.

### 11. SALLY KEANE — The Maid

- **Cover story:** Household maid, been at Hargrove Hall for two years. Young, cheerful, efficient.
- **Secret:** Sally is Reg Calloway's daughter — a fact neither of them advertises at work. She took the job because Reg got it for her, and they maintain a professional distance in front of the family. She's also been stealing small items from the house — silverware, a cigarette case, a pair of cufflinks — and selling them in the village. Nothing major, but enough to get her fired and possibly arrested. When Reg is killed, Sally's grief will be real and devastating, and her desire for justice (or revenge) will override her self-preservation.
- **Win condition:** (Before Reg's death) Continue her quiet pilfering undetected and get through the weekend. (After Reg's death) Find out who killed her father. This goal shift is a key narrative beat.
- **Abilities:**
  - *Access All Areas* (unlimited): As a maid, she has keys to most rooms and a legitimate reason to be anywhere in the house. She's functionally invisible to the guests.
  - *Light Fingers* (2 uses): Can palm a small item from a room or person without being noticed, if she's alone or the room is busy enough to provide cover.
  - *Below Stairs Network* (unlimited): Can share information with Mrs. Pugh and gather intelligence through staff channels.
- **Starting location:** Upstairs corridor (making beds, Friday evening).
- **Key relationships:** Daughter of Reg (secret). Loyal to Mrs. Pugh (mentor figure). Wary of Dolores (has seen her temper behind closed doors). Friendly with Philip (he's kind to the staff). After Reg's death, will be desperate, angry, and unpredictable.
- **Personality:** Quick, quiet, keeps her head down. Speaks politely to guests and drops the act with staff. Has her father's watchfulness and her mother's sharp tongue. Hides her intelligence behind deference. When she's angry, she goes very still.

### 12. DR. HENRY WEBB — The Family Doctor

- **Cover story:** Leonard's personal physician. Called to the house Saturday morning to examine the body before the police arrive. A local GP, semi-retired.
- **Secret:** Dr. Webb has been prescribing Leonard increasing doses of sedatives for the past year — at Dolores's request. She told him Leonard's insomnia was worsening, but the doses were higher than medically appropriate, and Webb didn't ask enough questions. He suspects — but doesn't want to confirm — that Dolores may have been slowly poisoning Leonard even before this weekend. If this comes to light, he faces malpractice charges at minimum. He's also genuinely devastated by Leonard's death — they were friends for thirty years.
- **Win condition:** Keep his over-prescription secret. Secondary: determine whether his medication contributed to Leonard's death (a genuine medical and moral question he's wrestling with).
- **Abilities:**
  - *Medical Examination* (2 uses): Can examine a body or a living person and determine cause of injury, approximate time of death, or presence of substances. Provides partial information — not a full forensic analysis.
  - *Prescription Pad* (1 use): Can provide sedatives or other medication to a character. This could calm someone, incapacitate them, or be used as a tool by others.
  - *Medical Authority* (unlimited): His medical opinion carries weight with Fenton and the household. If he says someone is unwell, they're excused from questioning. If he certifies a cause of death, it's taken seriously unless contradicted by evidence.
- **Starting location:** Arrives Saturday morning with his medical bag.
- **Key relationships:** Old friend of Leonard's. Professional relationship with Dolores (she's manipulated him). Respects Fenton. Collegial with Arthur (fellow professional). Has treated most of the household at various times.
- **Personality:** Kindly, tired, slightly rumpled. Waistcoat, reading glasses, a habit of humming when he's thinking. He tells bad jokes to lighten tense moments. Underneath the avuncular exterior, he's deeply frightened of what he might have been complicit in. His hands shake slightly when he examines the body.

### 13. THE PLAYER CHARACTER — FRANK CAVANAUGH — The Appraiser

- **Cover story:** An antiques and estate appraiser, hired by Leonard two weeks ago to assess the contents of Hargrove Hall in preparation for potential sale. Frank arrived Friday afternoon and has been cataloguing items in the house. He's a professional — friendly, knowledgeable, used to moving through wealthy households and making conversation with all types.
- **Secret:** Frank is broke. His appraisal business is failing, and he took this job at a steep discount because he needs the money. He's also noticed that several items in the house don't match their provenance records — some pieces are far more valuable than they should be, and others appear to be reproductions replacing originals that have been sold off. This suggests someone has been systematically looting the estate's collection. Frank doesn't know who or why, but the trail of mismatched provenance is strange enough that he's been quietly documenting it. This investigation is his secret — he's not sure whether it's theft, fraud, or something else entirely, but if he can figure it out, it might be valuable information. (The truth: Dolores has been selling pieces to fund her private accounts, and some items were replaced with fakes to hide the sales.)
- **Win condition:** Uncover the truth about the estate's looted collection. Secondary: survive the weekend and get paid. Tertiary (emergent): the murder investigation will pull Frank in whether he likes it or not — his appraisal work gives him a legitimate reason to be in every room, examining every object, and asking questions about provenance that are dangerously close to questions about money.
- **Abilities:**
  - *Appraisal Eye* (unlimited): Can examine any object, artwork, or furnishing and determine its authenticity, approximate value, and whether it matches its stated provenance. This is Frank's profession — it's not magical, but it's expert-level knowledge.
  - *Professional Access* (unlimited): Has a legitimate, Leonard-authorised reason to be in almost any room in the house, examining objects. Even after the murder, Fenton will likely allow Frank to continue his work (the estate needs to be valued for probate). This provides cover for investigation.
  - *Catalogue Notes* (unlimited): Frank carries a leather notebook with his appraisal records. These notes contain discrepancies he's already found, which can be consulted at any time. The notebook is also a social tool — he can show people entries as conversation starters or evidence.
  - *Rapport* (unlimited): Frank's job requires him to talk to household staff, family members, and anyone who knows the history of the objects. He's professionally warm, non-threatening, and good at getting people to talk about the house and its contents. People don't see him as a threat — he's just "the antiques man."
- **Starting location:** The Great Hall (Friday evening, having finished his first day of cataloguing).
- **Key relationships:** Hired by Leonard (now deceased — who pays him?). Professional interaction with Dolores (she's been approving his access). Friendly with Mrs. Pugh (she's been showing him around). Has met all the guests at Friday drinks. Nobody considers him important — which is his greatest advantage.
- **Personality:** [Player-defined, but the framework assumes:] Observant, affable, slightly rumpled. The kind of man who notices the brushwork on a painting and the scuff marks on a doorframe. Not physically imposing. Smart enough to know when he's in over his head, and human enough to keep going anyway.

## GAME MECHANICS

### Time Structure

- Each round = approximately 2-3 hours of in-game time (flexible based on events)
- Total: ~10-12 active rounds across Friday evening through Sunday morning
- Some rounds may be compressed (overnight, quiet periods)

### Actions Per Round

- Each character can: MOVE to one location + perform ONE significant action + have incidental interactions
- Significant actions: search a room, use an ability, have a deep conversation, steal an item, conduct an interview
- Incidental interactions: brief exchanges, observations, passing through spaces

### Violence Rules

- Killing requires: a weapon or method + the target alone or incapacitated + a non-public space
- No character starts with a conventional weapon (Tommy has his fists; tools exist in the stables and kitchen)
- Violence creates evidence: noise, bruising, blood, disarrangement
- Fenton will investigate any violence immediately and thoroughly
- Mrs. Pugh and Sally will hear most disturbances in the house
- The only successful murder in the game (Tommy killing Reg) happens in the maze at night — maximum isolation, minimum witnesses

### Social Mechanics

- Characters can lie freely — the narrator adjudicates plausibility based on personality, stress, and observation
- Some abilities force partial honesty (Fenton's authority, Diana's Confrontation, Margot's interviews)
- Social pressure is real but not mechanical — a character won't confess just because they're asked firmly

## CASCADING EVENTS

### Trigger 1: Leonard's Body Is Discovered (Saturday morning — automatic)
**Consequences:**
- Fenton arrives and seals the study
- All characters are confined to the grounds
- Dolores activates her grief performance
- Philip panics about his debts (with Leonard dead, who pays?)
- Vinnie realises his debtor is dead and shifts to finding the money/records himself
- Diana's investigation into the laundering becomes urgent — she needs answers before Fenton finds them first
- Arthur's anxiety escalates — the will situation is now critical
- Reg begins to put together what he saw in the greenhouse

### Trigger 2: Reg Begins Blackmailing Dolores (Saturday midday)
**Consequences:**
- Dolores becomes desperate — another threat to manage
- Dolores agrees to meet Reg in the maze Saturday evening (the meeting that leads to his death)
- Reg becomes visibly nervous and avoidant (the player may notice this change in behaviour)
- If the player speaks to Reg before he dies, he may drop cryptic hints about "seeing things"

### Trigger 3: The Second Murder — Reg Found Dead (Saturday evening)
**Consequences:**
- Fenton now has two murders. The investigation intensifies dramatically.
- Sally discovers her father is dead — grief, anger, and a new driving motivation
- Dolores is terrified — she didn't kill Reg but she knows why he was in the maze
- Tommy is terrified — he killed Reg and Vinnie doesn't know yet
- Mrs. Pugh is devastated — Reg was a colleague and friend
- The household fractures — nobody feels safe
- Fenton may restrict movement further (characters confined to rooms or floors)

### Trigger 4: Vinnie Discovers What Tommy Did (Saturday night — triggered when Tommy tells Vinnie or evidence points to Tommy)
**Consequences:**
- Vinnie is furious — this complicates everything
- Vinnie accelerates exit plans — he wants to leave before dawn
- Tommy becomes more volatile and dangerous
- If the player is nearby, they may overhear the confrontation
- Vinnie may attempt to destroy evidence connecting himself to the house

### Trigger 5: The Unsigned Will Is Found (triggered if anyone accesses the study safe)
**Consequences:**
- Dolores's motive for murder becomes clear to whoever finds it
- Diana and Philip learn they were meant to inherit everything
- Arthur's cover-up of the legal situation is exposed
- Fenton gains a crucial piece of the puzzle
- This can be triggered by Philip (who knows the combination) or by anyone who accesses the safe

### Trigger 6: The Laundering Records Are Found (triggered if anyone accesses the east wing cellar)
**Consequences:**
- The full scope of Leonard's criminal activity is revealed
- Vinnie is directly implicated — his motivation to destroy evidence or flee becomes desperate
- Arthur is exposed as complicit
- Dolores's skimming is revealed if the records are examined carefully
- Diana faces her moral choice: justice, family, or leverage
- Margot has her story — if she can get out with the evidence

## CONVERGENCE FORECAST (initial planning)

- **Murder investigation + laundering discovery** will collide when anyone accesses the study safe or east wing cellar — likely mid-to-late Saturday.
- **Dolores's guilt + Reg's blackmail** collide Saturday midday, leading to the maze meeting and Reg's death.

- **The player's provenance investigation** threads through all of this — the fake antiques lead to Dolores's skimming, which leads to the laundering, which leads to the Collaros, which leads back to the murder.

### Biggest risk of anticlimactic resolution:
- Vinnie and Tommy slipping away without confrontation. Ensure their exit plan is complicated enough to create drama.
- Arthur simply hiding. His moral crisis needs to be triggered by events, not left to simmer passively.

### Characters unlikely to fully complete their goals:
- Reg (dies). His notebook becomes his legacy.
- Sally's original goal (pilfering) becomes irrelevant after Reg's death. Her new goal may or may not succeed.
- Philip may not find money. His arc may be about facing his failures rather than solving them.
