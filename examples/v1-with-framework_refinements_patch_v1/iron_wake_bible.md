## SETTING

**The Aurelia Express**, a private luxury train belonging to the late **Admiral Erasmus Harlow**, makes its final overnight journey through the Karst Mountain passes from the coastal city of Varre to the inland capital of Ashmark. It is December, 1889. Snow falls heavily. The passes are treacherous.

Admiral Harlow died in his private car approximately two hours before the train departed. His solicitor, **Mr. Pemberton**, has gathered fourteen individuals aboard for the reading of the will at dawn in Ashmark. But the train won't reach Ashmark until 7:00 AM, and the night is long.

The Aurelia Express is a six-car train:
- Engine & Coal Car (inaccessible — crew only)
- First Class Carriage (passenger compartments)
- The Dining Car
- The Saloon Car (bar, lounge, card tables)
- The Admiral's Private Car (sealed — contains the body and the will)
- The Luggage Van (rear — storage, staff quarters)

The train departed Varre at 8:00 PM. It will stop twice: **Greyhollow Station** (~11:00 PM, a brief platform stop in a mountain village) and **Ironbridge Junction** (~3:00 AM, a switching point where the train changes tracks). Between stops, no one can leave. The mountains are dark, cold, and lethal in winter.

**The central secret:** Admiral Harlow was not merely a decorated naval officer. In his final years he became obsessed with an artifact recovered from a shipwreck off the Kelden coast — a **small iron compass** that does not point north. It points toward something else. The Admiral believed it pointed toward the boundary between life and death, and his private journals (locked in his car) describe experiments in communicating with the dead. His death may be connected to these experiments. The compass is among his effects in the sealed car, and several passengers want it for very different reasons.

**The Admiral's death — the truth (known only to the narrator):**
Admiral Harlow died of heart failure, but it was not natural. He was slowly poisoned over the preceding weeks by **arsenic administered in his evening tea** by his valet, **Colm Bryce**, who was paid to do so by **Mrs. Lillian Harlow**, the Admiral's estranged wife. She wanted the inheritance before Harlow could change his will to disinherit her (which he was planning to do). However, the Admiral's obsessive use of the iron compass in his final hours may have *accelerated* his death — the artifact has a genuine supernatural property: prolonged contact drains vitality from the living. So the truth is layered: Lillian ordered the poisoning, Colm administered it, but the compass contributed to the death's timing. This ambiguity should persist throughout the game. Characters may discover the poison, or the compass's properties, or both, or neither — but the full picture requires all three pieces.

## TRAIN MAP & LOCATIONS

### PUBLIC SPACES

**1. The Dining Car**
- Central social space. Long table with white linen, silver service, gas lamps. A steward serves drinks and light meals throughout the night.
- All passengers gather here for the initial meeting and reconnection beats.
- Observable: anyone present can see everyone else. Conversations can be overheard if nearby.

**2. The Saloon Car**
- Behind the Dining Car. Leather armchairs, a small bar, card tables, bookshelves, a coal stove. More informal than the Dining Car.
- Characters can cluster in small groups with relative privacy — the car has alcoves and wing-backed chairs that create semi-private spaces.
- Contains a writing desk with stationery (useful for forging or composing notes).

**3. The First Class Corridor**
- The carriage ahead of the Dining Car. A narrow corridor with doors to six private compartments (two passengers per compartment, assigned by Mr. Pemberton).
- Compartment assignments create forced proximity between specific characters.
- The corridor itself is a transitional space — encounters happen here when characters move between the forward compartments and the public cars.

### RESTRICTED SPACES

**4. The Admiral's Private Car**
- Sealed by Mr. Pemberton. He carries the only key. Contains: the Admiral's body (laid out on the bed), his personal effects, the iron compass, his private journals, the will (in a locked strongbox — Pemberton has this key too), and a small collection of maritime artifacts.
- Pemberton has stated no one may enter until the reading of the will at dawn.
- Can be accessed by: stealing or copying Pemberton's key, picking the lock (difficult — it's a high-quality lock), convincing Pemberton to open it (very difficult — he takes his duty seriously), or accessing from the Luggage Van via a connecting door (locked from the Admiral's side but the lock is simpler).

**5. The Luggage Van**
- Rear car. Cold, dark, stacked with trunks and cases. A small partitioned area for train staff.
- Contains passengers' checked luggage (some containing secrets — weapons, documents, disguises).
- The rear connecting door to the Admiral's car is locked but accessible with basic lockpicking tools or force.
- Also contains a telegraph apparatus that connects to stations when the train is within range of the wire (only at Greyhollow and Ironbridge). A character who reaches the telegraph could send a message — to summon police, to alert an accomplice at the destination, etc.

**6. Compartments (6 individual rooms)**
- Small, private. Two berths each. A window (sealed — cannot be opened while moving), a small table, a washstand.
- Private conversations here are truly private — no one can overhear through the heavy doors.
- Compartment assignments (designed to create tension):
  - **Compartment A:** Mrs. Lillian Harlow & Miss Cordelia Vane
  - **Compartment B:** Colonel Marsh & Dr. Ashworth
  - **Compartment C:** Mr. Pemberton & Reverend Doyle
  - **Compartment D:** Mr. Lazar Kessler & Señora Vega
  - **Compartment E:** Inspector Crowe & Mr. Falk
  - **Compartment F:** The Player Character & Miss Wynn

### TRANSITIONAL SPACES

**7. The Vestibules**
- The connecting passages between cars. Cold, loud (the noise of the train), semi-enclosed. Snow blows in through gaps.
- Where private conversations happen if you don't want to be in a compartment. Also where confrontations occur — narrow, nowhere to run.
- A character could be ambushed here but the noise makes stealth difficult.

**8. The Platform (at station stops only)**
- Available only at Greyhollow (11:00 PM) and Ironbridge Junction (3:00 AM).
- Briefly allows characters to step outside, meet anyone on the platform, potentially board or leave the train.
- Greyhollow has a small station house with a stove, a telegraph office, and a stationmaster.
- Ironbridge is unmanned — just a switching point. Dark, cold, isolated. The train stops for 10 minutes.

## ROLES (14 Total)

### PUBLIC ROLES

**1. MR. PEMBERTON — The Solicitor (NPC/Host equivalent)**
- **Public identity:** Admiral Harlow's solicitor, executor of the will, organizer of this gathering.
- **Secret:** Pemberton knows the will's contents but is legally bound not to reveal them until the formal reading. The will is explosive: Harlow disinherited his wife and left everything to someone unexpected. Pemberton also suspects the death wasn't natural — the Admiral was too healthy to simply drop dead — but he has no proof and is afraid of what he might find if he looks.
- **Win condition:** NONE (passive role — NPC). He wants to reach Ashmark, read the will, and discharge his duty. He will resist attempts to open the Admiral's car early. If presented with evidence of murder, his duty shifts to preserving evidence.
- **Abilities:** Carries both keys (Admiral's car door + will strongbox). Legal authority — passengers respect his role. Can invoke the Admiral's authority: "The Admiral's instructions were clear." Meticulous — notices when things are out of place.
- **Starting location:** Dining Car
- **Personality:** Fussy, principled, privately terrified. Drinks too much tea. Speaks in legal qualifications ("as it were," "in a manner of speaking").

---

### SECRET ROLES

**2. MRS. LILLIAN HARLOW — The Widow**
- **Cover story:** The Admiral's grieving wife, here for the reading of the will
- **Secret:** She arranged her husband's murder through the valet Colm Bryce. She has been slowly poisoning Harlow with arsenic over weeks. She believes the will leaves everything to her. She does not yet know Harlow changed the will to disinherit her — when she finds out, she'll become desperate.
- **Win condition:** Ensure the will is read as planned (she believes it favors her) AND prevent anyone from discovering the poisoning. If she learns the will disinherits her, her goal shifts to destroying or altering the will before dawn.
- **Fallback (if poisoning suspected):** Frame someone else — she carries a small quantity of arsenic that could be planted in another passenger's belongings. Cost: risk of discovery during the planting.
- **Abilities:** Social authority as the widow — people defer to her grief. Skilled manipulator. Carries a concealed packet of arsenic. Can send private instructions to Colm Bryce via handwritten notes.
- **Starting location:** Dining Car
- **Key relationships:** Secret employer of Colm Bryce (the valet). Hostile to anyone investigating the death. Unaware the will has changed.
- **Personality:** Composed, elegant, grieving just enough. Cold underneath. Every gesture calculated.

**3. COLM BRYCE — The Valet**
- **Cover story:** The Admiral's loyal personal valet, devastated by his master's death
- **Secret:** He administered the arsenic on Lillian's orders. He was paid handsomely but is consumed with guilt. He knows exactly how the Admiral died and carries the knowledge like a stone in his chest.
- **Win condition:** Survive the night without being exposed. If the poisoning is discovered, escape at one of the station stops. Secondary: he genuinely wants to know what the compass is — the Admiral spoke of it in his final days with a reverence that frightened Colm.
- **Fallback (if suspected):** Confess and implicate Lillian — trade his testimony for mercy. Cost: breaks his agreement with Lillian and makes a powerful enemy. But survival trumps loyalty.
- **Abilities:** Intimate knowledge of the Admiral's habits, possessions, and the layout of the private car. Carries a key to the Admiral's personal effects chest (NOT the car door — he needed Pemberton's permission to retrieve items). Can move through the service areas of the train without attracting notice. Knows the poisoning details.
- **Starting location:** Luggage Van (organizing the Admiral's effects)
- **Key relationships:** Controlled by Lillian. Guilty. Knows the private car's contents intimately. May seek absolution from the Reverend.
- **Personality:** Quiet, precise, haunted. Hands that shake when he thinks no one is watching. Reflexively deferential.

**4. COLONEL JAMES MARSH — The Old Friend**
- **Cover story:** A retired army colonel, the Admiral's oldest friend, here to pay respects
- **Secret:** He and the Admiral had a falling out five years ago over a disastrous military campaign in which Harlow's naval intelligence was wrong, resulting in the deaths of men under Marsh's command. Marsh publicly forgave Harlow but privately never did. He's here because the Admiral's letters hinted at possessing "proof" that the intelligence failure was sabotage, not incompetence — proof that could exonerate Marsh's reputation. He believes this proof is in the Admiral's private car.
- **Win condition:** Access the Admiral's car and find the intelligence documents. If they prove sabotage, take them. If they don't exist, Marsh must accept his ruined reputation.
- **Fallback (if car inaccessible):** Convince Pemberton to search for specific documents on his behalf. Cost: reveals his motive and makes him a suspect if foul play is discovered.
- **Abilities:** Military authority — commands respect, accustomed to issuing orders. Physically strong despite his age. Experienced interrogator — can pressure people into revealing information through sheer force of presence. Knows the Admiral's naval career in detail.
- **Starting location:** Dining Car
- **Key relationships:** Old friend/enemy of the Admiral. Shares compartment with Dr. Ashworth. Suspicious of anyone else trying to access the Admiral's car.
- **Personality:** Gruff, upright, carrying decades of repressed anger. Drinks whisky. Doesn't suffer fools. Unexpectedly tender when he lets his guard down.

**5. DR. HELENA ASHWORTH — The Physician**
- **Cover story:** The Admiral's personal physician, here because she was attending him in his final weeks
- **Secret:** She suspects the Admiral was poisoned. She noticed symptoms in his final days that were consistent with chronic arsenic exposure but she didn't act on her suspicions in time. She carries tremendous guilt and has come aboard determined to examine the body and confirm or deny her fears. She also knows about the compass — the Admiral showed it to her and she witnessed its unsettling effect on him. She is a woman of science struggling with something she cannot explain rationally.
- **Win condition:** Examine the Admiral's body and determine the cause of death. If it's poison, identify the poisoner. Secondary: understand the compass's nature — is there a rational explanation?
- **Fallback (if body inaccessible):** Examine the Admiral's medicines, his teacup, his food — traces of arsenic might be found in his belongings in the Luggage Van. Cost: less conclusive than a body examination.
- **Abilities:** Medical expertise — can identify poisons, treat injuries, assess physical evidence. The Admiral showed her the compass — she knows it exists and what it looks like. Analytical mind — gets extra detail when examining physical evidence. Carries a medical bag with basic instruments and chemicals (including reagents that can detect arsenic).
- **Starting location:** Dining Car
- **Key relationships:** Suspects Colm Bryce (he prepared the Admiral's tea). Shares compartment with Colonel Marsh. Scientifically fascinated by the compass.
- **Personality:** Precise, compassionate, quietly fierce. Speaks carefully. Doesn't raise her voice — doesn't need to.

**6. REVEREND THOMAS DOYLE — The Chaplain**
- **Cover story:** A clergyman who ministered to the Admiral spiritually in his final months
- **Secret:** The Admiral confessed something to Doyle in their last meeting — not the details of his death, but the nature of his experiments with the compass. Harlow told Doyle he had "spoken to someone on the other side" and that the experience terrified him. Doyle is shaken to his core. If communication with the dead is real, everything he's built his faith on is either vindicated or destroyed — he doesn't know which. He needs the compass to understand what the Admiral experienced.
- **Win condition:** Obtain the compass and test it himself — either to prove it's a fraud (preserving his rational faith) or to confirm its power (shattering and remaking his understanding of God). This is a deeply personal, non-violent goal.
- **Fallback (if compass inaccessible):** Find the Admiral's journals, which describe the experiments in detail. Cost: secondhand knowledge, not direct experience. The doubt remains.
- **Abilities:** Trusted confessor — people feel compelled to be honest with him. Can sense when someone is carrying guilt (reads body language and speech patterns with pastoral expertise). Knows details of the Admiral's spiritual crisis. Shares compartment with Pemberton — proximity to the man with the keys.
- **Starting location:** Dining Car
- **Key relationships:** Knows about the compass from the Admiral's confession. Shares compartment with Pemberton (opportunity and obligation). May conflict with Dr. Ashworth (faith vs. science regarding the compass).
- **Personality:** Warm, troubled, intellectually honest. Wrings his hands when anxious. Has a habit of quoting scripture and then questioning his own quotation.

**7. INSPECTOR VICTOR CROWE — The Detective**
- **Cover story:** A police inspector, here at the family's request to oversee the transfer of the Admiral's valuables
- **Secret:** Crowe is actually investigating a tip that the Admiral's death was murder. He was contacted by an anonymous letter (written by Dr. Ashworth, though he doesn't know the source) suggesting arsenic poisoning. He's here to investigate without revealing he suspects foul play — if the killer knows a detective is hunting them, evidence will disappear.
- **Win condition:** Determine whether the Admiral was murdered and, if so, identify the killer with enough evidence to make an arrest in Ashmark. He needs: proof of poison, method of administration, and motive.
- **Fallback (if evidence is destroyed):** Testimony — if he can get someone to confess or turn witness, that may be enough for an arrest. Cost: testimony is weaker than physical evidence and can be recanted.
- **Abilities:** Can "interrogate" one character per round — detailed questioning that creates social pressure (they can lie, but Crowe is experienced at detecting lies). Can examine crime scenes with professional thoroughness — finds details others miss. Carries handcuffs and a small revolver (unloaded — he's cautious about firearms on a train). Legal authority to detain.
- **Starting location:** Dining Car
- **Key relationships:** Unknown ally of Dr. Ashworth (she wrote the anonymous letter). Hunting Lillian and Colm (though he doesn't know it yet). Shares compartment with Mr. Falk.
- **Personality:** Methodical, patient, deceptively mild. Looks like a clerk. Thinks like a trap. Asks questions he already knows the answer to.

**8. MR. LAZAR KESSLER — The Collector**
- **Cover story:** An antiquities dealer, here because the Admiral owed him money for a previous transaction
- **Secret:** Kessler sold the Admiral the iron compass two years ago, acquiring it from a shipwreck salvager. At the time he thought it was merely a curiosity. Since then, he's learned what it actually is — an artifact connected to pre-Christian funerary practices, believed to guide the dead. He wants it back. Desperately. He has a buyer willing to pay a fortune, and he suspects the compass may be genuinely supernatural — which terrifies and excites him in equal measure.
- **Win condition:** Obtain the iron compass from the Admiral's car and leave the train with it. He doesn't care about the will or the murder — just the compass.
- **Fallback (if compass inaccessible):** Acquire the Admiral's journals, which may contain information about the compass's properties that his buyer would also value. Cost: less profitable, but still a score.
- **Abilities:** Expert appraiser — can assess the value and authenticity of any object. Experienced smuggler — knows how to conceal items on his person and in luggage. Charming negotiator — can make deals that seem fair but aren't. Carries tools for picking simple locks (NOT high-quality locks like the Admiral's car door — he'd need help or a different approach for that).
- **Starting location:** Saloon Car
- **Key relationships:** Knows the compass exists and what it looks like. Rival of anyone else seeking it. Shares compartment with Señora Vega.
- **Personality:** Urbane, witty, mercenary. Fidgets with a silver cigarette case. Smiles too easily. Eyes always evaluating.

**9. SEÑORA ELENA VEGA — The Medium**
- **Cover story:** A spiritualist and medium, invited by the Admiral to conduct séances in his final weeks
- **Secret:** She is genuinely psychic — or at least, she experiences things she cannot explain. She has faked many séances for paying clients, but her sessions with the Admiral were different. When she held the compass during their final séance, she heard a voice. It said: *"He is keeping me here. Set me free."* She doesn't know whose voice it was. She's terrified. She came aboard because she feels compelled — the voice is still calling to her from the Admiral's car.
- **Win condition:** Access the compass and conduct one final séance to understand what the voice is and what it wants. If the compass genuinely communicates with the dead, she must decide what to do with it — use it, destroy it, or give it to someone she trusts.
- **Fallback (if compass inaccessible):** Conduct a séance WITHOUT the compass, using proximity to the Admiral's body. Cost: less powerful, more ambiguous results. The voice may be too faint.
- **Abilities:** Genuine sensitivity — she can sense strong emotions in a room and gets impressions (vague, not specific) about people's hidden feelings. When near the Admiral's car, she feels the pull of the compass. Can conduct a séance that produces real (if ambiguous) results when holding the compass, or weaker impressions without it. Also skilled at cold reading and theatrical performance from her years of professional mediumship.
- **Starting location:** Saloon Car
- **Key relationships:** Knows the compass from her séances with the Admiral. Shares compartment with Kessler (who wants the same object for very different reasons). May ally with or oppose the Reverend.
- **Personality:** Dramatic, perceptive, privately frightened. Wears too many rings. Speaks with a faint accent she may or may not be exaggerating. Braver than she looks.

**10. MR. FALK — The Journalist**
- **Cover story:** A reporter for the Ashmark Gazette, here to cover the Admiral's death and the will reading as a news story
- **Secret:** Falk has been investigating Admiral Harlow for months. He has evidence that the Admiral was involved in a colonial atrocity during the Kelden campaign — the deliberate sinking of a refugee ship. If this comes to light, the Admiral's legacy is destroyed and several powerful people are implicated. Falk has documents but needs confirmation from someone who was there (Colonel Marsh was there). He's here to get that confirmation and break the biggest story of his career.
- **Win condition:** Confirm the Kelden atrocity story through Colonel Marsh's testimony (or other evidence found in the Admiral's papers) and ensure the story reaches his newspaper. The telegraph at Greyhollow or Ironbridge could transmit the story.
- **Fallback (if Marsh won't talk):** The Admiral's private papers may contain logs or correspondence that confirm the atrocity independently. Cost: documents without testimony are easier to deny.
- **Abilities:** Excellent interviewer — puts people at ease, then asks the question that cuts. Shorthand writer — can transcribe conversations from memory. Carries a press card that opens some doors and closes others. Knows the train's telegraph schedule (the wire is live at station stops).
- **Starting location:** Saloon Car
- **Key relationships:** Needs Colonel Marsh's testimony. Shares compartment with Inspector Crowe (interesting — a journalist and a detective in close quarters). His story could destroy the Harlow legacy, which affects Lillian's inheritance.
- **Personality:** Sharp, ambitious, charming when he wants to be, ruthless when he needs to be. Smokes constantly. Laughs easily but it never reaches his eyes.

**11. MISS CORDELIA VANE — The Ward**
- **Cover story:** The Admiral's goddaughter, a young woman of modest means, here out of filial duty
- **Secret:** She is actually the Admiral's illegitimate daughter. Her mother (now dead) was a Kelden woman — making Cordelia half-Kelden in an era of vicious colonial prejudice. The Admiral acknowledged her privately and provided for her through a trust, but never publicly claimed her. She believes the will may finally acknowledge her openly. She also suspects her father was murdered — she noticed his decline and wrote to Dr. Ashworth expressing her concerns (it was actually Cordelia, not Ashworth, who sent the anonymous letter to Inspector Crowe, though she used Ashworth's suspicions as the basis).
- **Win condition:** Learn the truth about her father's death AND be acknowledged in the will. If the will doesn't acknowledge her, she needs to find proof of her parentage in the Admiral's private papers.
- **Fallback (if neither achieved):** Find peace — hear the truth spoken aloud by someone who knew. Marsh, Pemberton, or even Lillian could provide this. Cost: emotional rather than material. She leaves with understanding but nothing tangible.
- **Abilities:** Knows the Admiral intimately — his habits, his moods, his handwriting (can spot forgeries). Observant and quiet — people talk freely around her because they underestimate her. Carries a locket containing a photograph of her mother with the Admiral — proof of the relationship if she chooses to reveal it. Wrote the anonymous letter to Crowe (she could reveal this to gain his trust).
- **Starting location:** Dining Car
- **Key relationships:** Shares compartment with Lillian (her father's wife — the tension is enormous). The will may name her as heir. She alerted Crowe to the murder. Connected to the Kelden atrocity through her heritage.
- **Personality:** Quiet, watchful, carrying a lifetime of concealment. Speaks precisely. Fiddles with her locket when stressed. Stronger than anyone expects.

**12. MISS ALICE WYNN — The Secretary**
- **Cover story:** The Admiral's personal secretary for the last three years, here to assist Pemberton with the estate
- **Secret:** Alice was the Admiral's confidante in his final months. She typed his private journals. She knows about the compass experiments, the will changes, and — crucially — she was present when the Admiral drafted a new codicil to the will just days before his death. The codicil, if valid, changes everything: it leaves the compass specifically to Señora Vega "so that what speaks through it may be heard." Alice has the codicil hidden in her luggage. She hasn't given it to Pemberton because she doesn't trust him — she suspects someone on this train killed the Admiral and she doesn't know who to trust.
- **Win condition:** Deliver the codicil to the right person — someone who will ensure it's honored, not suppressed. She also wants to understand what happened to the Admiral. Who killed him? Was it the poison she suspected, or was it the compass that drained him?
- **Fallback (if codicil is stolen or destroyed):** Testify to its existence. She can quote it from memory. Cost: legal weight of testimony vs. physical document.
- **Abilities:** Knowledge of the will's contents (both old and new codicil). Typed the Admiral's journals — knows their contents by heart. Excellent memory and organizational skills. Inconspicuous — people treat secretaries as furniture, which means she overhears everything. Carries the codicil in her luggage.
- **Starting location:** Dining Car
- **Key relationships:** Knows about the will changes (dangerous knowledge). Knows about the compass from typing the journals. Shares compartment with the player character. Trusts no one yet — needs to determine who is safe.
- **Personality:** Practical, alert, quietly loyal to the dead Admiral. Adjusts her spectacles when thinking. Doesn't volunteer information but answers honestly when directly asked. The most informed person on the train and the least powerful.

**13. MR. OTTO HEATH — The Stranger**
- **Cover story:** Claims to be a business associate of the Admiral's, involved in maritime insurance
- **Secret:** Heath is a fraud. He's actually a professional confidence man who learned about the Admiral's death and the valuable estate through underworld contacts. He forged an invitation letter and boarded the train intending to insert himself into the will reading and claim a debt the Admiral supposedly owed his "firm." His forged documents are good but not perfect — close inspection would reveal inconsistencies. He has no idea about the compass, the murder, or any of the real secrets. He's here to steal whatever he can.
- **Win condition:** Successfully claim a portion of the estate through his forged documents OR steal valuables from the Admiral's car or other passengers' luggage. Escape at Ironbridge Junction if things go wrong.
- **Fallback (if fraud is discovered):** Flee at the next station stop. Cost: loses everything and may be pursued.
- **Abilities:** Master impersonator — his cover story is smooth and detailed. Expert pickpocket — can lift small items from pockets or bags. Reads people quickly — identifies who is confident and who is vulnerable. Carries beautifully forged documents (letters, contracts) supporting his cover. Can forge handwriting passably.
- **Abilities (limitation):** He has NO real knowledge of the Admiral, the will, the compass, or the other passengers' histories. He is operating blind in a room full of people who actually knew Harlow. Every conversation is a minefield.
- **Starting location:** Saloon Car
- **Key relationships:** Enemy of anyone who examines his documents closely (Pemberton, Crowe). Potential tool for Lillian (she could use his presence as a distraction). Most vulnerable character — he could be exposed at any moment.
- **Personality:** Smoothly confident, adaptable, constantly performing. Tells excellent anecdotes about things that never happened. Sweats when Pemberton asks detailed questions.

### THE PLAYER'S ROLE

**14. GABRIEL CROSS — The Cartographer**
- **Cover story:** A naval cartographer who worked with the Admiral on mapping the Kelden coast
- **Secret:** Cross was on the ship that recovered the iron compass from the shipwreck. He saw what happened when it was first brought aboard — the compass needle spun wildly, the crew reported hearing whispers, and one sailor who held it for too long collapsed and didn't wake for three days. Cross helped the Admiral suppress the incident. He's been haunted by it since — literally. He hears whispers when he sleeps, faint and indistinct, and they've been getting louder over the past month. He came aboard because the Admiral's letter summoning him mentioned "the matter we buried at sea" — and Cross needs to know if Harlow found answers.
- **Win condition:** Discover what the compass truly is — its nature, its power, and whether it can be controlled or destroyed. Secondary: determine what happened to the Admiral. Was it the compass that killed him?
- **Abilities:** Naval knowledge — understands ships, charts, navigation, and maritime history. Was present at the compass's recovery — can identify it on sight and knows its physical properties. Hears whispers when near the compass (gives him a proximity sense — the closer he is to the Admiral's car, the louder the whispers become). Can read nautical charts and maritime code. Practical and physically capable — years at sea.
- **Starting location:** Dining Car
- **Key relationships:** Shares compartment with Alice Wynn (who knows about the compass from the journals). Connected to the Kelden expedition (links to Marsh, Falk's investigation, Cordelia's heritage). The compass responds to him.
- **Personality:** Quiet, practical, carrying a weight he doesn't discuss. Draws maps on napkins when nervous. Speaks plainly. Not a man of society — uncomfortable in formal settings. Trustworthy face.

### Why this role:
- Has a reason to investigate and talk to multiple characters (the compass connects to Kessler, Vega, Doyle, Ashworth)
- Has a supernatural sensitivity that creates eerie moments without giving too much away
- Connected to the colonial backstory (links to Marsh and Falk's storylines)
- Morally grey — he helped suppress the compass incident. His hands aren't clean.
- Can ally with multiple factions: the investigators (Crowe, Ashworth), the seekers (Doyle, Vega), or the schemers (Kessler)
- Shares a compartment with Alice Wynn, who is the most informed character — creating natural opportunities for the key relationship of the game
- Non-combatant who must rely on intelligence and alliances

## GAME MECHANICS

### TIME STRUCTURE
- Each round = approximately 30 minutes of in-game time
- Train departs Varre at 8:00 PM (Round 1)
- **Greyhollow Station stop: ~11:00 PM** (Reconnection Beat 1 — all passengers on the platform or in the Dining Car)
- **Ironbridge Junction: ~3:00 AM** (Reconnection Beat 2 — the train stops in darkness, tension peaks)
- Dawn / Ashmark arrival: 7:00 AM (game end)
- Expected active rounds: 8-12

### RECONNECTION BEATS

**Beat 1 — The Supper (Round 2-3, ~9:00 PM)**
- Pemberton calls all passengers to the Dining Car for a formal supper as a "mark of respect"
- Natural gathering — everyone seated, food served, forced social interaction
- Player hooks: who sits where, who avoids whom, who drinks too much

**Beat 2 — Greyhollow Station (Round 5-6, ~11:00 PM)**
- The train stops for 15 minutes. Passengers may step onto the platform.
- The station has a telegraph, a stationmaster, and a waiting room with a fire.
- Forces characters back into proximity. Some may try to leave the train — or send messages.
- Player hooks: who goes to the telegraph? Who steps off the train with luggage? Who meets someone on the platform?

**Beat 3 — The Disturbance (~Round 7-8, timing flexible)**
- A scream, a discovery, a confrontation — something erupts that draws people from their compartments into the corridor or Dining Car.
- Narrator-triggered based on game state. This replaces the Midnight Bell.
- Possible triggers: someone discovers evidence of poison, the compass produces an effect that multiple people witness, a character is caught where they shouldn't be.

**Beat 4 — Ironbridge Junction (Round 9-10, ~3:00 AM)**
- The train stops in total darkness. No station, no light, just snow and mountains.
- 10-minute stop. Characters may step out but there's nowhere to go — unless they're fleeing.
- Maximum tension — the darkest hour, the most isolated point.
- Player hooks: who tries to leave? Who uses the darkness for a private exchange? Does someone try to access the Admiral's car while everyone's distracted?

### ACTIONS PER ROUND
Same as base framework:
- MOVE to an adjacent car or space
- Perform ONE significant action
- Have incidental interactions at no cost

### VIOLENCE RULES
- Physical confrontation is possible but extremely constrained — the train is small and every car is adjacent to occupied cars. Noise carries.
- The only weapon easily available is a knife (from the Dining Car service). Crowe has an unloaded revolver.
- A violent act in any car will be heard in adjacent cars within one round.
- Characters can restrain others (Crowe has handcuffs, improvised restraints possible) but this requires overpowering them — most characters are not fighters.
- Colonel Marsh is the most physically capable passenger.

### INVESTIGATION MECHANICS
- **Examining evidence** takes one action and reveals primary findings. Medical evidence requires Dr. Ashworth's expertise. Legal documents require Pemberton's or Crowe's analysis.
- **Questioning** a character takes one action. They may lie. Crowe's interrogation ability applies social pressure. Doyle's pastoral manner encourages honesty.
- **Searching luggage** takes one action per trunk/case and requires access to the Luggage Van.
- **The telegraph** can only be used at station stops. Sending a message takes one action. The message reaches its destination — consequences follow.

## THE IRON COMPASS — SUPERNATURAL ELEMENT

The compass is a small iron disc, roughly palm-sized, with a single needle that does not point north. It is cold to the touch — unnaturally cold — and the cold doesn't dissipate with body heat.

**Properties:**
- The needle points toward "the boundary" — in practice, it points toward the recently dead. On this train, it points toward the Admiral's body.
- Prolonged contact (more than a few minutes) drains vitality. The holder feels tired, cold, heavy. Extended contact (hours) can cause collapse. This contributed to the Admiral's death.
- When held by someone with sensitivity (Señora Vega, Gabriel Cross), it produces whispers — faint, indistinct voices that seem to come from very far away or very deep down. The whispers are NOT intelligible words to most people. To Vega, during a proper séance, they can become a voice.
- The compass is NOT evil. It is a tool — like a radio tuned to a frequency most people can't hear. What speaks through it is ambiguous.
- The voice Vega heard ("He is keeping me here. Set me free.") may be the spirit of someone the Admiral wronged — possibly someone who died in the Kelden atrocity. Or it may be something else entirely. The game does not need to resolve this definitively.

## CASCADING EVENTS

**1. Body examined / poison discovered:**
Dr. Ashworth examines the Admiral's body or tests his belongings and finds arsenic traces. Consequences: the death becomes a murder investigation. Crowe shifts from covert to overt detective mode. Lillian becomes desperate. Colm begins to crack. Everyone becomes a suspect.

**2. The will is read early (or its contents leak):**
If Pemberton is convinced or coerced into revealing the will's contents: Harlow disinherited Lillian and left everything to Cordelia Vane. Consequences: Lillian is devastated and dangerous. Cordelia is thrust into the spotlight. The colonial heritage question explodes. Falk has a new angle on his story.

**3. The codicil surfaces:**
If Alice Wynn reveals or delivers the codicil: the compass is bequeathed to Señora Vega. Consequences: Kessler is cut out — he becomes desperate to steal the compass before the will becomes legal. The Reverend's path to the compass now requires Vega's cooperation. Legal questions arise about the codicil's validity.

**4. The Kelden atrocity is exposed:**
Falk publishes or Marsh confirms the story. Consequences: the Admiral's legacy is destroyed. The will reading becomes complicated — can you inherit from a war criminal? Cordelia's heritage becomes politically explosive. Lillian's motive for murder shifts — she may have known about the atrocity and been trying to prevent its exposure (or not).

**5. The compass is activated:**
Someone holds the compass and experiences its effects in front of witnesses. Consequences: the supernatural element becomes public. Rational characters (Ashworth, Crowe) are shaken. Faithful characters (Doyle) are either vindicated or terrified. Everyone wants the compass — or wants it destroyed.

**6. Heath is exposed as a fraud:**
His forged documents are examined and found wanting. Consequences: trust collapses — if one person is a fraud, who else might be? Paranoia spikes. Crowe's suspicion spreads to everyone. Heath tries to flee.

## NPC BEHAVIOR GUIDELINES

Same as base framework, plus:
- **The Friction Directive applies from Round 1.** Pemberton refuses early access to the car. Lillian deflects questions about the marriage. Marsh is taciturn. Information is earned, not given.
- **The train creates claustrophobia.** Characters cannot escape each other. This means grudges build, suspicions fester, and alliances form under pressure.
- **Class dynamics matter.** In 1889, a valet (Colm) and a secretary (Alice) occupy different social strata than a Colonel or a Countess. This affects who speaks to whom and how.
- **The darkness and cold matter.** The train is warm but the world outside is lethal. The windows show only blackness and snow. The isolation is psychological as well as physical.

## INITIAL ROUND PLAN (Round 1 — 8:00 PM, Departure)

All passengers gather in the Dining Car as the train pulls out of Varre station. Pemberton makes his announcement: the will shall be read at dawn in Ashmark. Until then, the Admiral's car is sealed. Passengers are welcome to the Dining Car and Saloon Car. Supper will be served at 9:00.

**Character intentions for Round 1:**
- **Pemberton:** Make his announcement, answer questions (evasively), establish authority
- **Lillian:** Project grief, assess who's aboard, ensure Colm stays in line
- **Colm:** Stay in the Luggage Van, avoid attention, wrestle with guilt
- **Colonel Marsh:** Study the train layout, identify the Admiral's car, plan his approach
- **Dr. Ashworth:** Observe Pemberton and Lillian, look for an opportunity to examine the body
- **Reverend Doyle:** Offer spiritual comfort, privately assess who might know about the compass
- **Inspector Crowe:** Introduce himself blandly, begin quietly assessing every passenger
- **Kessler:** Charm the room, determine who else knows about the compass
- **Señora Vega:** Feel the compass's pull from the Admiral's car, try to compose herself
- **Mr. Falk:** Observe, take mental notes, identify Colonel Marsh as his primary target
- **Cordelia Vane:** Watch Lillian (her father's wife who doesn't know Cordelia is his daughter), try to gauge Pemberton's disposition toward her
- **Alice Wynn:** Assist Pemberton while privately assessing who she can trust with the codicil
- **Otto Heath:** Work the room, establish his cover, identify marks and opportunities
- **Gabriel Cross (Player):** Arrive, take in the scene, feel the whispers grow louder as the train moves
