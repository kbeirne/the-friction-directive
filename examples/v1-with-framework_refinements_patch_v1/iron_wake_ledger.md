## PRE-GAME KNOWLEDGE (What Gabriel Cross knows before boarding)

### Characters Known
- **Admiral Erasmus Harlow:** Dead. Cross's former commanding officer during the Kelden coast mapping expedition. The man who recovered the compass. Cross has not seen him in over a year. Summoned by letter referencing "the matter we buried at sea."
- **All other passengers:** Unknown to Cross before boarding. He will learn names and cover stories during Round 1.

### Locations Known
- **The Aurelia Express:** Knows it's the Admiral's private train. Has never been aboard before.
- **The Admiral's Private Car:** Knows it exists (mentioned in the summons letter). Does not know its layout or contents.

### Plot Knowledge
- The iron compass was recovered from a shipwreck off the Kelden coast during an expedition Cross participated in.
- When first brought aboard, the compass caused: the needle spinning wildly, crew reporting whispers, one sailor collapsing after prolonged contact.
- Cross and the Admiral suppressed the incident. Cross has been haunted by faint whispers since, growing louder in the past month.
- The Admiral's letter mentioned "the matter we buried at sea" — implying the compass and the suppressed incident.
- Cross does NOT know: what the compass actually does, why the Admiral died, who else on the train knows about the compass, or what the will contains.

### Items Held
- The Admiral's summons letter
- Personal luggage (cartography tools, charts, personal effects)
- No weapons

### Active Commitments
- None yet

## ROUND 1 — KNOWLEDGE UPDATE (comprehensive, after all Round 1 conversations)

### Characters Known
- **Mr. Pemberton:** The Admiral's solicitor. Executor of the will. Fussy, formal, under pressure. Has the only key to the sealed car. Read Cross's letter — unsettled by "the matter we buried at sea." Could not explain why Cross was specifically summoned. Refused Dr. Ashworth's request to examine the body.
- **Mrs. Harlow:** The Admiral's wife. Black crepe, dry-eyed grief. Cross has not spoken to her. Cordelia Vane watches her with unusual intensity. Was in private conversation with Rev. Doyle.
- **Colonel James Marsh:** Retired army. Thirty years with the Admiral — "brothers or enemies, sometimes both." Falling out over "professional matters." "Kelden" is deeply significant to him. Said things happened on the coast that "never made it into official reports." Angry about sealed documents. Offered Cross a private conversation later tonight.
- **Miss Cordelia Vane:** The Admiral's goddaughter. Young, has been crying. Gold locket, touches it when stressed. Watches Mrs. Harlow with disproportionate intensity. Paused before saying "godfather" — the word didn't quite fit.
- **Miss Alice Wynn:** Admiral's secretary, three years. Typed his private journals. Compartment F (with Cross). Knows about: the Kelden object ("a navigational instrument"), the Admiral's experiments, work with a spiritualist, his rapid decline. Suspects death not natural — pointed Cross toward Dr. Ashworth. Named the spiritualist as Señora Vega. Trustworthy — respected Cross's silence. "I think the Admiral was right about you."
- **Dr. Ashworth:** Admiral's physician. Medical bag. Asked to examine body — denied. Wynn says she's "concerned" about cause of death. Cross has not spoken to her yet.
- **Señora Vega:** The spiritualist from the journals. Woman in plum dress. Appears to feel the same pull Cross feels toward the rear of the train. Cross has not spoken to her yet.
- **Reverend Doyle:** Led the prayer with eyes open. In private conversation with Mrs. Harlow.
- **Quiet man, untouched tea (name unknown):** Professional observer. Left Dining Car toward the rear of the train. Not seen since.
- **Man with notebook, ink stains (name unknown):** Passed Cross in corridor heading toward Saloon Car.
- **Sleek man, cologne (name unknown):** Charming, working the room. Stumbled on Marsh's insurance question.
- **One passenger absent from initial gathering** — count was short. Cross did not ask about it.

### Plot Knowledge
- Admiral's private journals exist — describe the Kelden salvage, experiments with the object, work with Señora Vega, increasing fear.
- Admiral described Cross as "the steadiest man on deck, and the only one I trusted to keep his silence."
- Admiral was healthy six months ago, then declined rapidly — fatigue, appetite loss, shaking hands, changed colour.
- Dr. Ashworth noticed the decline and is "concerned." Wynn suspects death not natural but has no proof.
- The Kelden object is aboard, presumably in the sealed car. Cross can feel it. Vega appears to feel it too.
- Marsh has Kelden business — things that "never made it into official reports." Unspecified.

### Items Held
- Admiral's summons letter
- Personal luggage (Compartment F)

- Tacit agreement: meet Marsh later tonight for private Kelden conversation
