## ROUND 4 — "THE RECKONING HOUR" (3:30 PM – 8:00 PM, Wednesday October 6th)

At 5:00 PM he goes to Vantrani's suite. Vantrani, still composed after the study scene, receives him with warmth. Ashby presents the blackmail file. Vantrani looks at it for a long time. Then he says: "This is thorough work, Henry." He uses his first name. "I'll need until tomorrow morning to consider my position." He neither capitulates nor refuses. He asks for time. Ashby grants it — he has no choice. He leaves at 5:45 PM without physical custody of anything. His blackmail leverage has been spent; Vantrani has absorbed it and is thinking. This is a partial result.

**Dr. Marta Voss** — Alone with Vantrani in the study after Elspeth leaves. Vantrani speaks to her in German for approximately 20 minutes. In exchange: she continues as his physician for the crossing and says nothing about what she saw or did. He is not threatening her. He is being genuinely practical. She does not destroy the page. She accepts the arrangement, with the specific condition that he shows her the page has been removed from the binding. He does — he removes pages 55-62 (the Wissenschaft/Reich section entirely) and gives them to her. She takes them to her stateroom and dissolves them with the acid. The Ledger now has a gap in its binding. Vantrani knows this and accepts it — he considers it a reasonable cost. Marta's win condition is achieved (her page is destroyed). She emerges from the study at approximately 3:50 PM looking composed and exhausted.

**Augustine Lowe** — Has been in the Library with Mercier all afternoon. At 4:15 PM he receives a note from Rosa: Vantrani has now met with the German (Kellner), the British-adjacent (Ashby, though Rosa doesn't know his name), and conducted what appeared to be a lengthy interview with Elspeth. Rosa's assessment: "Multiple parties are engaged. The British may have already moved." Lowe reads this with the controlled concern of a man who has come second when he expected to come first. He sends a wireless message to Washington: "British action possible. Advise on authorisation ceiling." He will not receive a response until tomorrow morning. He is now reactive rather than proactive — a significant strategic shift.

**Countess Elena Vaszary** — Has spent the afternoon making social rounds with deliberate patience. At 4:30 PM she is in the Grand Salon when she observes something significant: Marta Voss exits the stateroom corridor looking shaken, carrying something in her coat pocket (the dissolved pages, now just damp tissue). Elena files this as: Marta has done something that cost her something. At 5:30 PM she observes Ashby returning from Vantrani's end of the corridor with the specific expression of a man who has spent leverage and received a promissory note in return. By 6:00 PM Elena has three confirmed assessments: Elspeth (British intelligence, camera-based operation), Ashby (British intelligence, senior, blackmail leverage), and now Marta (connected to Vantrani in a covert capacity, German connection). Three identities. She has what she needs for transmission. She will use the 11 PM wireless window tonight.

Berlin responds at 6:30 PM: "Authorised for direct action if necessary. Diplomatic cover maintained." Answer: it makes everything more urgent. He will still go. He sends a note to Elspeth's door via the corridor: a single folded card. On it, in his handwriting: *9 o'clock. My room. Tomorrow.* He does not use names or explain.

**Dr. Marta Voss** — In her stateroom since 8:35 PM. The dissolved pages have been flushed. She is reading a medical journal and performing normalcy. ## ROUND 6 — "THE LAST DAY AT SEA" (Thursday October 7th — Friday October 8th dawn)
