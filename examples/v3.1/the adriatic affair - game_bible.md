# GAME BIBLE — THE ADRIATIC AFFAIR
## Asymmetric Deduction RPG — SS Leviathan, October 1937

---

## SETTING DOCUMENT

**The Environment:** The SS Leviathan is the jewel of the Vantrani Line — a 900-foot Art Deco ocean liner making the four-night crossing from Southampton to New York. She is all dark mahogany, brass fittings, and champagne in crystal. Her first-class promenade deck houses the Grand Salon, the smoking rooms, the private dining suites, and thirteen staterooms. The ocean is cold, the horizon vast, and no one is going anywhere.

**The Social Occasion:** A private charter. The official story is that Cornelius Vantrani — shipping magnate, host, and something of a political collector — is celebrating his 70th birthday with an intimate gathering of twenty guests. The invitations were impossible to refuse. In practice, several guests received invitations they did not expect, for reasons they are still working out.

**The Host:** CORNELIUS VANTRANI. A 70-year-old Austro-Hungarian émigré who built an empire in transatlantic shipping. Charming, imperious, and perpetually amused by the vulnerability of powerful people. His secret: he has spent five years compiling a dossier — THE LEDGER — a 200-page document cataloguing the true identities, crimes, and associations of every foreign intelligence operative working the Western corridors. He has offered it, simultaneously, to three different buyers. He plans to sell it to whichever buyer reaches him first with the right price. He will use the other two as leverage, or bargaining chips, or entertainment, depending on his mood.

**The Central Secret:** THE LEDGER exists. Everyone aboard suspects it might — almost no one knows for certain. The Ledger contains intelligence that would destroy careers and governments if it reached the wrong hands. Every spy aboard is trying to determine if it's real, and if so, where it is hidden. The Ledger is currently hidden in two pieces: Part One is in a hidden compartment in Vantrani's private study (Restricted), and Part Two is sealed in the ship's strongroom (Restricted). Vantrani keeps both keys on his person at all times — the study key on his watch chain, the strongroom key in the heel of his right shoe.

**Time Frame:** The game begins on the first full evening at sea — 7:00 PM, Tuesday, October 5th, 1937. The ship docks in New York at 7:00 AM on Saturday, October 9th. Rounds represent 30-minute intervals. The game runs approximately 10-12 active rounds before endgame.

**The Acceleration Event (fires at Round 6 or when mid-game sags):** A passenger is found dead. Discovered in the Lower Promenade — dressed for dinner, a thin wire around their neck. No visible assailant. Vantrani orders the ship's doctor to declare it a heart attack. The passenger was, in fact, **DOMINIK PRAGER (Character 9)**, killed by **ILSE BRANDT (Character 7)** who discovered he was about to expose her. The murder makes everyone dangerous and everyone scared.

## LOCATION MAP

### PUBLIC SPACES (freely accessible, fully observed)

**1. THE GRAND SALON**
The heart of first-class social life. A two-deck ballroom with a small orchestra and bar. Characters here are visible to all others present. Evening gatherings happen here. Rich in eavesdropping opportunities. A pair of high-backed armchairs near the starboard windows are a known gossip spot.
*Environmental detail:* A framed oil portrait of young Vantrani hangs above the bar — but the frame is slightly crooked, and someone has left a small scratch in the wainscoting beneath it, as if a tool was wedged there once.

**2. THE PROMENADE DECK**
The long exterior walkway along the ship's starboard side. Bracing at night, private in fog. Characters can follow each other here, overhear conversations, or be observed going places they shouldn't. At the stern, a locked door leads to the service stairwell.
*Environmental detail:* A cigarette burned to its filter has been ground into the teak rail at the aft end — someone stood here a long time, watching the wake.

**3. THE FIRST-CLASS DINING ROOM**
All fourteen characters eat here. Assigned seating is posted each morning by the purser. Social pressure to appear at meals. NPCs who skip dinner are noticed and remarked upon.
*Environmental detail:* The seating chart is laminated behind glass in the dining room foyer. A hairline scratch runs along one corner, as if someone has pried the frame open and re-sealed it.

**4. THE SMOKING ROOM**
A masculine, club-like space of leather chairs and dark wood. Women may enter but it carries a social cost. Late-night conversations happen here. Cards are played here. Alliances are made here.
*Environmental detail:* A copy of The Times from September 28th sits on the side table — but it's been folded to an inside page, an article about a missing Swiss economist circled in pencil.

**5. THE LIBRARY**
Small, hushed, excellent for private conversation. Accessible to all first-class passengers but rarely crowded. Books on navigation, history, adventure fiction. One shelf of reference texts appears to have been recently rearranged — the spines are slightly irregular.
*Environmental detail:* A book — a collected poetry anthology — has been pushed to the back of the second shelf. A small folded note marks a page, but the note is blank. The page itself is Neruda. Someone has underlined: *"I want to do with you what spring does with cherry blossoms."*

---

### RESTRICTED SPACES (require access)

**6. VANTRANI'S PRIVATE STUDY**
Connecting to his stateroom suite. Two locks: the outer door requires a passkey (available from the purser, with Vantrani's permission) and an inner cabinet requires Vantrani's personal study key (watch chain). Contains: Part One of the Ledger, private correspondence, a cipher machine (a modified Enigma variant), one bottle of Slovenian brandy. The room has a distinctive smell: cedar and old paper.
*Access methods:* (a) Vantrani's direct invitation, (b) the Purser's passkey — taken, borrowed, or forged, (c) the service corridor connecting to the cabin steward's closet, which shares a thin wall with the study — a very small person could enter through the adjoining utility hatch, (d) the fire-safety override panel in the engine room, which can be used to disengage the locks in the upper deck rooms.
*Environmental detail:* A map of the Atlantic is pinned above the desk. Several coordinates have been marked in red ink — but they correspond to no known ship route.

**7. THE STRONGROOM**
Belowdecks, accessible through the engine room corridor. Requires a crew escort (the Purser can arrange) and Vantrani's strongroom key. Contains: Part Two of the Ledger, several guests' personal valuables, one diplomatic bag sealed with the German Foreign Office stamp.
*Access methods:* (a) Vantrani's strongroom key + Purser escort, (b) a master safe key held by the Chief Engineer — he can be bribed, threatened, or charmed, (c) the maintenance conduit running alongside the strongroom (physical ability + lockpick tools required), (d) the fire-safety override (see study).
*Environmental detail:* The German diplomatic bag in the strongroom is the wrong colour — the seal is correct but the leather is a shade too light. Someone substituted it. The original bag is missing.

**8. THE CAPTAIN'S BRIDGE**
Controlled access. The Captain (an NPC) can grant access. Contains the ship's wireless radio — the only way to communicate with shore. Also contains the ship's manifest, passenger list (with real names), and the cargo log.
*Access methods:* (a) Captain's invitation, (b) A sailor (crew NPC) can be bribed or charmed into escort, (c) during a ship emergency, access control relaxes, (d) the wireless can also be accessed from a smaller repeater set in the purser's office.

**9. THE ENGINE ROOM**
Hot, loud, and manned round the clock. Chief Engineer MILO BAUER is effectively the gatekeeper. Contains the fire-safety override panel and the maintenance conduit entrance. Most passengers would look strikingly out of place here.
*Access methods:* (a) Bauer's cooperation, (b) a crew uniform from the laundry (passenger decks only, below-decks crew uniform requires specific access), (c) claiming a safety emergency, (d) the service stairwell from the promenade.

---

### TRANSITIONAL SPACES (corridors, observed movement)

**10. THE FIRST-CLASS STATEROOM CORRIDOR**
Fourteen staterooms along a carpeted hall. A character seen here at the wrong hour is noticed. The cabin steward OTTO makes his rounds every two hours and knows who is where. Characters can eavesdrop through thin walls.
*Environmental detail:* One stateroom — number 7 — has a tiny scratch at the base of the door lock, fresh. Someone has tried to pick it, unsuccessfully.

**11. THE SERVICE CORRIDOR**
Runs parallel to the stateroom corridor on the port side. Staff use it to move unseen. Connects to: the steward's closet, the kitchens, the study utility hatch, and the service stairwell. Characters who use this corridor risk being seen by crew — but crew can also be avoided. Passengers found here will attract significant attention.

**12. THE LOWER PROMENADE**
The exterior walk on the deck below the main promenade. Less social, less visible from the salon windows. A natural spot for secret meetings. Where the body is found in Round 6. At night, it's poorly lit.

**13. THE PURSER'S OFFICE**
Semi-public. The Purser (FELIX STAM, NPC) is almost always present. Contains: the passenger manifest, the seating assignment board, the passkey cabinet (locked), and the wireless repeater. Guests can visit for ostensibly legitimate reasons — lost luggage, messages from shore, etc.

## CHARACTER ROSTER

### CHARACTER 1 — ELSPETH CRANE — The Correspondent
**Cover story:** A features writer for the New York Tribune, assigned to write a profile of Vantrani for the paper's society pages.
**Secret:** Elspeth is genuinely a journalist — but she is also working for British Naval Intelligence, tasked with confirming whether the Ledger exists and, if so, photographing its contents to send to London. She has a miniature camera concealed in a cigarette case.
**Win condition (Discovery + Completion):** Confirm the Ledger exists AND photograph at least one part of it (Part One or Part Two) without being caught or exposed.
**Abilities:**
- *Press Access:* Once per game, can invoke journalistic privilege to obtain an audience with any character who has a public reputation — they will speak to her, briefly, before finding a reason to leave. Does not compel honesty.
- *Profile Read:* After observing a character for one full round in the same room, Elspeth can ask the narrator one factual question about their behavior or body language that she has observed — the answer will be specific but may be ambiguous.
- *Darkroom:* If Elspeth has photographed something, she can develop the film in the ship's photographic studio (a locker near the laundry, she has the key). This takes one round and must be done discreetly.
**Starting location:** Grand Salon
**Key relationships:** She has a pre-existing professional rapport with Vantrani — he agreed to the profile piece, which is how she got the invitation. She has been told (by her handler in London) that at least one other British asset may be aboard, but she doesn't know who. She has no idea that HENRY ASHBY (Character 2) is her handler's other operative.
**Personality:** Composed, perceptive, and socially fluent. Speaks with precision. Never says "I think" — always "I noticed." When nervous, she becomes more formal, not less.
**Fallback path:** If she cannot access the Ledger physically, she can still win by obtaining verbal confirmation from Vantrani himself that the Ledger is real — recorded on a concealed wire recorder she can retrieve from her luggage. This satisfies her intelligence objective at reduced value.

### CHARACTER 2 — HENRY ASHBY — The Retired Diplomat
**Cover story:** A retired Foreign Office attaché travelling to visit his daughter in New York. Cheerful, slightly hard of hearing, loves bridge.
**Secret:** Henry is NOT retired. He is a senior MI6 controller making the crossing to take personal custody of the Ledger — if it can be secured. He knows about Elspeth but has not told her, intending to use her as a front while he works the other side of the operation: blackmailing Vantrani into handing over the Ledger voluntarily by threatening to expose his tax fraud to the American authorities. He is the most dangerous person in the room and looks like everyone's uncle.
**Win condition (Completion):** Obtain physical custody of one complete part of the Ledger (Part One or Part Two) and secure it in his travel documents, which have diplomatic protection.
**Abilities:**
- *Blackmail File:* Henry carries documented evidence of Vantrani's tax fraud. Presenting this to Vantrani gives him significant leverage — Vantrani will grant him one request (access, information, audience) before the leverage is spent.
- *Old Boy Network:* Henry can invoke a shared membership of a private club, regiment, or society with any British male character — they will be disposed to trust him initially.
- *Misdirection:* Once per game, Henry can plant a false rumor with one character that will be believed and acted on. The rumor must be plausible.
**Starting location:** Smoking Room
**Key relationships:** His handler is technically above Elspeth's chain of command, but he has decided not to tell her this. He is mildly suspicious of COUNTESS VASZARY (Character 5) but not sure why. He finds STEFAN KELLNER (Character 6) too charming and has noted this as a warning sign.
**Personality:** Avuncular, slow-moving, apparently distracted. Occasionally drops names of obscure titled people. When pressed, becomes suddenly very precise and very quiet.
**Fallback path:** If blackmail fails (Vantrani calls his bluff or the evidence is stolen), Henry can pivot to offering Vantrani a British Government protection arrangement — witness status in exchange for the Ledger. This is a real offer and Henry has authority to make it, but it takes longer to negotiate and gives Vantrani more leverage.

### CHARACTER 3 — DR. MARTA VOSS — The Physician
**Cover story:** Vantrani's personal physician, attending to his health on the crossing.
**Secret:** Marta is a physicist, not a physician. She is a German-Jewish scientist who defected from a Munich research institute three months ago and is travelling under a false identity provided by a Zionist underground network. She is terrified. The German delegation aboard (ILSE BRANDT, STEFAN KELLNER) must not learn who she is. The Ledger contains her real name under her former identity — listed as an asset of the German Science Ministry. If anyone reads the Ledger, they will know her secret. She is trying to destroy Part One before it can be read.
**Win condition (Prevention):** Destroy or permanently corrupt Part One of the Ledger before it can be photographed, read, or transmitted.
**Abilities:**
- *Medical Access:* Marta can visit any character's stateroom under the pretext of a medical check without arousing immediate suspicion. She can also administer sedatives — requires close physical access and a drink or food item.
- *Chemical Knowledge:* Given access to the ship's medical supply cabinet (purser's office), she can obtain materials to destroy paper documents without obvious fire — a dissolving agent that leaves no trace except a faint smell of iodine.
- *Cover Maintenance:* Once per round, Marta can deflect a question about her medical credentials by pivoting to Vantrani's health — this works unless the questioner has medical knowledge.
**Starting location:** First-class stateroom corridor (returning from attending Vantrani)
**Key relationships:** VANTRANI trusts her professionally. She despises KELLNER on sight — she knew him in Munich — but must not show it. ELSPETH makes her nervous; she can tell the woman is watching people.
**Personality:** Quiet, careful, slightly stilted in social English. Warm when comfortable. Never volunteers information. When afraid, she performs calm so deliberately that the performance itself becomes visible.
**Fallback paths:**
- If chemical destruction is impossible: she can steal Part One and throw it overboard. This requires getting into the study alone and getting to an exterior deck unobserved.
- If both fail: she can attempt to alter a specific page within the Ledger — changing her listed name — rather than destroying the whole thing. This requires the study, a pen, and the ability to match the typeface.

### CHARACTER 4 — AUGUSTINE LOWE — The Benefactor
**Cover story:** An American philanthropist and arts patron aboard at Vantrani's personal invitation. Handsome, generous with drinks.
**Secret:** Augustine is a senior operative of the OSS (precursor to the CIA), aboard to assess whether the Ledger is worth acquiring for American interests. He is also carrying $40,000 in cash — his acquisition budget — sewn into the lining of his luggage. He has been told to buy the Ledger if possible, or ensure it doesn't reach British or German hands if not. He has a secondary mission: determine whether HENRY ASHBY is still an active intelligence officer. He suspects Ashby is, and this would be useful information.
**Win condition (Completion):** Acquire at least one part of the Ledger for American custody, OR confirm in writing (a message sent via the ship's wireless) that neither Britain nor Germany has acquired it.
**Abilities:**
- *Deep Pockets:* Augustine can bribe any NPC crew member (Chief Engineer, Purser, Steward). He can also offer cash incentives to other characters — they may or may not accept. Wealthy characters are harder to bribe.
- *Old Money Charm:* Social grace that opens doors — characters are predisposed to like and trust Augustine, making it easier for him to extract information in casual conversation.
- *Wireless Privilege:* Augustine has a pre-arranged code with the wireless operator — he can send and receive one encrypted message per day without the Captain's knowledge.
**Starting location:** Grand Salon
**Key relationships:** He is charmed by ELSPETH and has been watching her from the start. He has identified KELLNER as likely German intelligence but has not confirmed this. He is friendly with VANTRANI in a surface way — they've met at fundraisers.
**Personality:** Easy, unhurried, apparently delighted by everything. Has a habit of making expensive things sound ordinary. Never appears to be working. Is always working.
**Fallback path:** If he cannot acquire the Ledger, he can still achieve a partial win by exposing Kellner or Brandt's identities to the Captain — which will result in their diplomatic detention and prevent German acquisition. He considers this a last resort.

### CHARACTER 5 — COUNTESS ELENA VASZARY — The Widow
**Cover story:** A Hungarian aristocrat, old friend of Vantrani's. Travelling to New York to visit her estate holdings.
**Secret:** Elena is not a widow — her husband is alive, imprisoned in a Budapest detention facility. She has been told by the Hungarian Interior Ministry that she will receive her husband's release in exchange for one piece of intelligence: she must identify which guests aboard the Leviathan are intelligence operatives and report them via the wireless before the ship docks. She does not work for any intelligence service. She is a civilian under duress, doing this to save her husband's life.
**Win condition (Discovery):** Correctly identify at least three intelligence operatives aboard (character, cover, and real allegiance) and transmit this information via the ship's wireless before arrival.
**Abilities:**
- *Social Gravity:* Elena is titled, beautiful, and has a social authority that causes characters to seek her company. She can facilitate introductions between characters, which gives her access to conversations she wouldn't otherwise hear.
- *Sympathetic Ear:* When a character is emotionally distressed or isolated, Elena can offer comfort — and characters will often reveal more than they intend in these moments. Works once per character.
- *The Widow's Advantage:* Elena's cover as a politically disconnected aristocrat makes her seem harmless. Characters will speak more freely near her than they should.
**Starting location:** Grand Salon
**Key relationships:** Old friendship with VANTRANI — they have history the others don't know about (they were lovers, thirty years ago — she knows things about him that are useful). She is suspicious of LOWE but cannot place why. She finds MARTA's composure too studied.
**Personality:** Gracious, melancholy in an aristocratic way, very good at making people feel understood. Laughs rarely but genuinely. Under stress, becomes exquisitely polite — which reads as coldness.
**Fallback path:** If she cannot identify three operatives, she can still partially succeed by transmitting one confirmed identity — which will secure a reduction in her husband's sentence but not his release. If she is exposed as a spy herself, she will offer everything she knows in exchange for protection — she is not loyal to Budapest, only to her husband.

### CHARACTER 6 — STEFAN KELLNER — The Cultural Attaché
**Cover story:** A junior cultural attaché from the German Embassy in London, accompanying a small diplomatic consignment aboard the ship. Fluent in four languages. Terribly good at tennis.
**Secret:** Kellner is SD (Sicherheitsdienst — German intelligence). His real mission: Vantrani has offered the Ledger to Germany. Kellner is here to receive it, verify its authenticity, and ensure it reaches Berlin. He has a cipher device in his luggage and is in contact with Berlin via coded messages through the wireless. He is also aware that the German diplomatic bag in the strongroom has been tampered with — and suspects someone switched it, but doesn't know who.
**Win condition (Completion):** Receive Part Two of the Ledger from Vantrani and transmit at least its key contents via the ship's cipher to Berlin before docking.
**Abilities:**
- *Diplomatic Immunity:* If detained or confronted by ship's authority, Kellner can invoke diplomatic status — crew cannot search him or detain him without a significant international incident. This does NOT protect him from other passengers.
- *Cipher Fluency:* Kellner can encode and decode messages at speed. If he can access Part Two, he can transcribe the critical sections into coded form within a single round.
- *Pressure:* Kellner can apply controlled, professional intimidation. Once per character, he can imply that he knows something dangerous about them — whether he does or not — and they must either call his bluff or respond with fear. He will be damaged socially if called on a bluff.
**Starting location:** Dining Room
**Key relationships:** He is professionally respectful of ASHBY and suspects him correctly. He is drawn to ELSPETH and tries to engage her, partly because she seems to know things. He has been specifically told to avoid obvious contact with ILSE BRANDT in public.
**Personality:** Polished, warm, with excellent manners and very still eyes. Laughs at the right moments. Uses first names faster than he should. Occasionally goes very quiet before changing the subject.
**Fallback path:** If Vantrani refuses to hand over Part Two directly, Kellner will attempt to steal it — first by accessing the strongroom through the Chief Engineer, and if that fails, by attempting to burgle the study.

### CHARACTER 7 — ILSE BRANDT — The Concert Pianist
**Cover story:** A celebrated German concert pianist, touring to perform in New York.
**Secret:** Ilse is Gestapo. Her real mission is entirely separate from Kellner's — and neither knows about the other (SD and Gestapo are rival agencies). She has been tasked with eliminating a specific individual aboard: DOMINIK PRAGER (Character 9), a former Gestapo officer who defected and took a list of German assets in America with him. The list is concealed on his person. She must kill him, recover the asset list, and ensure it returns to Berlin. She has a garrote concealed in her concert gown's waistband.
**Win condition (Completion):** Kill Prager, recover the asset list (concealed in the lining of his jacket), and secure it — either deliver it to Kellner (who doesn't know she's coming) or hide it for recovery when the ship docks.
**Kills Prager in Round 5/6 (Acceleration Event).** After that, her win condition shifts to: secure the asset list without being identified as the murderer.
**Abilities:**
- *Unassuming Presence:* Ilse is so socially expected (charming, cultured, feminine) that she is the last person anyone suspects of violence. -1 to anyone's first suspicion roll against her.
- *The Garotte:* A physical assassination tool. Requires: target alone, no witnesses, a non-public space. If conditions are met, the kill is swift and nearly silent.
- *Performance:* Ilse can schedule a recital, which draws a large portion of the first-class passengers to the Grand Salon — clearing other spaces.
**Starting location:** Dining Room
**Key relationships:** PRAGER's presence terrifies her but she cannot show it. She has assessed KELLNER correctly as SD but treats him as a stranger. She has a professional respect, from a distance, for ASHBY.
**Personality:** Vivid, quick to laugh, often the loudest and most charming person in the room. Has opinions about music that are genuine. Very rarely still. Under professional stress, she focuses on objects rather than people — touches things, adjusts things.
**Fallback path:** If she cannot kill Prager cleanly (witnesses, complications), she will attempt to steal the asset list from his stateroom while he sleeps — a less certain but non-violent alternative. If the list is gone (Prager already passed it on), she will attempt to recover who he passed it to and neutralise that threat instead.

### CHARACTER 8 — PROFESSOR ALAIN MERCIER — The Historian
**Cover story:** A French academic historian, writing a book on transatlantic commerce. Invited by Vantrani, who is a subject of the book.
**Secret:** Mercier is a courier for the French Deuxième Bureau (intelligence). He carries an encrypted microfilm — not related to the Ledger — which he is transporting to a contact in New York. He does not know the microfilm's contents. His secondary objective (which he does know): if the Ledger is real, France wants to know what's in it regarding French assets. He is not empowered to acquire it — just to look if given the chance.
**Win condition (Completion):** Deliver the microfilm to his New York contact (a dockside arrangement). He must ensure the microfilm is not intercepted before docking.
**Abilities:**
- *Academic Access:* Can invoke research methodology — characters will allow him to ask probing questions framed as scholarly curiosity. Works once per character before they become suspicious.
- *Forgettable:* Mercier is so thoroughly unremarkable that people often fail to notice him entering or leaving a room. He can sometimes move in transitional spaces without being recorded as having passed through.
- *Memorisation:* If given access to a document, Mercier can memorise a specific piece of information (a name, a number, a key phrase) and retain it perfectly. He cannot photograph but he can remember.
**Starting location:** Library
**Key relationships:** He finds VANTRANI fascinating as a subject, and this is genuine — he almost forgets he's on a mission when they're talking. He has been warned to avoid KELLNER. He finds ELSPETH slightly too sharp for his comfort.
**Personality:** Distracted, self-deprecating, always slightly lost. Asks questions that seem naive and often aren't. Has a habit of summarizing things he's just been told back at the speaker in subtly different terms.
**Fallback path:** If the microfilm is discovered or confiscated, Mercier's fallback is to memorise the key data it contains (he has read it once) and deliver the information verbally. This is a significant security downgrade and will make his handler furious, but it works.

### CHARACTER 9 — DOMINIK PRAGER — The Businessman
**Cover story:** A Viennese export trader. Quiet, watchful, drinks alone.
**Secret:** A former Gestapo officer who defected to the Americans eighteen months ago. He carries a list of German intelligence assets embedded in American society — sewn into the lining of his jacket. He is being escorted, in a loose sense, by AUGUSTINE LOWE (Character 4), who arranged his passage. He does not know Ilse Brandt is aboard. He will be killed in Round 5/6.
**Win condition (Survival):** Reach New York alive with the asset list intact.
**Abilities:**
- *Tradecraft:* Prager can identify surveillance — if someone has been watching him for a round, he notices on the following round.
- *Counter-tail:* Can shake a pursuer in transitional spaces.
**Starting location:** Smoking Room
**Key relationships:** He trusts LOWE marginally. He is suspicious of nearly everyone else. He will not know ILSE is a threat until it is too late.
**Personality:** Economical. Drinks vodka. Answers questions with questions. Has the stillness of someone who has been frightened for a very long time.
**NOTE:** Prager is killed by Ilse in Round 5/6. After this, he becomes a narrative element — his body, his jacket lining, and who finds the asset list become significant.

### CHARACTER 10 — ROSA FERREIRA — The Translator
**Cover story:** A multilingual personal assistant to AUGUSTINE LOWE.
**Secret:** Rosa is a Communist Party courier. She has been placed in Lowe's employ by a Soviet contact in Lisbon. She knows Lowe is OSS — and is trying to determine the extent of American intelligence operations in Europe by monitoring Lowe's activities. She also wants the Ledger — not to acquire it but to determine whether it contains Soviet assets. If it does, she must warn her handlers.
**Win condition (Discovery):** Identify what Lowe is actually doing aboard (confirm his intelligence function) AND determine whether the Ledger contains Soviet-listed assets.
**Abilities:**
- *Linguistic Eavesdrop:* Rosa is fluent in eight languages. She can understand conversations in any European language without the speakers knowing she understands.
- *Shorthand:* Can make near-invisible notes during conversations without appearing to do so.
- *Access by Proximity:* As Lowe's assistant, she has legitimate reasons to accompany him to most spaces — this gives her a credible excuse to be wherever he goes.
**Starting location:** Grand Salon (with Lowe)
**Key relationships:** She is Lowe's shadow and uses his social access. She finds MERCIER interesting — he is clearly not just an academic. She is very cautious around KELLNER.
**Personality:** Efficient, pleasant, self-erasing. Never the center of any conversation. Translates idiom precisely, which is occasionally disconcerting.
**Fallback path:** If she cannot confirm Soviet assets, she can still win a partial victory by confirming Lowe's identity as intelligence and transmitting that to her Soviet handler via a coded letter she can post from the ship's mailbox (collected at docking).

### CHARACTER 11 — FATHER BENEDIKT HOLL — The Chaplain
**Cover story:** A Jesuit priest returning from a conference in Rome, travelling first class through the generosity of an anonymous benefactor (actually Vantrani, who collects priests the way some men collect political capital).
**Secret:** Father Holl is an agent of a Catholic underground network — the "Pontifical Intelligence" — which is trying to document Nazi atrocities for the Vatican's internal record. He knows the Ledger contains the identities of Catholic clergy who have been coerced into collaboration with the Reich. He wants to read those entries and destroy them — protecting those men from exposure and protecting the Church.
**Win condition (Completion + Prevention):** Read the entries in the Ledger relating to religious figures (he has a list of six specific names) AND ensure those entries are destroyed or removed before the Ledger changes hands.
**Abilities:**
- *Confession:* If a character voluntarily speaks to him alone, they will often reveal more than they intend — the power of the confessional is partly mechanical and partly psychological. He cannot force this; they must come to him.
- *Moral Authority:* Father Holl can invoke spiritual authority to de-escalate a violent situation — most characters will hesitate to harm him or act violently in his presence. This creates a window, not a wall.
- *Access to the Desperate:* Characters in acute distress are drawn to him. When the murder is discovered (Round 6), this becomes very powerful.
**Starting location:** Library
**Key relationships:** He knows VANTRANI from a shared philanthropic circle and trusts him slightly more than he should. He finds MARTA's obvious fear moving and does not know why she is afraid. He has identified ASHBY as a man who has done things he regrets.
**Personality:** Unhurried, genuinely curious, faintly sad in a way that reads as wisdom. Asks questions rather than giving answers. Does not judge aloud. Observes everything.
**Fallback path:** If he cannot access the Ledger, he can still achieve a partial win by receiving verbal confirmation from Vantrani that the named clergy are in the Ledger — and an agreement that those names will not be transmitted to any government. This requires Vantrani to make a moral concession, which is not impossible.

### CHARACTER 12 — NADINE MARCHETTI — The Fashion Designer
**Cover story:** An Italian fashion designer, attending a New York showcase.
**Secret:** Nadine is conducting an independent investigation into Vantrani. Her younger brother was an anti-fascist agitator who disappeared while aboard a Vantrani Line cargo vessel in 1934. She believes Vantrani handed her brother's identity to Italian fascist authorities, who had him quietly killed. She wants to find proof of this — which she believes exists in Vantrani's private files. She has no intelligence training. She is entirely self-taught and running entirely on grief and determination.
**Win condition (Discovery):** Find evidence in Vantrani's private papers that directly implicates him in her brother's death.
**Abilities:**
- *Artistic Eye:* Nadine notices visual and spatial details others miss — she can detect when a room has been searched, when documents have been moved, when someone is wearing something they borrowed. She can ask the narrator one environmental observation question per scene.
- *Social Chameleon:* Her profession gives her a reason to interact with almost everyone — measuring, discussing fabrics, flattering the self-conscious — without arousing suspicion.
- *Emotional Core:* Once per game, Nadine can make an appeal based purely on shared grief or loss that will cause one NPC to act against their own interest to help her. This works on characters with a parallel emotional wound (Marta, Elena, Holl).
**Starting location:** Dining Room
**Key relationships:** She is charming to VANTRANI and despises him. She has taken note of LOWE's luggage (she saw it being handled and the weight seemed wrong). She finds ELENA sympathetic.
**Personality:** Expressive, tactile, speaks with her hands. Generous with compliments. Occasionally goes completely still when something catches her professionally trained eye.
**Fallback path:** If the private papers don't contain direct proof, she can still find corroborating evidence — a name, a date, a correspondence that points circumstantially to guilt. This is a partial win: enough to pursue legal action post-voyage, not enough to confront Vantrani directly.

### CHARACTER 13 — OTTO REISS — The Steward
**Cover story:** A longtime cabin steward of the Leviathan, twenty years of service, impeccable.
**Secret:** Otto is Vantrani's personal informant. He reports to Vantrani every morning on what he has observed the guests doing — who visited whom, who seemed agitated, who asked unusual questions. He is not malicious. He believes he is protecting his employer. He also has a personal secret: he has been pilfering small valuables from luggage for three years. Nothing major — a cufflink here, a pen there. He is terrified of being caught.
**Win condition (Protection):** Ensure Vantrani is not betrayed or exposed this crossing — which means reporting any guest who shows signs of working against Vantrani's interests.
**Abilities:**
- *Master Key:* Otto has a passkey that opens all first-class staterooms. He does not have access to restricted spaces below deck.
- *Invisible:* Crew are treated as furniture. Otto can be present in almost any scene without the player or NPCs realizing they are being observed, unless they specifically look.
- *Loyalty Network:* Otto knows every crew member by name and can request small favors (a message delivered, a door left unlocked, a time recorded differently) from other stewards.
**Starting location:** Stateroom corridor
**Key relationships:** Loyalty to VANTRANI above all. He is fond of FATHER HOLL and will not inform on him. He has noted ELSPETH's camera case and has reported this to Vantrani already.
**Personality:** Professionally warm, self-effacing, deft. Never makes eye contact that lingers. Has a memory for preferences — he knows everyone's drink before they order it.
**Fallback path:** If Vantrani is exposed despite his efforts, Otto will prioritize protecting himself — he will provide a statement to the Captain that he "observed nothing unusual," and destroy any notes he has been keeping.

### CHARACTER 14 — CORNELIUS VANTRANI — The Host
**Cover story:** The birthday celebration.
**Secret:** As described. Holds the Ledger in two parts. Has simultaneously offered it to British (Ashby), German (Kellner), and American (Lowe) buyers. His plan is to auction it aboard ship — using each buyer's knowledge that others are interested to drive the price up — and then decide at the last moment who receives what. He has no ideological loyalty. He wants money and continued invisibility.

**Abilities:**

**Starting location:** Dining Room (making rounds)
**Key relationships:** All characters are connected to him in some way. He has read Otto's reports and knows about Elspeth's camera. He is beginning to suspect that Marta is not who she says she is.
**Personality:** Expansive, delighted, with a grandfather's warmth and a shark's attention. Tells long stories with embedded tests. Refills your glass before you notice it was empty.

## MECHANICS

### Time Structure
- Each round = 30 minutes in-game
- Game begins: 7:00 PM Tuesday, October 5th, 1937
- Game ends: Dawn, Friday October 8th (ship docks Saturday AM — endgame triggers on the final night)
- Planned active rounds: 10-12

### Actions Per Round
- Each character: MOVE to adjacent location + ONE significant action + incidental interactions
- Significant actions: use ability, search room, deep conversation, pick a lock, etc.
- Incidental: brief exchanges, observations (no action cost)

### Violence Rules
- Killing requires: weapon or method + target alone/incapacitated + non-public space
- Most characters lack weapons at start
- Violence creates cascading consequences (crew response, Captain involvement, social suspicion)
- Father Holl's Moral Authority can interrupt violence
- Physical confrontation defaults to mutual exposure risk on a passenger liner

### Social Mechanics
- Characters can lie freely — narrator adjudicates plausibility
- Some abilities force partial disclosure
- Social pressure creates narrative pressure but not mechanical compulsion
- Declarations of identity are always optional and may be false

### Hard Mode Rules
- All NPCs advance their goals aggressively each round
- Vantrani will begin his auction as early as Round 4
- Prager's death (Acceleration Event) will occur in Round 5 rather than later
- Key documents may change hands between NPCs before the player reaches them
- Any round the player wastes or stalls, two NPCs make significant progress

### Red Herrings (Design Note)
- The seating chart in the dining foyer has been tampered with — Kellner did this to ensure he sits near Vantrani on Night One. It reads as suspicious but is only logistical.
- The coordinates on the study map are locations of Vantrani Line shipwrecks — insurance claims. Not intelligence-related.
- Mercier is clearly hiding something. He is not a spy in the full sense. Players will likely overestimate his role.
- Nadine is clearly emotional and angry toward Vantrani. She is not a murderer and is not working for anyone. Players may suspect her of the eventual killing.
- The substituted German diplomatic bag looks like a sophisticated operation. It was actually swapped by Otto during a cleaning, who accidentally confused it with another guest's document case and is too afraid to admit it.
